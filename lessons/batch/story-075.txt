Gurukul Lesson — The Dissolving of the Seeker
Dawn spreads softly across the forest.
Birdsong echoes between ancient trees.
 The sacred fire burns quietly before Vashishtha.
You and Lakshmana sit in silence.
Today, the sage speaks very little.
For the teaching has entered the most delicate territory.
🕉️ Vashishtha Speaks
“Rama…
Earlier, you believed:
‘I am the body.’
Then:
‘I am the observer.’
Then:
‘I am awareness.’
Now even this must be examined:
‘I am the seeker of truth.’”
Lakshmana looks surprised.
“The seeker too?” he asks softly.
Vashishtha nods.
“Yes.
For as long as there is a seeker,
 truth appears distant.”
🔥 The Subtle Distance
“The seeker lives on one assumption:
‘What I seek is not here yet.’”
The sage lifts water from the river in his palm.
“Can the river search for water?”
He lets it fall back into itself.
🌊 The Final Effort of Mind
“The mind survives by movement.
If worldly desire fades,
 it creates spiritual pursuit.
If identity weakens,
 it becomes:
‘one who is awakening.’”
The forest becomes utterly still.
“Do you see, Rama?
Even becoming pure
 is still becoming.”
🕯️ Today’s Practice
Sit silently beside the breath.
Do not try to attain stillness.
Do not try to stop thoughts.
Do not even try to awaken.
Instead, notice gently:
👉 What remains when seeking pauses?
Not permanently.
Just for one moment.
🌌 A Glimpse Beyond Effort
For an instant…
There is no meditator.
No progress.
No distance between yourself and truth.
Only simple presence,
 without ownership.
Then the mind returns and says:
“I experienced something.”
Vashishtha smiles softly.
“That voice comes later.”
🌿 The Teaching of the Day
The seeker is the final form of psychological time.
It says:
“One day”
“Soon”
“I will arrive”
“I am nearing realization”
But truth is never future.
🔥 Vashishtha’s Closing Words
“Rama…
 cease chasing the Self
 and the Self stands revealed.”
