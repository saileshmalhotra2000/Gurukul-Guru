The sky is pale. A faint silver light rests on the river.
 Rama and Lakshmana sit before Vashishta.
Today the Rishi holds no staff.
 No lamp burns between them.
Only silence.
After a long while, Vashishta speaks.
“Rama.”
“Yes, Gurudev.”
“If tomorrow the kingdom praises you as the greatest prince, what will you feel?”
Rama thinks carefully.
“Pleasure, Gurudev.”
“And if tomorrow they call you unworthy?”
There is a small pause.
“Pain, Gurudev.”
Vashishta nods.
“So your peace depends on the tongue of the crowd?”
Rama lowers his gaze.
Lakshmana shifts slightly, impatient.
“Gurudev, should we not feel joy when praised?”
Vashishta turns to him.
“You may feel it.
 But must you lean on it?”
Lakshmana frowns.
Vashishta draws two circles in the sand.
“In this circle,” he says, pointing to the first, “is who you are.”
He points to the second.
“In this circle is what the world says about you.”
He wipes away the second circle with his palm.
“Which one remains?”
“The first,” Rama replies.
“Then why,” Vashishta asks gently, “do men live inside the second?”
Silence settles again.
He looks at Rama steadily.
“Yesterday you did not react to insult. Good.
 Today I ask — can you remain steady in praise?”
Lakshmana smiles slightly.
“That is easy, Gurudev.”
Vashishta’s eyes sharpen.
“Is it?”
He gestures to a young disciple standing nearby.
“Go. Tell the ashram that Rama has surpassed all princes in skill.”
The boy runs.
Within minutes, murmurs ripple through the hermitage. A few students glance toward Rama with admiration.
Lakshmana looks proud.
Rama feels warmth rise in his chest.
A subtle expansion.
A quiet satisfaction.
He notices it.
Vashishta watches him closely.
“Rama. What moves in you?”
“A sweetness, Gurudev.”
“And if you cling to that sweetness?”
Rama closes his eyes briefly.
“I will seek it again.”
“And if one day it is withheld?”
“I will suffer.”
Vashishta nods.
“Strength of character is not only freedom from anger.
 It is freedom from dependence on praise.”
Lakshmana’s brows knit.
“But Gurudev, how do we remain untouched?”
Vashishta shakes his head slightly.
“Not untouched.
 Uncontrolled.”
He points to Rama’s chest.
“Feel the sweetness.”
Then to his forehead.
“See it clearly.”
Then to the ground.
“Let it fall.”
Rama inhales slowly.
The warmth softens.
He bows slightly.
“Gurudev, it is fading.”
Vashishta smiles faintly.
“Good. Now it serves you.
 You do not serve it.”
The river flows quietly beside them.
After a while, Vashishta speaks once more.
“Children, insult tests your temper.
 Praise tests your ego.
 One burns you.
 The other inflates you.
If you master both, you are ready to master kingdoms.”
The sun rises fully now.
The lesson ends — but the watching continues.
Sit there a moment.
When praise comes to you —
 does it settle lightly…
 or does it stay longer than it should?
