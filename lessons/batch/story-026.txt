The dawn that day carried a quiet weight.
Not heavy… but deliberate.
Rama and Lakshmana found Vashishtha seated beneath a large tree, his eyes half-closed, as if listening to something beyond sound.
They sat before him in silence.
After some time, Vashishtha opened his eyes and said:
“Today, you will not learn by doing…”
“…you will learn by not doing.”
Lakshmana looked slightly puzzled.
Vashishtha pointed to a nearby spot.
“Sit. And remain as you are.”
They both straightened their posture.
At first, it seemed simple.
They had sat many times before.
But today, there was a difference.
No instruction.
No mantra.
No object to focus on.
Just… sitting.
Moments passed.
Then, the mind began its play.
Lakshmana felt an itch on his arm.
A strong urge arose to move.
He resisted for a moment… then shifted slightly.
Vashishtha said nothing.
Rama noticed his own breath becoming uneven.
A thought appeared:
“Am I doing this correctly?”
Then another:
“How long will this last?”
He watched… but soon, he too adjusted his posture.
After some time, Vashishtha spoke softly:
“Observe.”
“Not the world outside…”
“…but the movements within.”
They turned their attention inward again.
Now they began to see:
a thought appearing
a sensation pulling attention
an urge rising and passing
Like waves… small, constant.
Lakshmana spoke quietly,
“Gurudev… it does not stay still.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“It was never still.”
“You are only seeing it now.”
A bird called from a distance.
This time, Rama noticed the sound…
but did not follow it.
It came… and went.
Something had changed.
Vashishtha continued:
“Training does not begin when the mind becomes still.”
“It begins when you see clearly that it is not.”
Minutes passed again.
Lakshmana felt another itch.
Stronger this time.
But now he watched it.
He did not move immediately.
The urge rose…
peaked…
and slowly faded.
His eyes widened slightly.
“It went away…”
Vashishtha looked at him.
“Yes.”
“What you do not feed… weakens.”
Rama now saw something deeper.
Between two thoughts…
there was a small gap.
Very brief.
But peaceful.
Unmoving.
He stayed there for a moment.
Then another thought came.
But something within him had recognized:
There is a place untouched.
Vashishtha spoke one final time:
“You try to still the mind by force.”
“But stillness is already there…”
“…between movements.”
“Learn to notice it.”
“Learn to rest in it.”
The session ended without a formal closing.
The brothers rose slowly.
The world looked the same.
But their way of seeing had shifted… even if just slightly.
As they walked back, Lakshmana said softly,
“It is not about stopping the waves…”
Rama completed the thought,
“…but knowing the water.”
Behind them, Vashishtha remained seated…
as steady as the space between two thoughts.

The morning air was cool, and a soft wind moved through the trees.
Vashishtha was walking slowly along a narrow path.
Rama and Lakshmana followed behind him.
After some distance, Vashishtha stopped… but did not turn.
He simply said:
“Walk ahead of me.”
The brothers exchanged a glance, then stepped forward.
They walked side by side.
At first, their pace was natural.
But soon, Lakshmana became aware:
“Gurudev is behind us.”
His steps changed slightly.
More careful.
More deliberate.
Rama too felt it.
A subtle tightening.
A quiet awareness of being observed.
After a while, Vashishtha spoke:
“Do you feel the difference?”
Rama answered,
“Yes, Gurudev.”
“There is more alertness.”
Lakshmana added,
“And a slight tension.”
Vashishtha nodded, still behind them.
“This is how most people live.”
“Not in awareness…”
“…but in the feeling of being watched.”
They continued walking.
The path curved gently.
Birds moved in the branches above.
But now, both brothers noticed something deeper:
Their attention was divided.
part on walking
part on themselves
part on Vashishtha behind
Not fully anywhere.
After some time, Vashishtha said:
“Now… forget that I am here.”
“Walk as if you are alone.”
They tried.
But the thought remained:
“He is still behind us.”
Lakshmana smiled slightly.
“It is difficult to forget.”
Vashishtha’s voice came calm and clear:
“Yes.”
“Because you carry the watcher inside.”
They slowed their steps.
“What you feel is not me…”
“…but your own mind observing itself.”
Rama’s expression changed.
A deeper understanding arose.
“This is why the mind cannot rest…”
“…it is always watching, judging, adjusting.”
Vashishtha replied,
“Just so.”
They walked further.
Then Vashishtha gave a simple instruction:
“Do not try to remove the watcher.”
“Simply stop performing for it.”
Lakshmana relaxed his shoulders slightly.
His steps became more natural.
Rama allowed his breath to settle.
For a few moments…
walking happened without self-consciousness.
No effort to appear correct.
No inner commentary.
Just movement.
Vashishtha came forward and stood beside them now.
“Did you feel it?”
Rama nodded.
“There was ease.”
Lakshmana added,
“And less division.”
Vashishtha looked ahead at the path.
“When you act for the watcher…”
“…you become divided.”
“When you act from simple awareness…”
“…you become whole.”
They walked the rest of the path in silence.
This time, there was no sense of being observed.
Only the rhythm of steps…
the sound of wind…
and a quiet, unbroken presence.
And somewhere within…
the watcher had become still.
