04 June 2026 — Lesson 51: The Space Around the Cloud
The forest was silent after the night's rain.
Drops of water still hung from the leaves. The river moved gently through the gurukul grounds. Rama and Lakshmana sat before Rishi Vashishtha as the first sunlight entered the clearing.
For many days they had watched attention gather. They had seen the first leaning, the first grasp, the birth of ownership, and the subtle forgetting of awareness itself.
Now they were beginning to see something new.
Rama Asked
“Master, sometimes I can see restlessness arising.
I no longer immediately say:
'I am restless.'
Instead I see:
'Restlessness is arising.'
Is this the freedom you speak of?”
Vashishtha Replied
“It is a good beginning.
But look more carefully.”
He pointed toward a cloud drifting across the morning sky.
“Where do you place your attention?”
“On the cloud, Master.”
“And what surrounds the cloud?”
“The sky.”
Vashishtha smiled.
“The seeker first learns to stop being the cloud.
The sage learns to notice the sky.”
The Hidden Error
Vashishtha drew a circle in the sand.
Then he placed a small pebble inside it.
“The pebble is restlessness.”
“The circle is awareness.”
He looked at Rama.
“Many seekers become fascinated by the pebble.
They study it.
Analyze it.
Fight it.
Improve it.
Manage it.”
Then he swept his hand across the vast circle.
“And forget the space in which it appears.”
Experiential Insight
Close your eyes.
Allow some feeling to arise.
Perhaps restlessness.
Perhaps impatience.
Perhaps boredom.
Now notice:
The feeling occupies only a small portion of experience.
Around it there is space.
Around it there is awareness.
The mind looks at the cloud.
Awareness contains the entire sky.
Can the cloud exist without the sky?
Can restlessness exist without awareness?
Observe carefully.
Ordinary Life Examples
Waiting
Restlessness appears.
Immediately attention goes to the discomfort.
The cloud becomes everything.
Yet the awareness containing it remains unnoticed.
An Unfinished Task
Concern appears.
Thoughts gather.
Energy contracts.
But even the contraction is appearing within something larger.
Irritation
A tightening appears in the chest.
The mind studies the irritation.
Awareness quietly contains both the irritation and the studying.
Excitement
Pleasure appears.
Attention rushes toward it.
Yet pleasure too is only a cloud moving through the sky.
Lakshmana Asked
“Master, should we focus on awareness instead of the experience?”
Vashishtha shook his head.
“No.
Do not reject the cloud.
Simply include the sky.
See both.”
Today's Sadhana
Whenever a strong experience appears today:
Do not ask:
"How do I get rid of this?"
Instead ask:
"What is the space in which this is appearing?"
Feel the experience.
But also feel the openness around it.
The experience may remain.
The identification begins to weaken.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha spoke softly:
“Freedom is not found by removing the cloud.
Freedom is found by noticing the sky.
The cloud is seen.
The sky remains.
Awareness is the sky of every experience.”
Gurukul of Rishi Vashishtha
