Lesson: The Seer Is Not Personal
Until now, you have understood:
 “I am not my thoughts.”
Now you must inquire:
 “Who is this ‘I’ that watches?”
O Rama, when a thought arises and you notice it, there is a subtle feeling:
 “I am noticing.”
Look closely at this.
Is this “noticer” a form?
Does it have a boundary?
Does it come and go like thoughts do?
Sit quietly and observe.
You will find:
The thought appears.
The noticing happens.
But the “noticer” itself is never seen as an object.
O Lakshmana, this is the subtle trap:
The mind creates a new identity—
 “I am the observer.”
This too is a thought.
Just as a wave cannot contain the ocean,
 this idea cannot contain the seer.
Contemplation for Today
When you notice a thought, do not stop there.
Turn deeper:
Who is aware of this noticing?
Can that awareness be observed?
Do not answer with words.
 Let the question dissolve into silence.
Truth to Realize
The seer is not:
the body
the mind
the observer-idea
The seer is pure awareness itself, without center or edge.
It is not yours, O Rama.
 It is not yours, O Lakshmana.
It simply is.
Practice
Sit as before.
Let thoughts come and go like clouds drifting across the sky.
But now, do not cling even to the feeling:
 “I am watching.”
Let even that dissolve.
Remain as that which cannot be grasped.
When this is seen clearly, O Rama,
 the distinction between “you” and “the seer” begins to fade.
And like the morning mist under the rising sun,
 the illusion of separation quietly disappears.
What you must see here
Thoughts still appear → like clouds
Even the observer figure is faint… dissolving
What remains is not a person…
 but a vast, centerless presence
The key shift shown
Earlier:
You were lost in thoughts
 Then:
You became the observer
Now:
Even the observer is seen… and begins to dissolve
Look carefully
Is there a boundary to the light?
Is there a center from where it shines?
Can you locate the “one” who is aware?
This is the teaching in visual form
The seer is not a person
The observer is a temporary tool
Awareness remains… without ownership
Final Upadesh
“Do not replace the thinker with a watcher.
 See both… and remain as that which neither comes nor goes.”
