The dawn mist rested lightly upon the riverbanks.
Rama and Lakshmana sat beside Vashishtha beneath the ancient fig tree.
 Birdsong echoed softly through the forest.
For many days they had learned:
to notice thought
to observe sensation
to distinguish awareness from attention
to catch the first spark before it became a fire
That morning, Vashishtha drew a circle in the sand.
Then another circle around it.
And another beyond that.
He looked toward Rama.
🌿 Lesson: The Layers of Experience
“Rama…
 suffering deepens
 when the outer layers are mistaken for the Self.”
He touched the outermost circle.
1. The World Layer
This is:
people
events
praise
blame
gain
loss
Most humans believe:
“The world is causing my inner state.”
So they spend life trying to control the outer circle.
But the sage sees:
the event is only the first contact.
Not the full suffering.
2. The Thought Layer
Vashishtha touched the second circle.
After the event comes interpretation.
Not:
“He spoke.”
But:
“He insulted me.”
“I am not respected.”
“This should not happen.”
The mind quickly creates:
meaning
story
identity
And then forgets it created them.
3. The Sensation Layer
He touched the inner circle.
Before words fully form,
 the body already responds.
A tightening.
 A heat.
 A contraction in the chest.
 A pull in the stomach.
Most people never remain here long enough to see clearly.
They rush immediately:
 sensation → story → reaction
The seeker learns to pause inside sensation itself.
4. The Witness
Then Vashishtha erased all three circles with his palm.
Silence remained.
“Rama…
 that which knows all layers
 is untouched by all layers.”
The witness:
knows sensation
knows thought
knows the world
Yet itself remains unchanged.
Like the sky:
storms move through it
birds cross it
clouds gather and dissolve
But the sky itself is never burned by lightning.
🌿 The Practice of This Lesson
Today’s discipline was subtle.
Whenever disturbance arises:
Do not begin with:
“Why is this happening?”
Instead observe carefully:
Step 1 — See the outer event
What actually occurred?
Not interpretation.
 Only fact.
Step 2 — Notice the thought layer
What story formed instantly?
Watch without fighting it.
Step 3 — Feel the sensation layer
Where is it in the body?
chest?
throat?
stomach?
face?
Remain there quietly.
Step 4 — Notice the witness
Ask softly:
“What is aware of all this?”
Do not answer intellectually.
Simply notice.
Lakshmana asked:
“Gurudev, if the witness is untouched,
 why does suffering feel so real?”
Vashishtha smiled gently.
“Because attention becomes hypnotized by the layers
 and forgets the one who sees them.”
He picked up a clay pot filled with river water.
The moon reflected inside it.
Then he shook the pot violently.
The reflection shattered.
But the moon above remained whole.
🌙 Closing Teaching
“Rama…
 the disturbed mind is only shaking water.
Do not mistake the trembling reflection
 for the trembling Self.”
