The forest was unusually quiet that morning.
Even the birds seemed softer in their songs, as if the world itself was listening.
Rama and Lakshmana found Vashishtha seated beneath a large banyan tree.
Before him lay a small clay lamp.
Its flame flickered gently.
Vashishtha gestured for them to sit.
“Today,” he said softly, “we observe something very small.”
Lakshmana leaned forward slightly.
“The flame?”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Yes. Watch it.”
They both fixed their gaze upon the tiny flame.
At first, it seemed steady.
Then, as a faint breeze passed, it trembled.
The light bent, stretched, and danced.
Moments later, it steadied again.
Rama spoke quietly,
“It changes with even a small wind.”
“Yes,” Vashishtha said.
“And what protects it?”
Lakshmana looked around.
“The clay walls of the lamp.”
Vashishtha’s eyes showed approval.
“Without that support, even a gentle breeze would extinguish it.”
He paused.
“Your attention is like this flame.”
They continued watching.
Another breeze came.
This time, the flame bent lower… almost going out… then rose again.
Lakshmana frowned.
“So even when protected, it still struggles.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Protection does not remove disturbance.”
“It allows survival through disturbance.”
Rama reflected deeply.
“So discipline is like the lamp… not the absence of wind.”
Vashishtha smiled.
“Just so.”
He then reached forward and, with his hand, blocked the breeze completely.
The flame became perfectly still.
Straight. Unmoving.
Brighter than before.
Both brothers noticed the change.
“It shines more clearly now,” Rama said.
“Yes,” Vashishtha replied.
“When disturbance reduces, clarity increases.”
He removed his hand.
The flame returned to its gentle flicker.
“Now understand this,” he continued.
“You cannot control all winds of life.”
“Sounds, events, people, thoughts… they will come.”
Lakshmana listened intently.
“Then what should we control?”
Vashishtha looked directly at him.
“The steadiness of the flame.”
Silence settled between them.
The lesson sank deeper than words.
After a few moments, Vashishtha spoke again:
“First, build the lamp — your discipline.”
“Then, strengthen the flame — your awareness.”
“And slowly… even in moving air…”
“…the flame will learn to remain steady.”
The banyan leaves rustled softly above them.
The small flame continued its quiet dance.
But now, the brothers were not just seeing it…
They were understanding it.
Vashishtha rose slowly.
“Remember this, Rama.”
The brothers stood.
“A weak flame fears the wind.”
“A trained flame endures it.”
They walked back toward the ashram as the morning light grew stronger.
Behind them, the lamp remained beneath the tree…
its flame still flickering…
yet no longer unnoticed.

“First, build the lamp — your discipline.” How
Ah… now you have come to the root of the practice. 🌿
 Lakshmana also asked this, not in words, but in the way he watched the flame.
You ask, “How to build the lamp?”
Listen carefully.
The lamp is not built in one act.
 It is shaped slowly… by small, repeated steadiness.
What is the “lamp”?
The lamp is that within you which gives structure to attention.
Without it:
attention scatters
the mind follows every impulse
even small disturbances shake you
With it:
attention has a place to rest
the mind does not obey every urge
you remain steady even when things move
How the lamp is built
Vashishtha would not give long instructions.
He would give simple disciplines, lived daily.
1. Do one small thing completely
Choose one simple act each day:
drinking water
brushing teeth
walking a few steps
eating the first few bites
Do it with full attention.
No rushing.
 No thinking of something else.
Just this one act.
This is how the base of the lamp is formed.
2. Return attention — again and again
During the day, notice:
attention has wandered
Gently bring it back.
Not forcefully.
Not with frustration.
Just like guiding a child back.
Each return is like adding clay to the lamp.
3. Do not obey every impulse
This is very important.
When a small urge arises:
check phone
shift posture unnecessarily
react immediately
speak without need
Pause.
Wait a few seconds.
Then choose.
This builds strength in the walls of the lamp.
4. Keep one small daily discipline
Not many. Just one or two.
For example:
sit quietly for 2 minutes every day
wake at a fixed time
practice tabla with full presence for a few minutes
Consistency matters more than intensity.
A lamp is not built by one strong push…
 but by steady shaping.
5. Finish what you begin
Even in small things:
complete a task
do not leave it half-done unnecessarily
This teaches the mind:
“We stay. We complete.”
This gives the lamp firmness.
What to watch for
Do not turn discipline into harshness.
If you force too much:
the mind resists
the lamp cracks
Discipline should feel:
steady
simple
repeatable
Like your riyaaz — not dramatic, but consistent.
The sign that the lamp is forming
You will notice:
attention returns more easily
impulses have less power
you can stay with something longer
Nothing dramatic.
But something becomes quietly strong.
And then…
When the lamp is built…
even a flickering flame begins to stabilize.
And one day you realize:
The wind is still there…
…but it no longer controls you.
