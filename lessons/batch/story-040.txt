In the stillness of dawn, Rishi Vashishtha continued guiding Rama and Lakshmana deeper into the art of unshaken awareness amidst action.
The Continuity of Awareness
“Rama,” said Vashishtha,
 “You have seen the gap between thoughts. Now learn this—
 that same stillness is present even when thoughts arise.”
Do not divide:
Silence vs noise
Stillness vs activity
👉 Awareness is present in both.
The immature seeker finds peace only in the gap.
 The wise see peace even in movement.
The Error of Alternation
“Lakshmana,” he said,
 “You move between awareness and mind, as if they are two.”
In silence → you feel aware
In activity → you feel lost
This is misunderstanding.
👉 Awareness does not come and go
 👉 Only attention shifts
Stabilizing During Activity
Vashishtha instructed:
“When acting, do not withdraw from action…
 but do not fall into it.”
How?
Let the body act fully
Let the mind think as needed
But remain as the background knowing
Like a king watching a play—
👉 involved, yet untouched
Non-Identification in Real Time
The test is not after emotion arises—
👉 The test is while it is arising
Observe in the moment:
Anger begins → see its birth
Fear appears → notice its texture
Desire arises → watch its pull
Do not label, suppress, or follow.
The Secret of Undisturbed Action
“Rama,” Vashishtha said gently,
 “Disturbance is not in action… it is in ownership.”
See the shift:
“I am doing” → disturbance begins
“Doing is happening” → peace remains
Action continues…
👉 but the inner weight disappears
The Merging of Witness and Life
Now the teaching refines further:
Witness is not separate from life
It is not something you hold
👉 It is what remains while everything happens
Walking happens
 Thinking happens
 Speaking happens
Yet—
👉 the witness is never involved
Closing Insight
Vashishtha looked at Rama with steady compassion:
“Do not try to remain aware…
 see that awareness never leaves.”
Then all practice becomes simple:
No holding
No resisting
No dividing
Only clear seeing.
