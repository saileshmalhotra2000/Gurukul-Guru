Lesson 30 — The Space Between Impulse and Action
In the silent forest gurukul, the morning sun filtered through ancient banyan branches. Birds called across the riverbanks while the young princes sat before the sage.
Vashishtha drew a line in the earth with a wooden staff.
Then he spoke softly:
“Rama, Lakshmana —
 destiny is often decided in a single unguarded moment.”
The brothers listened carefully.
The sage pointed to a bow resting beside a tree.
“An arrow released cannot be recalled.
 A word spoken cannot be swallowed.
 An action completed becomes part of karma.”
Then he closed his eyes.
“Therefore the wise protect the space between impulse and action.”
Rama Asked
“Gurudev, what is this space?”
The sage smiled.
“It is the sacred doorway where consciousness may enter.”
He lifted a cup of river water and stirred it rapidly.
“When the mind is disturbed, action becomes reaction.”
Then he placed the cup down and allowed the water to become still.
“But when stillness enters first, action becomes dharma.”
The Three Fires That Seize the Mind
The sage drew three circles in the soil.
1. Anger
“It demands immediate expression.”
2. Fear
“It demands immediate escape.”
3. Desire
“It demands immediate possession.”
He looked toward Lakshmana.
“These forces become dangerous only when awareness is absent.”
The Warrior’s Discipline
“A true warrior is not one who acts quickly.
 A true warrior is one who can remain conscious while power rises within him.”
The forest wind became still.
“Most men are pushed by emotion like dry leaves in a storm.
 But the awakened man pauses.”
The sage placed his palm upon his heart.
“In that pause, wisdom speaks.”
Practice of the Sacred Pause
For the next phase of training, the princes were instructed:
Before every important action:
Pause for one breath.
Observe the emotion present.
Ask:
“Will this action create clarity or suffering?”
Act only after awareness returns to the body.
Vashishtha’s Final Teaching
As twilight descended upon the forest, the sage spoke one final sentence:
“Rama, freedom is not found when impulses disappear.
 Freedom is found when impulses no longer command you.”
You said “A true warrior is one who can remain conscious while power rises within him.” What do you mean by “While power rises within him”
Vashishtha would say:
“Rama… power is not only strength of body.
 Power is every intense force that rises within the human system.”
🌿 What is “power” here?
It means the raw energies that suddenly arise inside you:
anger
passion
ambition
fear
excitement
sexual energy
the urge to speak
the urge to dominate
the urge to defend
the urge to act immediately
These movements contain enormous force.
Most people are unconsciously carried away by them.
🕯️ Example
Someone insults you.
Instantly:
heat rises
heart beats faster
thoughts accelerate
the body wants to react
This surge is “power rising.”
The ordinary man
The ordinary man becomes the surge.
He reacts automatically.
The energy takes control.
The conscious warrior
But the trained being does something extraordinary:
👉 the energy rises
 👉 but awareness remains present
The anger is seen.
 The force is felt.
 The impulse is visible.
But there is no unconscious surrender to it.
🌊 Another example
Suppose ambition arises strongly.
A huge drive appears:
“I must achieve this.”
Most people become possessed by it.
But the conscious being can:
feel the energy fully
use its intelligence
without becoming psychologically enslaved by it
Then power becomes aligned instead of destructive.
🔥 The deeper meaning
The teaching is not:
“Destroy energy.”
The teaching is:
“Remain conscious while energy moves.”
Because energy itself is not the problem.
Unconscious identification is.
🌿 Think of a horse
A wild horse can:
throw the rider
 or
carry the rider nobly
The energy is the same.
Awareness determines whether it becomes:
chaos
 or
dharma
🕉️ Why Vashishtha calls this true strength
Anyone can react unconsciously.
Very few can:
feel rage without hatred
feel fear without collapse
feel power without ego
feel desire without slavery
This requires enormous inner steadiness.
🌌 The subtle insight
The sage is not weak because emotion disappears.
The sage is powerful because:
emotion no longer blinds awareness.
🌿 Gurudev’s final pointing
“Rama…
 when fire rises within you,
 do not become the fire.
Become the one who can hold fire consciously.”
