Dawn had only just touched the treetops.
 A cool breeze moved through the forest gurukul, stirring the prayer flags and carrying the scent of sandalwood from the sacred fire.
Beneath the ancient banyan, Rishi Vashishtha sat unmoving.
 Before him, Rama and Lakshmana bowed and took their seats upon the earth.
For a few moments, only silence taught.
Then Vashishtha opened his eyes.
The Next Teaching: Returning From Absorption
“Rama… yesterday you learned:
When thought happens within awareness, it serves you.
 When awareness is forgotten, thought becomes master.
Today you must learn the next discipline:
How to return when you realize you have been lost in thought.”
Rama bowed.
“Gurudev, often I notice only after many moments
 that I have become absorbed.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“That noticing itself is progress.”
Do Not Be Disturbed By Forgetting
Lakshmana asked—
“But Gurudev, when we notice we have forgotten awareness,
 should we be disappointed?”
Vashishtha smiled gently.
“No.
To become frustrated at forgetting
 is merely another thought consuming you.”
He looked at them steadily.
“The moment of noticing is not failure.
 It is awakening.”
The Sacred Return
Vashishtha picked up a leaf and let it fall.
“As often as the wind carries the leaf away,
 so often it returns to earth.
Likewise:
As often as the mind wanders,
 return awareness to itself.”
Not harshly.
 Not impatiently.
 Not with struggle.
Simply—
Recognize. Release. Return.
The Threefold Practice
Rama asked—
“How exactly should we return, Gurudev?”
Vashishtha answered:
1. Recognize
See clearly:
“I have been absorbed in thought.”
2. Release
Do not continue the thought.
 Do not analyze the distraction.
3. Return
Rest again as simple awareness:
“I am here. Aware.”
The Deeper Understanding
Vashishtha continued—
“Understand this well, Rama:
Every return strengthens remembrance.
 Every remembrance weakens identification.”
The path is not built by never wandering.
It is built by:
Returning again and again
 until remembrance becomes natural.
Today’s Practice
“Throughout the day:
Whenever you notice you are lost in thought—
Do not judge.
 Do not resist.
Simply:
Recognize. Release. Return.”
Final Upadesh
Vashishtha’s voice softened into stillness.
“Rama…
 The awakened one is not he who never forgets at first—
But he who returns so often
 that forgetting can no longer endure.”
The princes bowed deeply.
And in the silence that followed,
 the lesson entered more deeply than words.
