The Moment Attention Gets Captured
At dawn, the forest was still.
Mist hovered above the river.
Birdsong had not yet fully begun.
Rama and Lakshmana sat beside Vashishtha in silence.
Then suddenly—
a deer darted through the trees.
Lakshmana’s attention instantly moved toward it.
His body leaned slightly.
His breath changed.
For a few moments, the whole field of awareness had narrowed.
Vashishtha noticed.
And softly asked:
“Lakshmana,
 did you see the deer…
 or did the deer take your attention?”
Lakshmana became still.
The Next Discovery
Before this, you learned:
the stirring before action
the leaning before movement
the echo after action
Now begins a subtler lesson:
the instant attention gets hooked.
Not by force.
By attraction.
Vashishtha’s Teaching
“Rama,
 bondage rarely begins with action.
It begins when attention loses its center
 and becomes pulled unconsciously.”
Watch This Carefully Today
A sound appears.
A memory appears.
A notification appears.
A face.
A desire.
A fear.
A fantasy.
Observe the precise instant:
attention gets pulled outward
the nervous system contracts toward the object
awareness narrows
inner gravity forms
Usually this happens automatically.
The yogi learns to see it happening live.
The Forest Example
Vashishtha pointed to a spider web covered in morning dew.
“See how lightly the web moves.
A tiny touch —
 and the entire structure responds.
So too with the human mind.
One small attraction
 can shake the whole inner field.”
Today’s Sadhana
Do not fight distractions.
That creates another conflict.
Instead:
observe the mechanics of capture.
Notice:
the contact
the pull
the narrowing
the identification
the movement toward action
Especially study:
the first half-second.
That is where unconscious living begins.
Or conscious living begins.
The Deeper Understanding
Freedom is not:
never seeing objects.
Freedom is:
seeing clearly when attention begins to lose balance.
Then awareness regains its own center naturally.
Like a skilled tabla player returning effortlessly to the sam after complex movement.
As sunlight slowly entered the gurukul clearing,
 Vashishtha drew a circle in the earth beside Rama.
Then he placed a single leaf outside the circle.
And asked:
“Can attention perceive the leaf
 without abandoning the center?”
