03 June 2026 — Lesson 50: Before the Word "I"
The forest was unusually still that morning.
A thin mist hovered above the river. Sunlight slowly entered the clearing, illuminating particles of dust floating through the air.
Rama and Lakshmana sat quietly before Rishi Vashishtha.
For many days they had watched the gathering of attention. They had observed leaning, grasping, forgetting, and ownership. They had begun to recognize how the center gradually formed.
Today Vashishtha traced a single line in the sand.
Rama Asked
“Master, yesterday you showed us how ownership strengthens the center.
But what gives ownership its power?”
Vashishtha smiled.
“A word.”
Rama looked puzzled.
“A word, Master?”
“Yes.
The smallest word.
The most powerful word.
The word:
I.”
The Cry of the Bird
At that moment a bird called from a nearby tree.
The sound echoed through the forest.
Vashishtha asked:
“What happened?”
“A bird sang,” said Lakshmana.
“Good.
Was there suffering?”
“No.”
“Was there bondage?”
“No.”
“Was there a center?”
“No.”
Then Vashishtha continued:
“The sound appeared.
The sound disappeared.
Experience flowed naturally.”
He paused.
“But now imagine a thought appears:
I like that sound.
Or:
I wish it would stop.
Notice what has entered.”
The Hidden Addition
Vashishtha wrote two statements in the sand.
First
Anger is present.
Second
I am angry.
He pointed at the first.
“This is experience.”
Then he pointed at the second.
“This is identification.”
The anger itself is not yet bondage.
The word I binds itself to the experience.
Then the center gains weight.
Then the story gains momentum.
Then suffering becomes personal.
Seeing the Birth of the "I"
Vashishtha continued:
“Most seekers notice thoughts.
Fewer notice emotions.
Very few notice the moment the word I attaches itself to them.”
He drew a circle around a pebble.
“The pebble is experience.
The circle is ownership.
The circle says:
‘This is me.’
Without the circle,
the pebble remains only a pebble.”
Experiential Insight
Close your eyes.
Wait for any sensation.
Perhaps warmth.
Perhaps pressure.
Perhaps a sound.
Simply notice:
Sensation is present.
Remain there.
Now quietly add:
I am feeling this.
Notice carefully.
The sensation may remain unchanged.
Yet something contracts.
A center begins to organize itself around the experience.
Observe the difference.
The experience is not the source of contraction.
The attachment of "I" is.
Ordinary Life Examples
Waiting in Traffic
First:
Cars are moving slowly.
Then:
I am being delayed.
The center appears.
Missing a Golf Shot
First:
The ball curves away from the target.
Then:
I hit a bad shot.
The experience becomes personal.
Receiving Praise
First:
Pleasant words are heard.
Then:
I am successful.
The center gathers strength.
Feeling Tired
First:
Fatigue is present.
Then:
I cannot handle today.
A simple sensation becomes a personal story.
Lakshmana Asked
“Master, should we stop using the word ‘I’?”
Vashishtha laughed softly.
“No.
Language is not the problem.
Identification is the problem.
Use the word.
Just do not become trapped inside it.”
Today's Sadhana
Throughout the day, notice moments of strong reaction.
Whenever possible, pause and silently ask:
"What existed before the word 'I' appeared?"
Look directly.
Was there:
a sensation?
a thought?
an emotion?
an impulse?
See if you can detect the raw experience before ownership arrives.
Do not try to change anything.
Simply observe the sequence.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha spoke quietly:
“The center is not born from experience.
It is born from claiming experience.
Before the word ‘I’ appears,
life flows freely.
See the claim arise,
and the witness becomes visible once more.”
