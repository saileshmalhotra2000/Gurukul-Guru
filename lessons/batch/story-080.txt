LESSON — THE WITNESS AND THE STORM
In the quiet of the forest, Rama asked:
“Master, when strong emotions arise suddenly,
 how can awareness remain steady?”
Rishi Vashishtha pointed toward the river.
At dawn, the surface was calm,
 reflecting the sky perfectly.
But later, winds came.
 The water shook.
 The reflection broke.
Vashishtha said:
“Did the sky break —
 or only the reflection?”
Lakshmana answered softly:
“Only the reflection.”
The sage nodded.
“So it is with consciousness.
 Awareness itself is never disturbed.
 Only the mind’s surface trembles.”
THE TEACHING
The untrained mind believes:
“I am angry.”
“I am afraid.”
“I am disturbed.”
But the seeker begins to see:
Anger is appearing.
Fear is appearing.
Disturbance is appearing.
The witness remains untouched.
THE FOREST LESSON
Vashishtha asked Rama to sit beside a stormy river.
“Do not stop the river,” he said.
 “Do not calm the water.
 Only observe.”
At first, Rama became entangled in every sound,
 every movement,
 every thought.
But slowly,
 something changed.
The river continued flowing —
 yet a deeper stillness appeared behind observation itself.
Then Vashishtha spoke:
“Peace does not come because movement ends.
 Peace comes when you stop identifying with movement.”
THE INNER SHIFT
The ordinary mind says:
“I must fix every storm.”
Wisdom says:
“Let the storm move through the sky of awareness.”
Clouds cannot stain the sky.
 Thoughts cannot stain consciousness.
SYMBOL OF THIS LESSON
A lamp inside a cave during heavy rain outside.
The storm roars.
 The wind moves.
 The trees bend.
But the flame remains steady.
That flame is awareness.
PRACTICE FOR RAMA AND LAKSHMANA
For this stage of training:
Watch emotions without naming yourself by them.
Sit silently when reactions arise.
Feel fully, but do not become the feeling.
Return again and again to simple witnessing.
Not suppression.
 Not indulgence.
Only clear seeing.
VASHISHTHA’S CLOSING WORD
“Rama,
 the one who fights the storm remains exhausted.
The one who watches the storm discovers
 that the sky was never disturbed.”
