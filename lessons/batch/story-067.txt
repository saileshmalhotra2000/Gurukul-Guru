The Next Upadesha — The Discipline of Witnessing Without Grasping
O child of the solar lineage, Rama…
 and steadfast one, Lakshmana…
 listen as Vashishtha now unfolds the next step on your path.
You have seen that all arises in awareness.
 But still, the ancient habit remains —
 the hand of the mind reaches out… to hold, to reject, to shape.
Today, we refine this.
🌿 The Teaching
As the wind moves through the forest,
 it does not cling to the fragrance of flowers,
 nor resist the scent of decay.
So too must you become.
Let sight arise — but do not lean into it.
 Let sound arise — but do not follow it.
 Let thought arise — but do not become it.
You are not asked to destroy experience.
 You are asked to cease grasping at it.
🔥 The Subtle Error
Even now, a hidden movement persists:
“I understand.”
 “I am aware.”
 “I am progressing.”
These too are ripples in the same field.
If you grasp even awareness as an achievement,
 you have made it into an object —
 and placed yourself outside it.
But you are not outside.
🌊 The Deeper Recognition
See this clearly:
The river flows — yet you do not step into it.
 The bird sings — yet you do not follow it.
 The thought arises — yet you do not continue it.
All is allowed.
 Nothing is held.
This is freedom without withdrawal.
🕯️ Practice for This Day
Move through the world as before —
 but with one refinement:
Whatever appears,
 let it end where it begins.
Do not carry it forward.
A sound ends in hearing.
 A sight ends in seeing.
 A thought ends in awareness.
Let no echo remain.
🌺 The Fruit of This Practice
When grasping dissolves:
Time begins to loosen its hold
Identity becomes transparent
Effort falls away like a shed skin
And you will discover, O Rama —
 peace was never created…
 only uncovered.
Remain like the sky —
 untouched by the flight of birds.
Thus the journey deepens,
 not by adding knowledge…
 but by ceasing to hold anything at all.
