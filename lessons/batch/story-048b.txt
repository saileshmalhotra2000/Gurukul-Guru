Morning light filtered through the forest canopy, casting shifting patterns upon the gurukul grounds.
 The sacred fire crackled softly. Birds called from the trees.
 Before the venerable Rishi Vashishtha sat the princes—Rama calm and attentive, Lakshmana alert as ever.
Vashishtha looked upon them and said—
“Rama…
 You have learned to witness thought in stillness.
But wisdom is incomplete
 until witnessing remains unbroken in movement.”
Rama bowed.
“Gurudev, it is easier to remain aware in silence than in action.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Yes.
 For when action begins, the mind rushes outward.
The warrior forgets himself in battle.
 The speaker forgets himself in speech.
 The worker forgets himself in labor.
Thus hear today’s teaching:
Awareness must remain steady not only in stillness,
 but in the midst of activity.”
Lakshmana asked—
“How can one act fully and still remain aware, Gurudev?”
Vashishtha picked up a bowl filled to the brim with water and set it before them.
“If a servant carries this bowl through a crowded market,
 what must he remember?”
Lakshmana replied—
“To keep attention upon the bowl, Gurudev.”
“Even while walking?”
“Yes.”
“Even while avoiding others?”
“Yes.”
“Even while hearing noise?”
“Yes.”
Vashishtha smiled.
“So too must the seeker live.
Act in the world with full skill—
 yet keep part of attention resting in awareness itself.”
Rama reflected and asked—
“Should awareness then be divided, Gurudev?”
Vashishtha replied—
“Not divided—
 rooted.
A tree may extend many branches in all directions,
 yet its life remains rooted in one place.
Likewise:
Let action extend outward,
 while awareness remains rooted inward.”
He continued—
“When speaking—
Know that speaking is occurring.
When walking—
Know that walking is occurring.
When anger arises—
Know that anger is arising.
When praise or blame comes—
Know their effect upon the mind
 without becoming that effect.”
Rama asked—
“And if emotion becomes strong?”
Vashishtha answered—
“Then shorten the field of action.
Do less outwardly.
 Return more inwardly.
For one who cannot remain aware in storm
 must first regain stability in lesser winds.”
Today’s Practice
“Throughout this day:
Choose ordinary actions—walking, eating, training, speaking.
While doing them, maintain the silent recognition:
‘Action is happening within awareness.’
 ‘The body moves; the Self remains still.’”
Final Upadesh
Vashishtha’s voice grew quiet.
“Rama…
 The immature seeker is aware only in meditation.
The mature seeker remains aware in action.
When awareness survives movement,
 mastery has begun.”
The princes bowed deeply,
 and the forest returned to silence.
