In the quiet forest, after observing the princes in their practice, Rishi Vashishtha spoke again to Rama and Lakshmana:
🪶 The Movement of Mind within Stillness
“Rama,” said the sage,
 “you have begun to see that attention can move, and awareness can remain.
Now understand this deeper truth:
Mind will continue to move…
but you need not move with it.”
🌊 Thoughts are Movements, Not Identity
When a thought arises:
It appears
It stays for a moment
It dissolves
Just like ripples in the river beside you.
Do you become the ripple?
Or do you remain the river?
“You are not the thought, Rama.
 You are that in which the thought appears.”
🔥 The Subtle Trap
Lakshmana asked,
“Gurudev, even when I understand this, sometimes emotions pull me strongly.”
Vashishtha replied:
“That is because you are still touching the thought and calling it ‘mine.’”
The moment you say:
my anger
my fear
my distraction
👉 Identification is born
 👉 Disturbance follows
🕊️ Practice of Non-Identification
From today, practice this during all activity:
When a thought or emotion arises, do not fight it.
Do not follow it.
Simply recognize:
“This is a movement in the mind…
 not myself.”
Let it pass as clouds pass in the sky.
⚖️ Acting Without Inner Disturbance
“Rama,” said the sage,
“You must not become inactive.
 You must act—but without becoming disturbed within.”
This is the balance:
Hands act
Mind thinks
Attention moves
But…
👉 The witness remains untouched
🧘 Stabilizing Awareness During Activity
Do not wait for silence or meditation.
While walking…
While speaking…
While practicing…
Gently know:
“All this is happening within awareness.”
Even if attention becomes narrow—
 even if mind becomes active—
👉 Return, again and again, to this knowing.
Not forcefully.
But like remembering your own name.
🪔 The Guru’s Final Words for Today
Vashishtha looked deeply at Rama:
“Do not try to stop the mind.
 Do not try to control every thought.
Simply stop calling them yourself.
Then even in the midst of action…
 you will remain free.”
🌿 Today’s Inner Practice
Throughout the day, remember this single sentence:
“This is seen… therefore it is not me.”
Let this become your silent companion.
