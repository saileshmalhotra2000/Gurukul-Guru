Rishi Vashishtha spoke again to Rama and Lakshmana, now guiding them from witnessing into effortless steadiness amidst action.
The Subtle Gap in Activity
“Rama,” said Vashishtha,
 “Between two thoughts, there is a gap. Between two actions, there is stillness. Learn to notice this.”
When you begin an action, pause inwardly for a fraction
When one thought ends, do not rush to the next
Rest in that silent interval
This gap is not absence—it is pure awareness itself.
Awareness Behind Movement
“Lakshmana, even as the arrow leaves the bow, the archer remains steady.”
So too:
Let speech arise, but remain aware behind it
Let decisions happen, but remain untouched within
Let the body move, but remain as the unmoving witness
Do not try to hold awareness forcefully—
 let it remain like the sky behind all clouds
The Dissolving of Identification
Vashishtha continued:
“When you do not chase thoughts, they weaken. When you do not resist emotions, they dissolve.”
Observe carefully:
Identification says: ‘I am angry’
Wisdom sees: ‘Anger is arising and passing’
This shift alone frees the mind from bondage.
Action Without Inner Echo
“Rama,” he said softly,
 “The ignorant act and then carry the echo of action within them.”
But the wise:
Act completely in the moment
Leave no residue in the mind
Do not revisit, replay, or cling
Thus, action ends where it happens—
 and the mind remains undisturbed.
The Living Integration
Now the teaching deepens:
Witness is not only in stillness
It must remain unbroken in movement
Walking, speaking, deciding—
 all happen in the field of awareness,
 but awareness itself does not move.
Closing Insight
Vashishtha looked upon them with compassion:
“Rama, do not try to control the river of life.
 Simply know that you are not the river.”
Then:
Thoughts flow — you remain
Emotions rise — you remain
Actions occur — you remain
Abide as that which remains.
