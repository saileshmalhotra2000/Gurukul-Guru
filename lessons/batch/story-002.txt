Gurukul — Dawn Teaching
The morning air is cool. A thin mist rests over the river Sarayu.
 Rama and Lakshmana sit before Rishi Vashishta.
A wooden staff lies beside the Rishi.
A bow rests near Lakshmana.
Rama’s hands are folded loosely in his lap.
Silence stretches.
Vashishta opens his eyes.
Vashishta:
 “Rama… Lakshmana… yesterday you practiced holding attention.
 Today we shall see what holds you.”
Lakshmana’s eyes sharpen slightly.
Rama listens.
A young disciple approaches hurriedly and whispers something into Rishi's ear. Rishi nods. The boy leaves.
Vashishta turns to the princes.
“There is news from Ayodhya.
 Another kingdom speaks ill of your father.”
Lakshmana’s jaw tightens instantly.
“Who dares?” he bursts out.
Rama’s breath deepens — just slightly.
Vashishta watches both without expression.
Vashishta (calmly):
 “Lakshmana, what rose in you?”
“Anger, Gurudev.”
“And if anger commands you?”
Lakshmana hesitates.
 “I would like to fight.”
“Even without knowing the truth?”
Lakshmana lowers his gaze.
Vashishta turns to Rama.
“And you, Rama?”
Rama speaks slowly.
“There was heat in my chest, Gurudev… but also a question.”
“What question?”
“Is it true?”
Rishi's eyes soften.
He draws a line in the sand with his staff.
“This line is your nature.”
He draws another line crossing it.
“This is the world.”
“Every day the world will cross your nature — through insult, praise, loss, gain.”
He looks at Lakshmana.
“If your reaction is immediate, the world controls you.”
He looks at Rama.
“If there is a pause… you remain sovereign.”
Lakshmana frowns slightly.
“Gurudev, is anger always wrong?”
Vashishta smiles faintly.
“No, child.
 But anger must obey you.
 You must not obey anger.”
He taps the staff lightly against the ground.
“Strength of character is not absence of fire.
 It is  command over fire.”
He stands.
“Now both of you stand.”
They rise.
“Lakshmana — insult Rama.”
Lakshmana freezes.
“Gurudev…?”
“Do it.”
Lakshmana turns reluctantly.
“Brother… you are too soft for war.”
The words land.
A bird calls in the distance.
Vashishta watches Rama carefully.
Inside Rama, there is a flicker —
 a tightening in the throat —
 a brief spark of defense rising.
He feels it.
He does not suppress it.
He lets it pass.
Rama bows slightly to Lakshmana.
“Thank you.”
Lakshmana’s eyes widened.
Vashishta nods.
“Rama, what moved within you?”
“A wish to defend myself, Gurudev.”
“And why did you not?”
“It would have been to protect pride, not Dharma.”
Silence.
The river moves quietly beside them.
Vashishta speaks softly now.
“Remember this, both of you:
The world will test your pride before it tests your skill.
If you cannot endure a word,
 you cannot endure a war.”
He looks at Lakshmana.
“Your fire is precious. Guard it.”
He looks at Rama.
“Your steadiness is powerful. Do not let it become passivity.”
The lamp between them burns steadily.
Now you sit there.
Feel honestly:
If someone spoke to you as Lakshmana did to Rama —
 what would rise first in you?
Not what should rise.
What would actually rise? 🌿
