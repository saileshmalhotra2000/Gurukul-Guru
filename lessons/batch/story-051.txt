The Witness in Motion
Vashishtha said:
“Rama, it is easy to feel peaceful when seated in stillness.
 The greater attainment is to remain established in awareness while life moves around you.”
Lakshmana asked:
“Gurudev, how may one remain the witness while acting?”
Vashishtha replied:
1. Let Action Happen in Awareness
Do not think:
“First I become aware, then I act.”
Rather understand:
Awareness and action can coexist.
Walk while aware.
Speak while aware.
Listen while aware.
Decide while aware.
Let awareness remain as the background of all movement.
2. Feel the Body Acting
While engaged in any task:
Observe:
the feet moving
the hands working
the breath flowing
the voice speaking
This anchors awareness in present activity and prevents full absorption into thought.
3. Notice Disturbance at Its Birth
When emotion arises—
before it becomes speech or reaction—
see its first movement:
tightening in the chest
heat in the face
contraction in the belly
urgency in thought
Witness disturbance in its seed form.
What is seen early is easier not to become.
4. Do Not Add “I” to Inner Weather
Instead of:
“I am angry”
 “I am anxious”
 “I am distracted”
See more accurately:
“Anger is arising”
 “Fear is arising”
 “Restlessness is present”
This loosens false identification.
You are the knower of the storm, not the storm.
5. Act From Clarity, Not From Agitation
When disturbed:
Do not rush to act.
First return to awareness.
Then act.
“Action born from agitation binds.
 Action born from clarity liberates.”
Practice for Today
Throughout the day, during ordinary activity:
Maintain slight awareness of breath or body.
When emotion arises, name it inwardly without “I.”
Delay reaction for one conscious breath.
Then choose action deliberately.
Vashishtha concluded:
“Rama, the witness established only in meditation is untested.
 The witness maintained in action becomes wisdom.
When awareness remains unshaken amidst movement,
 mastery begins.”
