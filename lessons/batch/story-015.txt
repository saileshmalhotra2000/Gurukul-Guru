The night rain had washed the forest.
Leaves still held small drops of water that caught the morning light. The air was cool and clean, and the earth smelled dark and alive.
Mist drifted slowly above the river.
Rama and Lakshmana had risen before dawn, as was their habit. They had finished their morning duties and now walked toward the eastern edge of the ashram where the river bent around a wide stretch of sand.
Vashishtha was already there.
He stood near the water, looking at the slow current.
The rising sun had just begun to touch the surface of the river, turning it pale gold.
Rama and Lakshmana approached quietly and bowed.
Vashishtha acknowledged them with a slight nod but did not speak.
For a long time he continued looking at the river.
Then he said softly,
“Sit.”
They sat beside him on the cool sand.
The river moved steadily before them.
After some time Vashishtha spoke.
“Yesterday we saw how attention wanders.”
Rama nodded.
Vashishtha picked up a small pebble from the sand.
He held it between his fingers.
“Rama.”
“Yes, Gurudev.”
“Take this pebble.”
Rama accepted it.
“Throw it into the river.”
Rama rose, stepped toward the water, and tossed the pebble gently into the current.
It disappeared with a small sound.
Ripples spread outward across the surface of the river.
They watched the widening circles.
Soon the surface of the water returned to its quiet flow.
Vashishtha asked,
“What did you see?”
Lakshmana answered first.
“The pebble disturbed the water.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“And then?”
Rama said,
“The ripples faded.”
Vashishtha looked at the river again.
“Did the river go somewhere to remove the ripples?”
“No, Gurudev.”
“Did the river struggle to become calm again?”
“No.”
“The disturbance appeared.”
“The disturbance disappeared.”
“The river remained the river.”
They sat quietly.
The sun had risen higher now. A pair of cranes walked slowly through the shallow water downstream.
Then Vashishtha turned slightly toward Rama.
“Yesterday, when the bird appeared, your attention moved.”
“Yes, Gurudev.”
“Who moved it?”
Rama did not answer immediately.
He looked down at the sand.
Lakshmana looked between his brother and the guru.
Rama finally said,
“My mind moved toward the bird.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“And who saw the mind move?”
Rama looked up slowly.
There was silence.
The river flowed beside them.
Lakshmana frowned slightly, thinking.
Vashishtha did not hurry the moment.
A breeze passed across the water.
Rama spoke quietly.
“I saw it, Gurudev.”
Vashishtha’s eyes softened.
“Yes.”
Lakshmana leaned forward slightly.
“Then… Rama and the mind are not the same?”
Vashishtha looked at him.
“Tell me, Lakshmana.”
“When a cloud passes across the sky, does the sky move?”
“No, Gurudev.”
“When waves move on the river, does the river cease to exist?”
“No.”
Vashishtha drew a small circle in the sand with his finger.
“The mind moves.”
“Thoughts appear.”
“Attention shifts.”
Then he gently erased the circle with his palm.
“But something sees all of this.”
Rama’s gaze had grown very still.
Vashishtha looked directly at him.
“Yesterday you saw attention move toward the bird.”
“Today you must begin to see the one who noticed it move.”
Lakshmana asked slowly,
“Is that the self, Gurudev?”
Vashishtha shook his head gently.
“Do not hurry to give it a name.”
“First see it.”
The three of them fell into silence again.
The sun rose higher above the trees.
The river glowed brighter.
After a long time Vashishtha stood.
“Come.”
They followed him along the riverbank.
As they walked, he said,
“Today you will watch your own attention.”
“Not to control it.”
“Only to see it.”
They walked through tall grasses where dragonflies moved above the water.
Vashishtha stopped near a fallen tree trunk.
He turned to them.
“When attention moves…”
“…simply notice.”
“When a thought appears…”
“…simply notice.”
“When the mind becomes restless…”
“…notice that also.”
Lakshmana asked,
“And who should notice, Gurudev?”
Vashishtha looked at the quiet river beside them.
A faint smile touched his face.
“That is the discovery.”
He began walking back toward the ashram.
Behind him, Rama and Lakshmana followed in thoughtful silence.
The forest seemed to listen with them.
