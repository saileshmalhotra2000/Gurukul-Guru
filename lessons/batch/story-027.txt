The morning air was cool, and a soft wind moved through the trees.
Vashishtha was walking slowly along a narrow path.
Rama and Lakshmana followed behind him.
After some distance, Vashishtha stopped… but did not turn.
He simply said:
“Walk ahead of me.”
The brothers exchanged a glance, then stepped forward.
They walked side by side.
At first, their pace was natural.
But soon, Lakshmana became aware:
“Gurudev is behind us.”
His steps changed slightly.
More careful.
More deliberate.
Rama too felt it.
A subtle tightening.
A quiet awareness of being observed.
After a while, Vashishtha spoke:
“Do you feel the difference?”
Rama answered,
“Yes, Gurudev.”
“There is more alertness.”
Lakshmana added,
“And a slight tension.”
Vashishtha nodded, still behind them.
“This is how most people live.”
“Not in awareness…”
“…but in the feeling of being watched.”
They continued walking.
The path curved gently.
Birds moved in the branches above.
But now, both brothers noticed something deeper:
Their attention was divided.
part on walking
part on themselves
part on Vashishtha behind
Not fully anywhere.
After some time, Vashishtha said:
“Now… forget that I am here.”
“Walk as if you are alone.”
They tried.
But the thought remained:
“He is still behind us.”
Lakshmana smiled slightly.
“It is difficult to forget.”
Vashishtha’s voice came calm and clear:
“Yes.”
“Because you carry the watcher inside.”
They slowed their steps.
“What you feel is not me…”
“…but your own mind observing itself.”
Rama’s expression changed.
A deeper understanding arose.
“This is why the mind cannot rest…”
“…it is always watching, judging, adjusting.”
Vashishtha replied,
“Just so.”
They walked further.
Then Vashishtha gave a simple instruction:
“Do not try to remove the watcher.”
“Simply stop performing for it.”
Lakshmana relaxed his shoulders slightly.
His steps became more natural.
Rama allowed his breath to settle.
For a few moments…
walking happened without self-consciousness.
No effort to appear correct.
No inner commentary.
Just movement.
Vashishtha came forward and stood beside them now.
“Did you feel it?”
Rama nodded.
“There was ease.”
Lakshmana added,
“And less division.”
Vashishtha looked ahead at the path.
“When you act for the watcher…”
“…you become divided.”
“When you act from simple awareness…”
“…you become whole.”
They walked the rest of the path in silence.
This time, there was no sense of being observed.
Only the rhythm of steps…
the sound of wind…
and a quiet, unbroken presence.
And somewhere within…
the watcher had become still.
