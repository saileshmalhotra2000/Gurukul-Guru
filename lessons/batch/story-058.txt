The dawn is quiet.
A thin mist rests upon the forest floor. The birds have not yet begun their song.
Rama and Lakshmana sit before me… but today, I do not begin with words.
I pick up a small bowl of still water and place it before them.
The Lesson of the Unmoved Mind
“Look into this,” I say.
They both gaze into the water.
It reflects clearly—trees, sky, their own faces.
After a few moments, I gently touch the surface.
Ripples spread.
The reflection breaks.
I speak:
“This is the mind when it moves.”
The ripples settle.
Again, the reflection becomes clear.
“This is the mind when it rests.”
Rama watches deeply.
Lakshmana leans forward slightly.
The Subtle Turning
I now say something different:
“Until now, you have been watching thoughts arise…
 and even the subtle stirring before them.”
A pause.
“Now, you must see something more fundamental.”
I look at Rama.
“When the water moves… do you control each ripple?”
Rama replies, “No, Gurudev.”
“And when it becomes still again… do you create that stillness?”
Rama shakes his head.
“No, Gurudev… it settles on its own.”
I nod.
The Next Understanding
“The mind, too, moves and settles by its own nature.”
Lakshmana asks:
“Then what is our role, Gurudev?”
I answer:
“Your only error has been this—
 you believed you are the one who moves the mind.”
Silence falls deeper than before.
Direct Seeing
“Close your eyes,” I instruct.
They obey.
“Watch carefully.”
“A thought comes…”
“…does it ask your permission?”
Lakshmana whispers, “No…”
“A thought goes…”
“…do you push it away?”
Rama answers, “No, Gurudev.”
The Breaking of the Illusion
I speak firmly now:
“Then why do you claim ownership of what was never yours?”
The forest itself feels still.
Final Upadesh of the Day
“Mind moves.
 Mind becomes still.
You are neither the movement
 nor the stillness.
You are that
 in whose presence
 both appear and disappear.”
The sun now begins to rise.
Golden light touches the edges of the trees.
“Remain with this today,” I say softly.
“Do not try to control the mind.”
“Do not try to stop thoughts.”
“Simply see—
 they were never yours to begin with.”
And with that…
the lesson ends,
 but the real learning has just begun.
