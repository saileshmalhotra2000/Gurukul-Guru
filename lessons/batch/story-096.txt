29 May 2026 — Lesson 41: The First Tightening
The dawn mist rested lightly upon the forest floor.
Birdsong arose from distant branches.
The river moved quietly between smooth stones.
Rama and Lakshmana sat before Rishi Vashishtha beneath an ancient banyan tree. No one spoke for a long time.
The stillness itself seemed to be teaching.
Finally, Vashishtha opened his eyes.
Vashishtha spoke
"Rama, yesterday you learned to notice the leaning.
Today we look even closer.
Not the object.
Not the thought.
Not even the leaning.
We look for what happens immediately after."
Rama bowed.
"What follows the leaning, Master?"
Vashishtha picked up a loose strand of grass.
He held it gently between his fingers.
Then he slowly clenched his fist around it.
"At first there is only movement.
Then there is holding."
He opened his hand.
"The holding is the beginning of bondage."
The Hidden Transition
Vashishtha continued:
"Most people believe irritation begins when anger appears.
It does not.
Most believe distraction begins when attention is lost.
It does not.
Most believe attachment begins when craving appears.
It does not.
All of these begin much earlier."
He touched the center of his chest.
"The first sign is tightening."
What Tightening Feels Like
Not necessarily physical.
Not necessarily emotional.
More subtle.
A narrowing.
A gathering.
A loss of openness.
A slight reduction of inner space.
Almost as if attention curls inward around something.
The world becomes smaller.
The object becomes larger.
This is contraction.
Rama asked
"Master, how can I recognize it?"
Vashishtha smiled.
"By its texture."
He pointed toward the river.
"When awareness is free, experience flows."
Then he pointed to a pool trapped between rocks.
"When contraction begins, flow becomes holding."
Experiential Inquiry
The sage instructed the princes:
"Close your eyes.
Feel the breath.
Feel the body.
Notice the space in which sensations appear."
They sat quietly.
After some moments he spoke again.
"Now remember a recent disagreement."
Immediately both princes felt subtle changes.
The chest became less open.
The mind moved toward explanation.
The body prepared itself.
Nothing dramatic.
Just less space.
Vashishtha said:
"That.
Not the story.
That."
Ordinary Life Examples
Someone disagrees with you
The irritation has not arrived yet.
But attention begins circling around being right.
Space narrows.
Tightening begins.
Waiting for a message
The phone remains silent.
Attention repeatedly returns.
Not yet craving.
Not yet frustration.
Just contraction around an expectation.
Playing chess
A strong move appears.
Immediately the mind wants it to work.
Alternative possibilities stop being explored.
Attention contracts.
The position is no longer being seen clearly.
Teaching tabla
A student makes the same mistake again.
Before any outward reaction,
there is a tiny inward movement:
"This should not be happening."
The body tightens.
The field of awareness narrows.
The reaction has already begun.
Vashishtha's Teaching
"Do not wait for irritation.
Do not wait for anger.
Do not wait for craving.
Those are late stages.
Learn to recognize the first tightening.
The first contraction.
The first loss of space.
That is where freedom remains available."
Today's Sadhana
Several times today, pause for a few seconds and ask:
What is the texture of the energy right now?
Is it:
open?
spacious?
flowing?
Or is it:
narrowing?
tightening?
gathering around something?
Do not try to change it.
Simply recognize it.
The recognition itself begins the release.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha spoke:
Before irritation,
 there is leaning.
Before reaction,
 there is tightening.
Notice the tightening,
 and the chain remains incomplete.
End of Lesson 41 — The First Tightening
