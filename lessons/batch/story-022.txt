The morning air was cool, and a soft mist hovered above the river.
Rama and Lakshmana arrived to find Vashishtha seated beside a small, still pond hidden behind thick reeds.
Unlike the flowing river, this water was quiet… almost unmoving.
Vashishtha did not speak immediately.
He simply pointed toward the surface.
“Look.”
The brothers stepped closer.
At first, they saw only their reflections.
Clear. Undisturbed.
Then a single leaf fell from above and touched the water.
Ripples spread outward.
Their reflections broke, distorted into fragments.
Lakshmana watched closely.
“It changed so quickly.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Yes.”
He picked up a small pebble and dropped it into the pond.
More ripples. Stronger this time.
The water trembled.
The reflections vanished completely.
Then he turned to them.
“Tell me—did your faces change?”
Rama answered calmly,
“No, Gurudev.”
“But what you saw changed,” Vashishtha said.
“Yes.”
He looked again at the pond.
“The mind is like this water.”
“When it is still, it reflects truth clearly.”
“When disturbed, it distorts everything.”
Lakshmana crossed his arms slightly, thinking.
“So when the mind is disturbed…”
“…you do not see things as they are,” Rama completed.
Vashishtha smiled.
“Just so.”
A gentle breeze passed over the pond.
Small ripples appeared again, even without a pebble.
“Notice this,” Vashishtha said.
“Not all disturbance comes from great events.”
“Even small movements… small thoughts… can disturb clarity.”
Lakshmana frowned slightly.
“Then how can the mind ever remain still?”
Vashishtha looked at him steadily.
“By not chasing every ripple.”
Silence settled again.
The pond slowly began to calm.
The surface softened.
Shapes returned.
Reflections reformed.
“Stillness is not created,” Vashishtha continued.
“It is revealed when disturbance is not fed.”
Rama’s gaze remained fixed on the water.
“So the goal is not to force silence…”
“…but to stop adding noise,” Lakshmana said quietly.
Vashishtha’s eyes shone with approval.
They sat together without speaking.
Time passed.
The pond became completely still again.
This time, the reflection was even clearer than before.
Not just their faces…
…but the sky, the trees, the light.
Everything appeared in perfect detail.
Vashishtha spoke one final teaching for the day:
“A disturbed mind reacts.”
“A still mind reflects.”
“A mastered mind understands.”
He rose slowly.
“Remember this, Rama.”
The brothers stood and followed him.
“In moments of confusion…”
“…do not trust the ripples.”
“Wait for the water to become clear.”
They walked back through the quiet forest.
Behind them, the pond remained undisturbed…
holding the sky within itself.
