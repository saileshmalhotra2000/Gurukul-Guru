The First Leaning
Before desire becomes action…
 before anger becomes speech…
 before distraction becomes loss of presence…
there is:
a micro-turn of attention
a slight energetic tilt
a movement toward or away
This is the doorway.
Most people notice only the final action.
The yogi notices:
the leaning before the action.
Vashishtha’s Teaching
Leaning toward
The mind moves toward pleasure, praise, stimulation, comfort.
You feel:
pulling
anticipation
grasping
acceleration
Leaning against
The mind moves away from discomfort.
You feel:
tightening
resistance
inner “no”
subtle contraction
Leaning into imagination
Attention leaves direct experience and enters mental imagery.
The body remains here.
Attention does not.
Leaning toward future
The nervous system exits the present and begins rehearsing.
Not life.
Possibilities.
Leaning toward past
Energy returns to memory trying to reopen unfinished experience.
The body is now.
Attention is then.
The Real Practice
Do not fight leaning.
Do not suppress it.
Do not judge it.
Instead:
Notice the exact moment attention tilts
Feel the sensation in the body
Stay relaxed
Return softly
Again.
And again.
And again.
Vashishtha said quietly:
“Freedom is not gained
 by controlling every movement of mind.
Freedom begins
 when the leaning is seen early.”
