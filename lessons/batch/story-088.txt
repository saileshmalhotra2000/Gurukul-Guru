The First Taste of Inner Momentum
The morning mist still floated above the river.
Rama sat quietly beneath the sal tree,
 watching the current move over the stones.
No question arose in him.
For the first time,
 he was not trying to control the mind.
He was studying it.
Vashishtha watched silently for a long while,
 then spoke:
“Rama…
 before action,
 there is a leaning.”
Lakshmana looked confused.
“A leaning, Gurudev?”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Not yet thought.
 Not yet movement.
A subtle current.
Attention begins to bend toward something.”
He picked up a dry leaf
 and released it into the stream.
At first the leaf barely moved.
Then slowly,
 almost invisibly,
 the current took hold.
Only later did the movement become obvious.
Vashishtha looked toward Rama.
“Most people notice action
 only after the feet are already moving.
The yogi learns to detect movement
 while it is still unborn.”
Rama closed his eyes.
At first he noticed nothing.
Then—
a tiny impulse to adjust posture.
Before the body moved,
 there was something subtler:
a forward tendency.
An energetic preparation.
Not physical.
Not verbal.
Just a faint inner movement
 leaning toward action.
His eyes opened slightly.
“Gurudev…
 it feels like attention itself
 begins moving first.”
A small smile appeared on Vashishtha’s face.
“Yes.
Action is only the final echo.
The deeper movement begins earlier.”
Lakshmana asked:
“Can this be stopped?”
Vashishtha shook his head gently.
“Do not interfere yet.
First learn to see.
The one who interferes too early
 creates suppression.
The one who observes clearly
 discovers freedom.”
He drew four lines in the sand:
The Sequence
Contact
Stirring
Inner leaning
Action
Then he erased the final line with his hand.
“Most humans only know step four.
Sadhana begins
 when awareness enters step three.”
The forest had become utterly still.
Even the birds seemed distant.
Then Vashishtha gave the day’s practice:
Today’s Gurukul Practice
Several times today,
 pause during ordinary activities.
Not after movement.
Before movement.
Especially before:
speaking
reaching
checking something
reacting
standing up
turning the head
See if you can detect:
the subtle energetic leaning
the gathering of attention
the beginning of momentum
Do not stop the action.
Just witness the birth of movement.
As the sun rose through the trees,
 Rama sat unmoving beside the river.
And for the first time in his life,
 he began sensing
 that actions were not appearing suddenly.
They were arriving
 from deeper currents within consciousness itself.
