Gurukul Lesson — The Fascination of Attention

The dawn was unusually quiet.

Not a single leaf moved.

The river flowed as it always had—unhurried, undisturbed.

Rama sat beside the water, watching its surface.

After a long silence he spoke.

"Master..."

"What if Peace never left..."

"...and it was only attention that became fascinated with the disturbance?"

Vashishtha did not answer.

Instead, he picked up a small crystal from the riverbank.

He held it toward the rising sun.

Immediately, a brilliant ray of light danced upon the ground.

The sage slowly moved the crystal.

The ray moved with it.

"Rama..."

"Did the sun move?"

"No, Master."

"Did the light disappear?"

"No."

"What moved?"

Rama watched the dancing beam.

"The crystal."

Vashishtha smiled.

"And because the crystal moved..."

"the light appeared somewhere else."

The sage placed the crystal on the ground.

The sunlight filled the entire forest again.

Nothing had been lost.

Only the point of reflection had changed.

Then he looked at Rama.

"Attention is like this crystal."

"It illuminates whatever it rests upon."

He pointed toward the river.

"If attention rests upon the flowing water..."

There was openness.

"If attention rests upon a single floating leaf..."

Soon the whole river seems forgotten.

The princes remained silent.

The sage picked up a single dry leaf.

He placed it gently upon the river.

The river continued flowing.

The leaf drifted quietly downstream.

Then Vashishtha asked,

"Which is larger?"

"The leaf..."

"Or the river?"

"The river."

"Yet if attention clings only to the leaf..."

"What appears to become your world?"

Rama smiled.

"The leaf."

The sage nodded.

"And when attention becomes fascinated with disturbance..."

"The disturbance begins to feel larger than Peace."

He drew nothing in the sand today.

Instead, he simply swept away yesterday's circles with his hand.

Source.

Reminder.

Harmony.

Peace.

All disappeared beneath the smooth earth.

"What remained?"

Lakshmana looked around.

"The river."

"The forest."

"The sky."

Vashishtha smiled.

"They remained before the circles."

"They remained after the circles."

"So too..."

He gently touched Rama's chest.

"...that which knows Peace."

The river continued flowing.

It had never struggled to remain a river.

🌿 Today's Sadhana

Three times today, when attention becomes absorbed in a disturbance, pause for a single breath and ask:

What is larger right now...

the disturbance...

or the field in which I am noticing it?

Do not answer.

Simply allow attention to widen.

Notice whether the disturbance changes size.

🌅 Final Teaching

Attention does not create reality.

It creates prominence.

What fills awareness is not always what is greatest...

...only what attention has chosen to illuminate.
