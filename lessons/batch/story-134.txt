Am I Looking at the Bowl or the Whole River?

The morning river was wide and full.
Sunlight moved across its surface like a quiet blessing.

Rama sat near the bank, still carrying yesterday's inquiry.

Master, he said, when attention rests on one disturbance, it becomes large.
But how do I remember the larger field?

Vashishtha did not answer at once.

He took a small wooden bowl and filled it with river water.
Then he placed a single dry leaf upon the water in the bowl.

Look, Rama.

Rama looked into the bowl.
The leaf seemed large.
It filled the small circle of water.

Now look at the river.

Rama lifted his eyes.
The same river flowed before him, vast and untroubled.

Vashishtha asked gently,

Am I looking at the bowl...
or the whole river?

Lakshmana smiled.

In the bowl, the leaf seems important.
In the river, it is only passing.

The sage nodded.

This is how attention narrows.
It takes a small portion of life and calls it the whole.

The disturbance is not always large.
The bowl is small.

Rama became very still.

So the practice is not to fight the leaf?

No, said Vashishtha.
The practice is to notice the bowl.

The river continued flowing.
It had room for leaf, ripple, stone, reflection, and sky.

Today's Sadhana
When one disturbance seems to fill the whole mind, ask gently: Am I looking at the bowl, or the whole river?

Do not push the disturbance away.
Let attention widen.

Final Teaching
The small becomes overwhelming when attention forgets the vast. Return from the bowl to the river.
