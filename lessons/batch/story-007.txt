Tuesday, 3 March 2026
The dawn mist lies low over the river. The forest is pale with first light. A koel calls once, then falls silent.
Rama sits with eyes half-closed. Lakshmana watches a line of ants carrying crumbs twice their size.
Vashishtha draws a long breath of the cool air.
“Yesterday,” he says softly, “we placed the ego in its rightful seat — as a servant.”
He looks at Rama.
“But today we must see what happens when the servant is wounded.”
Lakshmana glances up.
“Wounded, Gurudev?”
“Yes.”
He lifts a small twig and snaps it cleanly in two.
“When someone ignores you.
When someone chooses another over you.
When someone does not see your effort.”
Rama’s eyes open fully.
Vashishtha’s gaze rests on him.
“What moves first in such a moment?”
Rama answers quietly.
“A tightening.”
“Where?”
“In the chest.”
Lakshmana nods. “And heat in the face.”
Vashishtha smiles faintly.
“The servant says, ‘I was not acknowledged.’”
He draws a small line in the sand.
“Recognition is food for ego.”
He draws another line crossing it.
“When the food is withheld, it feels diminished.”
Rama watches the lines.
“Is that wrong, Gurudev?”
“No.”
The answer is immediate.
“Even a servant must eat.”
Silence stretches gently.
“But watch carefully,” Vashishtha continues.
“When the ego is not fed, two paths arise.”
He presses the twig into the earth.
“One — it becomes small and resentful.”
He presses deeper.
“Two — it becomes spacious and steady.”
Lakshmana frowns slightly.
“How can it become spacious if it feels hurt?”
Vashishtha turns toward the river.
“When the river meets a rock, does it argue?”
“No.”
“Does it stop?”
“No.”
“It flows around.”
He looks back at them.
“If recognition does not come, let action still be pure.”
Rama’s breath slows.
“But if the ego says, ‘I will not give unless I am praised,’
then it has left its dharma.”
Lakshmana lowers his gaze.
Vashishtha continues gently.
“The servant’s dharma is service.
Not applause.”
A breeze stirs the leaves.
Rama speaks after a long pause.
“And if hurt remains?”
Vashishtha nods.
“Then sit with it.”
He places his palm over his heart.
“Do not cover it with pride.
Do not strike outward.”
He closes his eyes briefly.
“Feel the ache of being unseen.”
Silence deepens.
“In that ache,” he says softly,
“you will discover how much you depended on being reflected.”
Lakshmana whispers,
“Reflected?”
“Yes.”
He gestures toward the river again.
“When you look into still water, you see yourself.”
They both look.
“If the water is disturbed, you think you have disappeared.”
Rama inhales slowly.
“But you have not.”
Vashishtha’s voice grows very quiet.
“When ego rests in its true dharma,
it performs action whether seen or unseen.”
He lifts the clay pot from yesterday and fills it from the river.
“Does the pot ask the river to admire it?”
“No.”
“It simply carries.”
He sets the pot down between them.
“Let your ego be like this.”
The sun now touches the treetops. Gold slips into the clearing.
Vashishtha rises slowly.
“Today you will serve in the ashram without announcing your work.”
Lakshmana’s eyes brighten.
“And if no one notices?”
Vashishtha’s gaze sharpens kindly.
“Then you will notice.”
Rama bows his head slightly.
The forest awakens fully — birds calling, leaves trembling, light spreading.
And somewhere within, the servant learns to stand without demand.
Tuesday, 3 March 2026
You said Even the servant must eat. What food keeps ego healthy
The river is quiet this morning.
A soft mist rises again, though the sun is already climbing. Rama watches the current. Lakshmana turns to Vashishtha.
“Gurudev… you said even the servant must eat. What food keeps the ego healthy?”
Vashishtha folds his hands loosely in his lap.
“A good question.”
He points toward the ashram kitchen where smoke rises gently.
“If a servant is never fed, what happens?”
Lakshmana answers quickly.
“He grows weak… resentful.”
“And if he is overfed?”
“He grows lazy… demanding.”
Vashishtha nods.
“So it is with ego.”
He draws a small circle in the sand.
“The right food for ego is responsibility.”
Rama listens carefully.
“When you say, ‘I will do this well,’
ego is nourished properly.”
He draws another small mark.
“When you keep your word,
ego stands upright.”
Lakshmana tilts his head.
“So achievement feeds it?”
Vashishtha shakes his head gently.
“Not applause. Fulfillment of duty.”
He looks at Rama.
“When you protect another.
When you complete what you began.
When you correct your mistake without collapsing.”
He pauses.
“That is wholesome food.”
Lakshmana asks,
“And praise?”
Vashishtha smiles faintly.
“Praise is like sweetmeats.”
The boys smile slightly.
“A little does no harm.
Too much disturbs balance.”
Rama speaks softly.
“And what starves ego?”
Vashishtha replies calmly,
“Neglect of responsibility.”
He gestures toward the riverbank.
“If you avoid action out of fear,
ego shrinks.”
“If you depend only on approval,
ego becomes fragile.”
He looks at both of them steadily.
“A healthy ego says:
‘I am responsible.’
‘I can act.’
‘I can learn.’”
He taps his chest lightly.
“A sick ego says:
‘I must be admired.’
‘I must never fail.’
‘I am nothing without praise.’”
Silence stretches gently.
Lakshmana asks,
“How do we know it is healthy?”
Vashishtha answers without hesitation.
“When you can act fully…
and rest peacefully.”
The river moves without urgency.
“When action is done and you do not replay it endlessly,
ego is balanced.”
Rama’s breathing deepens.
“And when criticism comes?”
“If it is true, you learn.
If it is false, you remain steady.”
He gestures toward the pot beside them.
“The pot holds water.
It does not beg the river to confirm its worth.”
The mist thins.
Vashishtha concludes softly:
“Feed ego with duty.
Water it with humility.
Do not intoxicate it with constant praise.”
The sun warms the clearing.
And the servant within learns that its strength comes not from applause…
but from standing upright in its work. 🌿
