Morning mist lay low upon the forest floor.
 Birdsong drifted softly through the sal trees.
 The first rays of dawn filtered through the leaves, falling upon the quiet āshram where young princes sat before their guru.
Rishi Vashishtha sat upon a flat stone beside the sacred fire.
 Before him, with straight backs and attentive eyes, sat Rama and Lakshmana.
For a time, no one spoke.
Then Vashishtha said quietly—
The Mind Must Become Servant, Not Master
“Rama… yesterday you learned that an impulse is not a command.”
Rama bowed.
“Yes, Gurudev.
 A thought or emotion may arise…
 but I need not obey it.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Good.
 But now you must understand something subtler.
Even when you do not obey every impulse—
 the mind still performs many functions.
Tell me, Rama…
 when preparing for battle, must the mind not plan?”
“It must, Gurudev.”
“When recalling scripture, must the mind not remember?”
“It must.”
“When solving a problem, must the mind not analyze?”
“Yes, Gurudev.”
“When discerning right from wrong, must the mind not judge?”
“Yes.”
Vashishtha leaned forward.
“Then hear carefully:
The goal is not to stop the mind from functioning.
 The goal is to stop mistaking yourself for its movements.”
Watching the Mind at Work
Lakshmana asked—
“Gurudev, then how should we relate to thought?”
Vashishtha pointed toward the stable nearby.
“As a king watches a skilled servant.
When the servant works well—
 the king does not interfere.
But neither does the king forget he is the king.”
He looked at Rama.
“So too with the mind.
When thought arises:
Planning may appear
Memory may appear
Analysis may appear
Judgment may appear
Let them function.
But remain clear:
‘The mind is operating;
 I am the awareness in which it operates.’”
The Difference Between Bondage and Service
Rama asked—
“How do I know whether thought is serving me or binding me, Gurudev?”
Vashishtha replied—
“When you forget yourself in thought,
 it becomes bondage.
When thought happens within awareness,
 it becomes service.”
He drew two circles in the dust.
“In the ignorant man:
Thought captures awareness.
In the wise man:
Thought appears within awareness.”
Today’s Practice
Vashishtha instructed:
“Throughout the day, observe the mind at work.
When planning happens, know:
‘Planning is appearing.’
When memory arises, know:
‘Memory is appearing.’
When judgment forms, know:
‘Judgment is appearing.’
Do not interfere.
Do not identify.
Simply remain as the field in which it all occurs.”
Final Upadesh
As the lesson ended, Vashishtha spoke one final sentence:
“Rama, when the mind is no longer mistaken for the Self,
 it becomes a sacred instrument.
Then thought serves wisdom
 instead of obscuring it.”
The forest fell silent once more.
And in that silence, the princes contemplated deeply.
