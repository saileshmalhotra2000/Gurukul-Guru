8 June 2026 — The First Taste of Preference
The ancient forest was waking.
Mist drifted above the river like a thin veil.
Birds called from the banyan trees.
Rama and Lakshmana sat beside Vashishtha beneath the morning light. The previous day's inquiry had left them attentive to the subtle movement called leaning.
After a long silence, Vashishtha picked up a fallen leaf.
He held it before them.
"Tell me what happens when this leaf is seen."
Rama replied,
"The leaf appears."
"Good," said Vashishtha.
"And then?"
Lakshmana answered,
"Attention may lean toward it."
Vashishtha nodded.
"Today we look even closer."
He placed the leaf upon the ground.
"Before attention fully leans, something even more subtle appears."
Rama watched carefully.
Vashishtha said softly:
"A tiny taste."
"A taste?" asked Rama.
"Yes."
"The mind has not yet formed a thought."
"It has not yet created a story."
"It has not yet said 'I want' or 'I dislike.'"
"But already there is the faintest flavor."
He paused.
"A preference."
The disciples listened closely.
"One experience feels slightly attractive."
"Another feels slightly unpleasant."
"That tiny flavor is often unnoticed."
"But attention begins to organize around it."
"And soon the leaning follows."
Dialogue
Rama:
 "Master, is preference the same as desire?"
Vashishtha:
 "No."
"Desire is preference that has gathered strength."
"The seed appears before the tree."
Lakshmana:
 "Is preference wrong?"
Vashishtha:
 "No."
"Rain is not wrong."
"Wind is not wrong."
"Preference is simply a movement within experience."
"Suffering begins when it is unconsciously followed."
Rama:
 "So freedom lies in seeing the preference before the leaning?"
Vashishtha:
"Yes."
"When the seed is seen, the forest cannot surprise you."
Experiential Insight
Close your eyes.
Allow sounds, sensations, thoughts, and feelings to arise naturally.
Notice:
A sound appears.
Before thought comments on it...
Before attention moves toward or away from it...
Can you detect a faint flavor?
Perhaps:
pleasant
unpleasant
neutral
Do not analyze.
Simply notice.
This tiny coloring often appears before leaning.
Preference whispers.
Leaning follows.
Identification comes later.
The earlier it is seen, the greater the freedom.
Ordinary Life Examples
While Working
An email arrives.
Before opening it, there is a subtle hope or concern.
That flavor appears before attention rushes toward the message.
During Conversation
Someone praises you.
Before the mind creates a story, there is a pleasant taste.
Attention begins to gather around it.
On the Golf Course
A shot leaves the clubface.
Before judgment appears, there is a subtle feeling:
good or bad.
Notice the flavor before the commentary.
During Meditation
A thought arises.
Before following it, there may be curiosity.
Or resistance.
The preference arrives first.
The leaning comes next.
Today's Sadhana
Throughout the day, practice:
Catch the Flavor
When experience appears, ask:
What is the first taste here?
Pleasant?
Unpleasant?
Neutral?
Do not change it.
Do not suppress it.
Simply see it.
Then notice whether attention begins to lean.
Remain with the seeing.
Let preference be known without becoming a command.
Final Teaching
Preference is the first coloring of experience.
Leaning is attention following the color.
See the coloring early,
and attention remains free.
