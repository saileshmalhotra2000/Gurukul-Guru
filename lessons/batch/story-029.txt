That morning, Vashishtha did not call them to sit.
Instead, he handed each of them a pot… filled to the brim with water.
“Walk with this,” he said, pointing toward a narrow forest trail.
“But be careful…”
“Not a single drop should fall.”
Lakshmana straightened immediately, gripping the pot firmly.
Rama lifted his with calm steadiness.
They began walking.
The path was uneven.
Roots crossed their way.
Small stones shifted beneath their feet.
Lakshmana’s entire attention went to the pot.
Every step was cautious.
Every movement controlled.
Rama too remained attentive… his gaze soft, but unwavering.
A bird suddenly flew across their path.
Lakshmana almost looked up…
…but stopped himself.
“The water,” he reminded himself.
They continued.
After some time, they reached the end of the path.
Not a drop had spilled.
Lakshmana exhaled deeply.
Rama gently lowered the pot.
Vashishtha, who had been watching from a distance, approached them.
“Tell me,” he said,
“what did you notice on the way?”
Lakshmana replied,
“Nothing, Gurudev.”
“I saw only the pot.”
Rama nodded,
“The forest was there… but attention remained here.”
Vashishtha smiled slightly.
“Good.”
He then said:
“Now walk again…”
“But this time…”
“…do not hold the pot.”
They looked at him, surprised.
But obeyed.
They walked the same path again.
This time, their eyes wandered.
They noticed:
the trees
the movement of leaves
the sound of birds
the feeling of the ground
The world felt wide again.
When they returned, Vashishtha asked:
“What changed?”
Lakshmana said,
“The mind spread outward.”
Rama added,
“Without the pot… attention had no anchor.”
Vashishtha took the pot and placed it between them.
“This is your practice.”
“When you hold it…”
“…the mind gathers.”
“When you drop it…”
“…the mind scatters.”
Lakshmana reflected,
“So one must always hold something?”
Vashishtha shook his head gently.
“No.”
“At first, you need support.”
“But eventually…”
“…the steadiness must remain…”
“…even without the pot.”
Rama understood.
“The pot is not the goal…”
“…it is the training.”
Vashishtha nodded.
He concluded:
“In the beginning, focus is effort.”
“Later, it becomes natural.”
“And finally…”
“…whether you hold the pot or not…”
“…the mind does not spill.”
The brothers looked once more at the simple clay pot.
It seemed ordinary.
But now they knew—
It was teaching them how to carry the mind itself.
