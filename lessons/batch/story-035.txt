The forest was awake with movement.
Not chaotic… but continuous.
Leaves trembled, insects hummed, light shifted through branches without pause.
Vashishtha stood still and said:
“Today… you will not practice in stillness.”
He began walking—not steadily, but unpredictably.
Sometimes fast, sometimes slow… sometimes stopping suddenly.
Rama and Lakshmana followed.
Without warning, Vashishtha spoke:
“Rama… are you aware?”
“Yes, Gurudev.”
“Is that awareness continuous… or appearing in moments?”
Rama observed carefully.
As the pace changed, as attention adjusted…
He saw something subtle.
“It seems continuous… but my noticing of it comes and goes.”
Vashishtha stopped.
“Good. This must be seen clearly.”
He drew a line on the ground.
“Awareness is like this—unbroken.”
Then he tapped at different points along it.
“These are moments when mind notices it.”
He looked at Lakshmana.
“You believe awareness is coming and going…”
“…but it is only recognition that comes and goes.”
They resumed walking.
Suddenly, Vashishtha changed direction sharply.
Lakshmana stumbled slightly trying to adjust.
A flicker of irritation arose.
Vashishtha said immediately:
“Do not fix the irritation. See it.”
Lakshmana watched.
The irritation rose like a wave…
Stayed briefly…
And began to dissolve.
“I did not remove it, Gurudev… yet it passed.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Because you did not become it.”
They continued.
After some time, Vashishtha spoke again:
“Now a deeper test.”
He began giving instructions rapidly:
“Walk faster.”
“Stop.”
“Turn.”
“Pick that branch.”
“Drop it.”
The body responded quickly.
Mind tried to keep up.
Attention moved rapidly between commands.
After a few minutes, he stopped.
“Rama… what did you observe?”
Rama replied:
“Action was happening quickly… mind was processing… but something remained untouched.”
“And did that untouched presence need time to adjust?”
“No, Gurudev.”
Vashishtha’s eyes brightened.
“That… is stability.”
He turned to both of them:
“Stability of awareness is not slow… not rigid… not controlled.”
A pause.
“It is that which does not move… even when everything moves.”
A breeze passed strongly now, bending branches.
Vashishtha pointed to a tree.
“See this tree.”
“The leaves move wildly.”
“The branches sway.”
“But the trunk remains steady.”
He looked at them deeply.
“Be like the trunk… not by holding… but by being rooted in what does not move.”
Silence deepened.
Lakshmana then asked softly:
“Gurudev… sometimes I feel I am stable… and then suddenly I am lost again.”
Vashishtha replied:
“You are not lost.”
“You only begin to follow what appears.”
He continued:
“A thought comes—you follow.”
“An emotion comes—you follow.”
“An action comes—you enter.”
Then firmly:
“Today, see this clearly—”
“Nothing pulls you…
 you move toward it.”
Lakshmana stood still.
This struck deeply.
They resumed walking one final time.
Now…
Thoughts came → not followed
Sensations arose → not resisted
Actions happened → not claimed
Rama spoke after a long silence:
“Gurudev… everything is happening… but nothing is sticking.”
Vashishtha smiled.
“When nothing sticks…
 the witness is no longer practice…
 it is your natural state.”
The forest continued its endless movement.
But within…
There was no movement to manage anymore.
