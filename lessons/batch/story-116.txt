14 June 2026 — Lesson: The Contract Writer
The morning mist still floated between the trees.
Birdsong echoed softly through the gurukul.
Rama and Lakshmana sat beneath an ancient banyan tree while Vashishtha quietly watched the forest awaken.
After a long silence, Vashishtha picked up a palm leaf manuscript.
He held it up.
Then he slowly tore it into pieces.
The fragments drifted to the ground.
Rama looked surprised.
"Master, why did you destroy it?"
Vashishtha smiled.
"I did not destroy it."
"I merely destroyed the writing."
Then he pointed to the blank side of another palm leaf.
"What do you see?"
"A blank leaf."
"Good."
Then Vashishtha began writing upon it.
First:
People should behave properly.
Then:
Life should be fair.
Then:
Others should understand me.
Then:
Conflict should not happen.
Then:
Things should go according to plan.
"What do you see now?"
"Words, Master."
Vashishtha nodded.
"Yesterday we studied the contracts."
"Today we study the writer."
Rama became attentive.
"You mean the mind?"
"No."
"The mind carries the contracts."
"But something writes them."
The forest became very quiet.
Even Lakshmana leaned forward.
Vashishtha pointed toward the river.
"The river carries leaves."
"But the river does not create the leaves."
"The mind carries contracts."
"But the mind did not create them."
Then he asked:
"When did you decide that people should understand you?"
Rama was silent.
"I do not know."
"When did you decide conflict should not happen?"
"I do not know."
"When did you decide life should be fair?"
"I do not know."
Vashishtha smiled.
"Exactly."
The Great Discovery
Most contracts were never consciously chosen.
They were assembled gradually.
From:
repeated experiences
repeated attention
repeated emotional charge
repeated conclusions
Eventually a sentence appeared.
And the sentence became truth.
The Demonstration
Vashishtha drew a simple sequence in the sand.
Experience
↓
Repeated Visits
↓
Tendency
↓
Conditioning
↓
Contract
Then he said:
"The contract is the visible part.
The conditioning is hidden beneath it."
Ordinary Life Examples
A child repeatedly criticized.
Over time:
I should not make mistakes.
Later:
People should not criticize me.
A child repeatedly praised for achievement.
Over time:
Success is important.
Later:
I should succeed.
Things should progress.
A child repeatedly exposed to conflict.
Over time:
Conflict is dangerous.
Later:
Harmony should exist.
The contract is the surface.
The conditioning is the root.
The Experiential Insight
Today, when a "should" appears, do not stop there.
Ask:
Where did this contract come from?
Not intellectually.
Feel into it.
Notice whether the contract carries:
fear
hope
protection
desire
memory
The charge often reveals the origin.
Today's Sadhana
Several times today, when a "should" appears, ask:
Who taught me this?
Do not answer quickly.
Simply remain with the question.
Observe what arises.
A memory.
A feeling.
A parent.
A teacher.
A past experience.
Or perhaps nothing at all.
Just observe.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha gathered the torn manuscript pieces and released them into the wind.
"Most suffering comes not from reality.
Nor from the contract.
But from forgetting that the contract was written.
What is written can be questioned.
What is questioned can be seen.
What is clearly seen begins to lose its power."
14 June 2026 — Lesson: The Contract Writer
Stage 4: Discovering not only the contract, but the conditioning that silently wrote it.
When the body expresses excitement or anticipation, which conditioning is generally touched
Vashishtha would say:
"Excitement and anticipation are very revealing, Rama.
Most seekers investigate irritation and fear.
Few investigate excitement.
Yet excitement often reveals conditioning just as clearly."
When anticipation appears, it usually means:
Something important is expected to happen.
And wherever importance is assigned, a tendency is often active.
Achievement
If the excitement is:
"I may succeed."
"I may reach my goal."
"This may work."
Then the tendency may be:
Achievement
The hidden contract:
"Success should happen."
Approval
If the excitement is:
"People may appreciate me."
"They may like what I did."
Then the tendency may be:
Approval
The hidden contract:
"I should be valued."
Security
If the excitement is:
"This will solve my problem."
"Things will finally be okay."
Then the tendency may be:
Security
The hidden contract:
"I need this outcome to feel safe."
Possession / Acquisition
If the excitement is:
"I am about to get something."
Then the tendency may be:
Possession
or
More
The hidden contract:
"Having this will improve me."
Self-Image
If the excitement is:
"This proves something about me."
Then the tendency may be:
Self-Image
The hidden contract:
"I should become a certain kind of person."
The Deeper Principle
Notice the symmetry.
When reality moves away from what conditioning wants:
→ fear
 → irritation
 → disappointment
When reality moves toward what conditioning wants:
→ excitement
 → anticipation
 → hope
In both cases,
the same conditioning is active.
A Simple Inquiry
The next time excitement appears, ask:
"What am I hoping to obtain?"
Then ask:
"What would this give me that I believe I do not already have?"
The answer often reveals the conditioning immediately.
Vashishtha smiled.
"Fear reveals what conditioning wishes to avoid.
Excitement reveals what conditioning wishes to obtain.
Therefore study both equally.
Both point to the same field."
What conditioning is touched when there is anticipation of getting undisturbed time to continue my learning further without disturbance
Vashishtha would become very interested in this question.
Because the anticipation itself is not the issue.
The question is:
What do you believe uninterrupted learning will give you?
Different answers reveal different conditionings.
If the feeling is:
"At last, I can go deeper."
"I can continue discovering."
Then the tendency may be:
Understanding or Truth-Seeking
This is one of the healthiest tendencies and is often present in sincere seekers.
The hidden movement is:
"I value clarity."
If the feeling is:
"I don't want interruptions."
"Nothing should disturb this."
Then the tendency may be:
Control
The hidden contract:
"My environment should cooperate with my learning."
If the feeling is:
"I feel peaceful when I can study quietly."
Then the tendency may be:
Harmony
The hidden contract:
"Inner and outer peace should be present."
If the feeling is:
"I am making progress."
"I am advancing."
Then the tendency may be:
Achievement
The hidden contract:
"I should keep progressing."
If the feeling is:
"I want freedom from demands and disturbances."
Then the tendency may be:
Security or Avoidance
The hidden movement is:
"I want a protected space."
In Your Case
Given the direction of our recent inquiry, I would not immediately assume Achievement.
What I would investigate first is:
Does the anticipation arise from love of understanding?
or
Does it arise from wanting conditions to be a certain way?
These feel very different in direct experience.
Love of Understanding
There is eagerness.
Curiosity.
Aliveness.
But little tension.
If an interruption comes, there may be disappointment, but not much irritation.
Conditioning
There is eagerness plus subtle contraction.
A feeling that:
"This time must not be disturbed."
If interruption occurs, irritation appears quickly.
That reveals charge.
Vashishtha smiled and said:
"The desire to understand is not the problem.
The question is whether understanding is being loved...
or whether a condition is being demanded.
One is freedom moving toward truth.
The other is conditioning negotiating with reality."
So the next time anticipation arises, simply ask:
"What am I hoping this uninterrupted time will give me?"
And then:
"If I were interrupted, what exactly would be lost?"
The answer will point directly to the conditioning—or reveal that it is simply the natural joy of learning.
