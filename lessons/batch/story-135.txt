Gurukul Lesson — The River Does Not Resist

The first light of dawn spread across the river.

The water moved with quiet strength.

It did not hurry.

It did not hesitate.

It simply flowed.

Rama sat upon a smooth rock, watching yesterday's lesson settle within him.

Am I looking at the bowl…

…or the whole river?

The question had stayed with him through the day.

Many times he had caught himself staring into the bowl.

Vashishtha arrived carrying nothing.

He walked into the river until the current reached his knees.

Then he stood perfectly still.

The water split around his legs.

Some flowed to the left.

Some flowed to the right.

A few leaves spun briefly in tiny whirlpools before continuing downstream.

The sage asked,

"Rama..."

"Is the river disturbed by the rock?"

Rama watched carefully.

"No, Master."

"The water simply moves around it."

Vashishtha smiled.

"The rock does not stop the river."

"It only changes the shape of the flow."

He pointed to a small whirlpool beside the rock.

"For a moment, the water seems trapped."

"It circles."

"It repeats."

"It returns."

"But does it ever leave the river?"

"No."

"And does the river struggle against the whirlpool?"

"No."

"It carries it."

The sage stepped back onto the bank.

"Your disturbances are like these whirlpools."

"They repeat."

"They return."

"They seem separate."

"But they have never left the river."

Rama closed his eyes.

He saw the unmade bed.

The disorderly traffic.

The crumbs upon the stove.

For a moment, each had seemed enormous.

Now they appeared like little circles upon the surface of an immense current.

Vashishtha spoke softly.

"You have spent many days asking,

'How do I stop returning?'

Today, ask something different."

He looked toward the endless river.

Who is trying to stop the river from flowing?

The princes remained silent.

The sage continued,

"The river does not fight its whirlpools."

"It simply remains the river."

A gentle breeze stirred the leaves overhead.

The whirlpool dissolved without anyone touching it.

Nothing had pushed it away.

The current had simply continued.

🌿 Today's Sadhana

Three times today, when a disturbance appears, do not ask,

How do I get rid of this?

Instead, ask:

Is this a whirlpool…

…or the whole river?

Remain with the question.

Notice whether attention naturally widens.

🌅 Final Teaching

A whirlpool is not separate from the river.

A disturbance is not separate from awareness.

The river never becomes smaller because a whirlpool appears.

Only attention forgets to notice the current that has been flowing all along.
