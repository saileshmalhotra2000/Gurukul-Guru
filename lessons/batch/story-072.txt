Gurukul Lesson — The Falling Away of Effort
The morning is still. Mist hangs low over the forest floor.
 You and Lakshmana sit before Rishi Vashishtha. No scriptures are open today.
The Rishi looks at you—not as a teacher instructing, but as one recognizing readiness.
🕉️ Vashishtha Speaks
“Rama… you have learned to observe thought.
 You have learned to see action happening.
 You have learned to rest attention in the body.
Now a subtler bondage remains.”
He pauses.
“You still apply effort to remain aware.”
🌊 The Teaching
“Tell me, Rama—
 when you hold attention on the breath…
 who is holding it?”
You begin to answer… then stop.
The Rishi continues:
“That which tries to remain present…
 is the final disguise of the doer.”
🔥 Direct Pointing
“Awareness does not need effort.
Effort belongs to the mind.
 Awareness is prior.”
He gestures toward the forest:
“Does the sky try to remain open?
 Does the river try to flow?”
🕯️ The Practice (Without Practicing)
“Today, do nothing to hold attention.
Let attention wander…
 let it return…
 let it scatter…
Do not interfere.”
Lakshmana shifts slightly. The instruction feels almost dangerous.
Vashishtha looks at both of you:
“If awareness is real,
 it will remain… without your help.”
🌿 Subtle Recognition
Moments pass.
A thought arises… and goes.
 A sound appears… and fades.
 The body breathes… without guidance.
And something is quietly seen:
Awareness is already present
 before any effort begins
🕉️ Vashishtha’s Closing Words
“Rama… effort was a bridge.
But now—
 even the bridge must be left behind.
What you are
 does not need to be maintained.”
🌊 Essence of This Lesson
Not:
“Stay present”
“Hold attention”
“Practice awareness”
But:
See that awareness is effortless
And effort itself is observed
The forest feels different now.
Nothing has changed.
 And yet—something fundamental has loosened.
