The Gap Before Movement
Morning mist covered the gurukul forest.
 The river moved silently beside the ancient trees.
 Rama sat before Rishi Vashishtha, attentive and still, while Lakshmana watched closely beside him.
A squirrel leapt suddenly from the bushes.
Lakshmana’s head turned immediately.
But Rama noticed something subtle.
Before the body moved…
 before the mind named the sound…
 there was a tiny instant.
A gap.
Vashishtha smiled softly.
“Rama, bondage is not in movement.
 Bondage is in unconscious movement.”
He picked up a dry leaf and dropped it into the river.
“See carefully.
 First there is perception.
 Then a stirring.
 Then attention rushes outward.
 Then the mind says:
 ‘I must react.’”
The sage looked directly at Rama.
“Most people never see the stirring.
 They only know themselves after they are already moving.”
A strong wind passed through the forest.
 Branches swayed violently overhead.
But the lake nearby reflected the sky without resistance.
Vashishtha continued:
“Freedom begins the moment you notice
 the impulse before you obey it.”
He asked Rama to stand.
“Walk to the river.”
Rama stood immediately.
Vashishtha raised his hand.
“Again. But this time, do not move quickly.
 Watch the very first impulse to move.”
Rama closed his eyes.
And for the first time, he saw it:
A subtle energetic leaning forward inside the mind…
 before the feet moved.
A current.
Not yet action.
 Not yet thought.
 Just movement beginning.
Rama opened his eyes slowly.
“Gurudev… the movement appears by itself.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Yes.
 And awareness sees it.”
The forest became completely silent.
Then came the lesson:
“You believe you are choosing every movement.
 But most movements are ancient conditioning flowing automatically.
Watch carefully enough, and action becomes conscious.
Conscious action becomes light.
Light action leaves no residue.”
Today’s Practice from the Gurukul
For one simple activity today —
 walking, speaking, reaching for your phone, drinking water, touching the tabla —
pause for half a second before the movement.
Not to stop the action.
Only to see:
the impulse arise
the body preparing
attention leaning outward
Do not interfere.
Just witness the birth of movement.
That tiny gap is the doorway Vashishtha points toward.
“The unaware mind lives in reaction.
 The aware one discovers the stillness before action.”
