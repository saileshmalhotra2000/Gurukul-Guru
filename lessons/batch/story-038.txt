Stabilizing Awareness in Activity
“Rama,” said Vashishtha, “it is easy to feel awareness when the world is quiet. The true mastery is to remain aware while acting.”
He explained:
Let the body act, the senses engage, the mind respond
But keep a subtle thread of awareness unbroken
Just as a musician plays while remaining aware of rhythm, remain aware of being aware
Do not withdraw from action—refine your presence within action.
Non-Identification with Thoughts and Emotions
Vashishtha continued:
“Thoughts arise like ripples in water. Emotions surge like passing storms. You are neither.”
When a thought appears, do not follow it—observe it
When an emotion arises, do not suppress it—witness it
Say inwardly: ‘This too is seen’
By this, the chain breaks:
No identification → No disturbance → Natural stillness
Acting Without Inner Disturbance
“Lakshmana,” he said, “action does not disturb you—attachment and identification do.”
Act with full clarity, but:
Without clinging to outcomes
Without internal commentary
Without ‘I am doing’
Let action flow like a river—effortless, continuous, untroubled.
The Living Practice
Vashishtha summarized the progression:
Attention gathers the scattered mind
Mind becomes steady and clear
Awareness becomes continuous
Witness remains untouched
Now the next step:
Carry the Witness into every action
Walking, speaking, deciding, responding—remain as the silent knower behind it all.
Closing Insight
“Rama,” Vashishtha said gently,
 “The world need not be renounced. Only the false identification must fall.”
When awareness remains steady:
Thoughts lose their grip
Emotions lose their force
Actions become pure
Then life itself becomes meditation.
