n the quiet grove, as the first light settles upon the earth, Rishi Vashishtha continues guiding Rama and Lakshmana deeper into the subtle art of living in awareness.
Stabilizing Awareness in the Midst of Action
Vashishtha speaks:
“Rama, it is easy to feel stillness when seated in silence. The true mastery is to remain undisturbed while moving through the world.”
He explains that awareness must no longer depend on stillness of the body or absence of activity. Just as the sky remains unchanged whether winds blow or clouds gather, awareness remains untouched by action.
Let the body act
Let the mind think
Let the senses engage
But you remain as the knower of all movements
From Witness to Steady Abidance
Previously, Rama learned to observe thoughts as the Witness. Now Vashishtha refines this:
“Do not merely visit the Witness. Abide as it.”
The difference is subtle but profound:
Earlier: “I am watching thoughts”
Now: “Thoughts appear in me, but I am unchanged”
Awareness is no longer something you “do” — it becomes your natural position
Non-Identification in Real Time
Vashishtha continues:
“When emotion arises, do not resist it, and do not become it.”
He teaches a threefold recognition:
This is a movement in the mind
It is known to me
Therefore, it is not me
Whether it is irritation, desire, or fear:
Do not suppress
Do not express blindly
Simply know it as an appearance
Like ripples on water, they arise and dissolve — the water is never disturbed in its essence.
Acting Without Inner Disturbance
Rama asks, “How should I act in the world then?”
Vashishtha replies:
“Act fully, but remain empty within.”
This means:
Perform your duties with precision
Speak, decide, and respond appropriately
But do not carry psychological residue
After action is done:
No replaying
No clinging
No self-judgment
Action happens. Awareness remains untouched.
The Integration
Vashishtha concludes:
“When awareness remains steady in activity, and the mind is seen but not owned, then life flows without bondage.”
This is the shift:
From controlling the mind → to seeing the mind
From practicing awareness → to being awareness
From reacting → to responding without disturbance
Practice for Today
As you move through your day:
Notice moments of activity (walking, speaking, working)
Gently remain aware of being aware
When thoughts or emotions arise, silently note:
 “Known… not me”
Continue action without interruption
No withdrawal is needed.
 Awareness and activity now coexist.
