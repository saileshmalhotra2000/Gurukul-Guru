The dawn was quiet, but the forest was not.
Voices of birds overlapped, winds moved in different directions, and distant animal calls echoed unpredictably.
Rama and Lakshmana walked beside Vashishtha through this layered noise.
Vashishtha spoke:
“Until now, you have seen awareness in simple conditions.”
He paused.
“Today… remain aware amidst confusion.”
They entered a denser part of the forest.
Multiple sounds arose at once—sharp, soft, near, far.
Attention began to split.
Vashishtha asked:
“Rama… what is happening?”
Rama replied:
“Many things are appearing at once, Gurudev. Attention is moving rapidly.”
“And are you moving with it?”
Rama observed carefully.
“For moments… yes. Then I return.”
Vashishtha shook his head gently.
“Do not return.”
Rama looked at him.
“Returning means you believe you left.”
A silence followed.
Vashishtha continued:
“Awareness does not go anywhere.”
“It is attention that wanders.”
Lakshmana listened intently.
Vashishtha picked up a stick and drew a small circle in the dust.
“This is attention.”
Then he gestured to the vast forest around them.
“This… is awareness.”
He erased the circle.
“Do not confuse the movement of attention…”
“…with the movement of awareness.”
They resumed walking.
Sounds continued—unpredictable, overlapping.
But now, Rama did not try to gather them.
After some time, he spoke:
“Gurudev… attention is still moving… but I am not following each movement.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Good. Now see more clearly.”
A loud cry suddenly pierced the air.
Lakshmana’s attention jumped toward it.
For a moment, his body tensed.
Vashishtha said:
“Let attention jump.”
“Do not jump with it.”
Lakshmana relaxed—not by force, but by seeing.
“The senses will move.”
“The mind will interpret.”
“The body may react.”
Vashishtha’s voice became deeper:
“But you…”
“…are not required to participate.”
They walked further.
After a while, Rama said:
“Gurudev… everything is happening more freely now.”
“What do you mean?”
“I am not trying to control attention… and yet, there is less disturbance.”
Vashishtha smiled.
“Because control was the disturbance.”
They reached an open clearing.
Wind moved in wide, unrestricted patterns.
Vashishtha stood still.
“Final seeing for today.”
He looked at both of them.
“You have understood that you are not the thought.”
“You have seen that you are not the emotion.”
“Now see this…”
A long pause.
“You are not even the movement of attention.”
Rama’s breath slowed.
Vashishtha continued:
“Attention moves within awareness.”
“Mind appears within awareness.”
“Action unfolds within awareness.”
He stepped closer.
“But awareness…”
“…does not belong to any of them.”
Silence deepened.
Rama stood with eyes open.
Sounds came and went.
Attention shifted lightly.
The body breathed.
And for the first time—
Even the movement of attention was seen… as something separate.
He spoke softly:
“Gurudev… even attention is appearing and disappearing.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Then remain as that in which it appears.”
No effort.
No correction.
No holding.
Just this—
Unbroken, untouched presence.
“When even attention is not ‘you’…
 then nothing can disturb you.”
The wind passed.
The forest remained alive.
And yet…
There was no center being shaken anymore.
