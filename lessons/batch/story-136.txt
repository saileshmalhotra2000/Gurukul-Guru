The Whirlpool Does Not Choose

The forest awakened slowly.

A cool breeze moved through the banyan leaves.

The river was fuller today after rain in the distant mountains.

Its current was stronger.

Yet wherever it met a large stone, small whirlpools quietly appeared.

Vashishtha and the two princes stood watching.

No one spoke.

After several minutes the sage pointed to one whirlpool.

"Rama..."

"Did the whirlpool decide to appear?"

"No, Master."

"Did it choose this rock?"

"No."

"Did the river make a mistake?"

Rama smiled.

"No."

The sage nodded.

"The whirlpool is simply what happens..."

"...when flowing water meets a certain shape."

He picked up a stick and drew upon the wet sand.

First he drew a straight flowing line.

Then he placed a stone in its path.

Immediately he drew a spiral beside it.

Flow.

Stone.

Whirlpool.

"Notice carefully."

"The river is not trying to become a whirlpool."

"The whirlpool is not trying to disturb the river."

"It is simply the meeting of the current and the stone."

Rama watched the water with new eyes.

The sage continued.

"When someone leaves the bed unmade..."

"When traffic becomes disorderly..."

"When crumbs remain upon the stove..."

"Something inside meets that experience."

"And immediately..."

"...a whirlpool forms."

He looked gently at Rama.

"The experience is the river."

"The contract is the stone."

"The disturbance is the whirlpool."

The princes remained silent.

Vashishtha bent down and lifted the stone from the river.

Within moments, the whirlpool disappeared.

No one pushed it away.

No one argued with it.

No one meditated it away.

Without the stone...

there was nothing for the current to circle around.

The sage turned toward Rama.

"For many days you have been observing the whirlpool."

"Now begin observing the stone."

Rama looked puzzled.

"The stone, Master?"

"The contract."

"'It should be made.'"

"'People should behave properly.'"

"'Things should be orderly.'"

The sage placed the stone back into the current.

Again the whirlpool quietly returned.

"Do you see?"

"The river is innocent."

"The whirlpool is innocent."

"The stone quietly shapes the flow."

The forest fell silent.

Even the birds seemed to pause.

Then Vashishtha asked the question that would guide the coming days.

What stone is this disturbance flowing around?

He did not say another word.

The river answered by continuing to flow.

🌿 Today's Sadhana

Three times today, when a disturbance appears, ask:

What is the stone here?

Do not answer:

"The bed."

"The traffic."

"The crumbs."

Look deeper.

Find the silent contract around which attention has begun to circle.

🌅 Final Teaching

The river does not create the whirlpool alone.

The stone quietly gives it shape.

Observe the stone, and the whirlpool is understood.
