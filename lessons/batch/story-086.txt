Dawn mist floated gently above the river.
The gurukul had not yet awakened.
Only the crackling of the sacred fire could be heard.
Rama sat silently after yesterday’s practice of observing sensations in the body.
Something had become clearer to him.
He bowed to Vashistha and spoke:
“Gurudev…
 I can now sometimes notice sensations before thought begins.
But once attention gets captured,
 it feels as if I disappear into the experience.”
Vashishtha smiled softly.
He picked up a dry leaf and dropped it into the flowing river.
The leaf was immediately carried away.
Then the sage pointed toward a large stone resting in the middle of the current.
Water moved around it endlessly.
But the stone remained unmoved.
🌿 Lesson — Attention Is Pulled by Movement
“Rama…
 the mind does not cling to peace.
It clings to movement.”
The human system is naturally drawn toward:
novelty
danger
pleasure
unfinished things
emotional intensity
Anything with strong movement pulls attention outward.
This is why:
one criticism outweighs ten praises
one fear dominates many blessings
one desire can overpower wisdom
Attention flows toward disturbance
 unless trained otherwise.
🌊 The Untrained Mind
Vashishtha drew circles in the sand.
At the center he wrote:
Awareness
Around it:
thought
memory
sensation
desire
fear
Then he scattered dry leaves into the circles.
The wind moved them endlessly.
“This is the ordinary mind, Rama.
Every passing movement becomes important.”
A sensation arises.
Attention rushes toward it.
A thought appears.
Attention follows.
A memory appears.
Attention merges with it.
Soon:
 the witness is forgotten.
🔥 The Ancient Error
Lakshmana asked:
“Gurudev, why does attention get trapped so easily?”
Vashishtha replied:
“Because the mind assumes:
movement means importance.”
But this is not always true.
Many inner disturbances are merely:
old conditioning
habit
fear patterns
incomplete reactions repeating themselves
Yet attention feeds them as though they are urgent truths.
And whatever attention repeatedly feeds,
 grows stronger.
🕯️ The Practice of Returning
Vashishtha then said something Rama would never forget:
“Freedom is not that attention never wanders.
Freedom is that it can return.”
Not once.
Again and again.
Without frustration.
Without violence.
Without self-condemnation.
The training is not:
“Never lose awareness.”
The training is:
“Notice sooner.
 Return gently.”
🌿 Today’s Practice — Catch the Pull
Throughout the day, quietly observe:
What pulls attention most strongly?
anticipation?
irritation?
planning?
fantasy?
fear?
emotional replay?
Do not fight the movement.
Simply notice:
“Attention has been pulled.”
That recognition itself is already awakening.
Then gently return:
to breath
to bodily sensation
to simple presence
Again.
And again.
And again.
🌙 Evening Contemplation
Tonight reflect quietly:
What captured my attention most today?
Which movements felt irresistible?
How long before I noticed I was lost?
Did I return gently or harshly?
The sage says:
 harsh returning strengthens ego.
Gentle returning strengthens awareness.
🕉️ Closing Words of Vashishtha
The river continued flowing around the stone.
Vashishtha looked toward Rama and spoke softly:
“Do not measure progress
 by how still the mind becomes.
Measure it
 by how lovingly awareness returns.”
what should attention feed that frees me
Vashishtha looked toward the rising sun filtering through the forest trees.
Then he asked Rama:
“If you water weeds daily,
 what grows?”
Rama replied:
“The weeds, Gurudev.”
The sage nodded gently.
“And if you water fruit-bearing trees?”
Rama smiled softly.
“Then nourishment and shade grow.”
Vashishtha said:
“Attention is water, Rama.
Wherever it repeatedly flows,
 inner life grows in that direction.”
🌿 What Should Attention Feed?
Not every movement deserves nourishment.
The seeker must learn discernment.
🕯️ Feed What Brings You Back to Presence
🌬️ The breath
Not as escape.
As returning.
The breath anchors attention in the living present.
🌊 Bodily sensation
Especially subtle sensation.
Not the story.
The raw feeling itself.
This weakens compulsive mental narration.
🔥 Clarity instead of drama
The ordinary mind feeds:
outrage
comparison
fantasy
fear
replaying conversations
The seeker feeds:
observation
steadiness
understanding
direct experience
🌿 Silence
Not merely absence of sound.
But moments without compulsive stimulation.
The mind becomes strong through intervals of stillness.
🕉️ That which expands awareness
Feed:
contemplation
sincere inquiry
gratitude
truthfulness
disciplined practice
sacred study
noble company
beauty
seva
deep listening
These refine the inner instrument.
🌙 But the Deepest Answer Is More Subtle
Lakshmana asked:
“Gurudev, should attention avoid all difficulty?”
Vashishtha shook his head.
“No, Lakshmana.
Freedom is not created
 by feeding only pleasant experience.”
Even pain can liberate
 when illuminated consciously.
So the real teaching is this:
Feed awareness itself.
Not merely objects of awareness.
🌿 What Does This Mean?
Instead of feeding:
the story
the identity
the emotional momentum
Feed:
noticing
presence
returning
witnessing
Example:
Anger appears.
Ordinary attention feeds:
“Why did this happen?
 I am right.
 They are wrong.”
Awakened attention feeds:
“Anger is arising.
 Heat is present.
 Tightness is here.
 Awareness sees it.”
One strengthens bondage.
The other strengthens freedom.
🔥 The Final Turning
At first,
 attention feeds better objects.
Later,
 attention feeds awareness itself.
Eventually,
 even the one “doing the practice”
 begins dissolving.
Only luminous presence remains.
🕉️ Vashishtha’s Closing Words
“Rama…
 feed whatever increases clarity,
 steadiness,
 compassion,
 and remembrance of the Self.
Starve whatever strengthens unconsciousness.”
