Lesson 27: The Lamp Behind Thought
In the cool silence before sunrise, the princes sat beside the river. Mist floated over the water like wandering thoughts. Birds had not yet begun their song.
The great sage spoke:
“Rama, Lakshmana — today you shall learn the difference between mind and awareness.”
Lakshmana asked:
“Gurudev, are they not the same?”
Vashishtha smiled gently.
He lifted a small oil lamp and placed it between them.
“Observe carefully.
The flame shines upon the walls, the trees, the river, and your faces.
 Yet the flame itself does not become the tree, the river, or the face.
In the same way, awareness illuminates thoughts, emotions, memories, and desires —
 but it is not any of them.”
The sage then instructed them:
The Practice of Seeing the Source
“Close your eyes.
Do not fight thoughts.
 Do not follow thoughts.
 Instead, ask:
What is it that knows this thought?”
At first the mind wandered.
Memories appeared.
 Plans appeared.
 Old conversations appeared.
But Vashishtha continued:
“Good.
 Now see not the thought —
 see the light by which the thought is seen.”
Rama gradually noticed something subtle.
Thoughts moved like clouds,
 but something unmoving remained behind them.
Silent.
 Steady.
 Luminous.
Then the sage said:
“That silent knower is closer to you than breath itself.
Thoughts are waves.
 Awareness is the moonlight upon them.”
The Forest Exercise
Vashishtha sent the princes into the forest separately.
Their instruction:
Walk slowly.
Hear every sound.
Observe every reaction.
Each time a thought arises, do not enter it.
Simply notice:
 ‘Illuminated.’
Rama heard rustling leaves.
Fear briefly arose.
Instead of becoming fear, he silently observed:
“Fear is illuminated.”
Lakshmana heard pride arise after successfully climbing a steep rock.
He observed:
“Pride is illuminated.”
By sunset, both returned quieter than before.
Not because thoughts had disappeared —
 but because they had discovered the space behind thought.
The Teaching
That evening, Vashishtha drew three circles upon the earth.
First Circle — Objects
Things seen.
 Sounds heard.
 Sensations felt.
Second Circle — Thoughts
Interpretations.
 Memories.
 Judgments.
 Imaginations.
Third Circle — Awareness
The silent light knowing both.
Then he erased the first two circles.
The third remained.
“Rama,
 the world changes.
The mind changes.
But awareness itself remains untouched.
One who knows this becomes impossible to disturb deeply.”
Practice for Today
Three times today, pause for one minute.
During that minute:
Observe the current thought.
Do not suppress it.
Ask silently:


 “What is aware of this?”
Rest as the observer, not the thought.
Do this especially during:
irritation,
excitement,
distraction,
anticipation.
Sutra of the Day
“Thoughts borrow their light from awareness.
 Awareness borrows from nothing.”
