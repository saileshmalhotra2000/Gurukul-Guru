The morning air was cool, and a gentle fog rested over the forest paths of the gurukul.
Rama and Lakshmana arrived at the practice ground after completing their early chores. Dew clung to the grass, and the rising sun painted the sky with soft gold.
Vashishtha was waiting near a quiet pond.
The water was perfectly still, reflecting the trees and the pale sky above.
The princes bowed.
The sage gestured toward the pond.
“Come closer.”
They stood beside him and looked down at the clear reflection of the forest.
Vashishtha picked up a small pebble and dropped it into the water.
Ripples spread across the surface, and the reflection of the trees began to shake and distort.
“Look carefully,” Vashishtha said.
Rama and Lakshmana watched the moving water.
“What has disturbed the reflection?” the sage asked.
“The ripples in the water,” Lakshmana replied.
Vashishtha nodded.
“The trees above have not moved. Yet their reflection appears broken.”
He then looked at the princes.
“The mind is like this pond.”
“When the mind is disturbed by thoughts, emotions, and reactions, the world appears confused and distorted.”
The ripples slowly began to fade.
The water gradually became still again.
As the surface settled, the reflection of the trees returned clearly.
Vashishtha continued,
“When the mind becomes calm, reality is seen more clearly.”
Lakshmana asked thoughtfully,
“Gurudev, how do we calm the ripples?”
The sage smiled gently.
“You have already begun learning.”
He counted softly on his fingers.
“First, by steady attention.”
“Second, by wide awareness.”
“And third…”
He pointed again to the pond.
“…by not throwing unnecessary stones.”
Lakshmana understood immediately.
“Unnecessary reactions.”
“Yes,” Vashishtha said.
“Many disturbances of the mind are created not by the world, but by our own reactions to it.”
Just then a breeze moved across the pond, creating small natural ripples.
Vashishtha noticed and said,
“Some ripples will always appear.”
“The wind will blow. Life will move.”
“But if the water is deep and undisturbed within, the surface will settle quickly.”
He then gave them a small exercise.
“Sit by the pond.”
“Watch the water as you watch your mind.”
“If a ripple appears, simply notice it.”
“Do not try to push it away.”
“Do not add another stone.”
Rama and Lakshmana sat quietly beside the pond.
For several minutes they watched the gentle movements of the water.
A thought arose in Lakshmana’s mind.
But instead of following it, he simply noticed it—like watching a ripple pass across the surface.
The thought faded on its own.
When they finally stood, the pond had become still again.
Vashishtha looked pleased.
“Good.”
As they walked back toward the gurukul, the sage said,
“Tomorrow we will observe something even deeper…”
“…the difference between the mind that reflects and the awareness that sees the reflection.”
The forest had fully awakened now, and the day’s learning had only just begun.
