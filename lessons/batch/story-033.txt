The forest path narrowed.
Thorns brushed against their legs as they walked. The sun had not yet risen fully, and the air carried a quiet intensity.
Vashishtha walked ahead, then suddenly stopped.
Without turning, he said:
“Today… do not slow down your actions.”
Rama and Lakshmana listened carefully.
“Let the body move as it wishes. Let the pace be natural.”
He turned now.
“But remain unmoving within.”
They began walking again—this time faster.
Feet struck the ground with more force. Branches were pushed aside. Breath grew stronger.
The body was no longer gentle.
After some time, Vashishtha spoke while still walking:
“Rama… what is happening?”
“The body is moving quickly, Gurudev. The senses are active.”
“And you?”
Rama answered after observing carefully:
“There is movement… but I am not moving with it.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Good. Now go deeper.”
A thorn suddenly pierced Lakshmana’s foot.
His body reacted instantly—he pulled back sharply.
A flash of irritation arose.
Vashishtha’s voice came immediately:
“Lakshmana… do not correct it. Look.”
Lakshmana froze—not suppressing, not expressing.
Just seeing.
The pain pulsed.
The irritation flickered.
For a moment, it tried to pull him in.
But he remained.
After a few breaths, Lakshmana spoke:
“The pain is there… the reaction came… but something did not get disturbed.”
Vashishtha stepped closer.
“This is the fire where your understanding is tested.”
He pointed to Lakshmana’s foot.
“The body feels pain.”
He pointed to his chest.
“The mind creates reaction.”
Then he looked into his eyes.
“But you…”
A pause.
“…are neither.”
They resumed walking.
After some time, Vashishtha picked up a dry leaf and crushed it in his hand.
“Listen carefully.”
“When pleasant experience comes… you do not question disturbance.”
“When unpleasant experience comes… you immediately try to escape disturbance.”
He opened his hand. The crushed leaf fell.
“This is attachment to comfort.”
Rama listened deeply.
Vashishtha continued:
“If your awareness is true… it must remain the same in both.”
A cool breeze passed.
Then, moments later, the heat of the sun intensified.
Vashishtha asked:
“Rama… the breeze came. The heat followed. What changed in you?”
Rama observed silently.
Then replied:
“Sensation changed… but that which knows it did not.”
Vashishtha smiled faintly.
“Then remain there… not only in ease… but in discomfort.”
They reached an uneven rocky path.
Walking became unstable. The body had to constantly adjust.
Vashishtha spoke:
“Now observe this carefully.”
“Balance is not created by stopping movement…”
“…but by allowing constant adjustment without losing center.”
Rama walked over the stones.
The body shifted, corrected, adapted.
And yet…
There was no inner tightening.
After a while, he said:
“Gurudev… the body is adjusting continuously… but something remains steady.”
Vashishtha’s voice became soft, almost like the wind:
“That steadiness is not created by you.”
“Do not try to hold it.”
“Do not try to deepen it.”
“Do not even try to understand it.”
A long silence followed.
Only footsteps… breath… wind.
Then Vashishtha spoke one final time:
“When awareness is stable during movement…
 when thoughts arise without ownership…
 when emotions pass without leaving a trace…
then action becomes pure…
 and the doer disappears.”
Rama walked on.
No effort to remain aware.
No effort to let go.
And yet…
Nothing was lost.
