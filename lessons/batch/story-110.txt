9 June 2026 — The Gathering Begins
The ancient forest was quiet after dawn.
Mist floated above the river.
A few leaves drifted slowly on the current.
Rama and Lakshmana sat beside Rishi Vashishtha beneath a great banyan tree.
For a long time, no one spoke.
Then Vashishtha pointed toward the river.
A single leaf had touched an eddy.
Immediately the water began circling around it.
Not violently.
Gently.
Almost invisibly.
Vashishtha said,
"Yesterday you learned to notice the first spark.
Today you will learn what follows."
Rama watched the leaf.
The circling became slightly stronger.
The leaf no longer drifted freely.
It had begun to gather movement around itself.
Vashishtha continued,
"The first spark is simply:
something became important.
The next movement is:
attention begins gathering around it."
Rama asked
"Master, is this identification?"
Vashishtha shook his head.
"No.
Identification comes later.
This is earlier.
Much earlier."
He pointed again toward the leaf.
"The river has not yet become trapped.
But its movement is already changing."
Lakshmana asked
"How does this appear within us?"
Vashishtha replied,
"A sound appears.
Attention gathers.
A thought appears.
Attention gathers.
A memory appears.
Attention gathers.
A desire appears.
Attention gathers.
The gathering itself is the beginning of the chain."
Ordinary Examples
A golfer hears praise for a good shot.
For a moment there is only sound.
Then attention gathers around it.
Soon:
"I played well."
Later:
"I hope I can do that again."
Then:
"What if I fail?"
The story has begun.
A person opens a message.
One sentence stands out.
Attention gathers around a single phrase.
Minutes later the mind is replaying conversations that never happened.
The gathering came first.
A parent hears a child's comment.
Attention gathers.
Emotion gathers.
Reaction gathers.
Only later does the story appear.
Vashishtha said,
"Most people notice the story.
Few notice the gathering.
The yogi notices the gathering."
Experiential Insight
Close the eyes.
Feel the breath.
Wait.
Something will become important.
A sound.
A sensation.
A thought.
Do not follow it.
Instead ask:
Can I feel attention gathering?
Not the object.
The gathering.
Its texture is often:
subtle
magnetic
leaning
narrowing
A quiet movement toward something.
This is Stage 4 beginning to reveal itself.
Today's Sadhana
Several times today, pause and observe:
What just became important?
Is attention gathering around it?
What is the texture of the gathering?
Can the gathering be seen before the story forms?
No need to stop it.
Simply notice it.
Again and again.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha spoke softly:
"The first spark says:
'This matters.'
The gathering says:
'Come closer.'
See the invitation early,
and freedom remains."
