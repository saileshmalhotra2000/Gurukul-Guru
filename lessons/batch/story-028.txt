The forest was unusually quiet that morning.
Even the birds seemed to sing less.
Vashishtha sat with a small clay lamp placed before him.
Its flame was steady… unmoving.
Rama and Lakshmana sat on either side, watching.
After a while, Vashishtha asked:
“What do you see?”
Lakshmana replied,
“A flame, Gurudev.”
Rama added,
“It is steady… despite the breeze.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Yes.”
He then gently waved his hand near the lamp.
The flame flickered… bent… almost went out… then steadied again.
“Watch closely,” he said.
He waved again, this time a little stronger.
The flame trembled more intensely.
But still… it did not die.
Lakshmana leaned forward slightly.
“It moves… but remains.”
Vashishtha looked at him.
“Just like the trained mind.”
Rama observed deeply.
“The flame does not resist the wind…”
“…it bends with it.”
Vashishtha’s eyes brightened.
“You are seeing well.”
He placed his hand still again.
The air calmed.
The flame returned to a straight, unwavering form.
Vashishtha spoke:
“If the flame were rigid…”
“…it would break.”
“If it were too weak…”
“…it would vanish.”
“But because it is balanced…”
“…it survives both stillness and disturbance.”
Lakshmana reflected,
“So the mind must not be stiff…”
“…nor scattered.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Yes.”
“Strength is not hardness.”
“Calmness is not dullness.”
He then did something unexpected.
He placed a small cover over the lamp.
The flame dimmed… starved of air… and almost disappeared.
After a few moments, he removed the cover.
The flame struggled… then slowly rose again.
Rama understood.
“If there is no movement at all…”
“…even the flame weakens.”
Vashishtha replied,
“Just so.”
“A mind that avoids all challenge…”
“…loses its vitality.”
The brothers sat silently.
The lesson had deepened beyond the visible.
Vashishtha concluded:
“Do not seek a life without wind.”
“Do not fear when the flame bends.”
“Train it so that…”
“…it neither clings to stillness…”
“…nor fears movement.”
The lamp continued to burn.
Sometimes steady…
sometimes gently swaying…
but always present.
And in that small flame…
the brothers saw the shape of true balance.
