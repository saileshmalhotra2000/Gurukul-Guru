02 June 2026 — Lesson 49: The First Taste of Ownership
The morning sun filtered through the banyan leaves. The river moved quietly beside the gurukul. Birds called from distant branches and then vanished again into silence.
Rama and Lakshmana sat before Rishi Vashishtha. For many days they had watched the subtle movements of attention. They had seen the first leaning, the first grasp, and the moment awareness seemed to forget itself.
Today Vashishtha drew a small circle in the sand.
Rama Asked
“Master, when does the center truly begin?
Is it born when awareness forgets itself?”
Vashishtha looked at the circle.
“No.
Forgetting prepares the ground.
The center appears when ownership enters.”
The Fallen Leaf
A leaf drifted from a tree.
It landed softly upon the river.
The current carried it away.
Vashishtha pointed toward it.
“The leaf does not resist the river.
Therefore it remains light.”
Then he picked up a twig and pressed it into the mud.
“This one says:
'I belong here.'
Immediately there is holding.”
He looked at Rama.
“Ownership is holding.”
The First Taste of "Mine"
Vashishtha continued:
“An experience appears.
Attention leans.
Attention gathers.
Witnessing is forgotten.
But still there is no center.
The center is born at the next movement.”
He drew an arrow toward the circle.
“The experience becomes:
my thought
my feeling
my success
my problem
The moment ‘mine’ appears,
the center gains substance.”
The Hidden Sequence
Vashishtha wrote in the sand:
Experience Appears
A sensation.
A thought.
An emotion.
Attention Leans
Interest gathers.
Energy gathers.
Witnessing Fades
Awareness becomes absorbed.
Ownership Appears
"This is happening to me."
"This belongs to me."
"I must protect it."
The Center Strengthens
The story now has an owner.
The owner feels real.
Experiential Insight
Close your eyes.
Wait for a thought.
When a thought appears, simply notice:
A thought is present.
Remain there for a moment.
Then intentionally say:
My thought.
Feel the difference.
The thought itself changes very little.
But ownership creates contraction.
A center immediately begins to form around the experience.
Notice the subtle tightening.
That tightening is the beginning of identification.
Ordinary Life Examples
Receiving Praise
First comes pleasant sensation.
Then comes:
"My achievement."
The center grows stronger.
Receiving Criticism
First comes discomfort.
Then comes:
"They criticized me."
Ownership deepens suffering.
A Possession Breaks
The object breaks.
Pain appears.
But the strongest reaction often comes from:
"My possession."
Ownership magnifies the experience.
An Opinion Is Challenged
A viewpoint is questioned.
Soon there is:
"My belief is under attack."
The center rushes forward to defend itself.
Lakshmana Asked
“Master, should ownership be destroyed?”
Vashishtha smiled.
“No.
Simply see it.
What is clearly seen loses its power to deceive.”
Today's Sadhana
Several times today, whenever a strong reaction appears, pause and ask:
“What has become mine?”
Do not analyze.
Do not judge.
Simply notice:
my image
my success
my comfort
my opinion
my expectation
Observe the ownership.
Observe the contraction that follows.
Observe how the center feeds upon it.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha spoke softly:
“Experience does not create bondage.
Ownership creates bondage.
The river flows freely until something claims a piece of it.
See the claim,
and freedom begins to return.”
Gurukul of Rishi Vashishtha
