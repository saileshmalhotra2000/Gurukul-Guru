5 June 2026 — The Hook
The dawn air was cool and fragrant.
Mist floated above the river.
Birdsong echoed softly through the forest canopy.
Rama and Lakshmana sat before Vashishtha beneath a vast banyan tree.
For many days they had learned to observe the first leaning of attention.
Now Vashishtha picked up a small iron hook used by fishermen from a nearby village.
Holding it before them, he asked:
“Rama, does a fish become trapped the moment it sees the hook?”
“No, Master.”
“When does it become trapped?”
“When it bites.”
Vashishtha smiled.
“Exactly.”
The Teaching
“The mind is much the same.”
“A thought appears.”
“A memory appears.”
“A feeling appears.”
“These are merely appearances.”
He placed the hook upon the ground.
“The problem is not the appearance.”
“The problem is the bite.”
Lakshmana asked,
“What is the bite, Master?”
Vashishtha replied:
“The bite is the moment attention says:
This is me.
This is mine.
I must follow this.
I must resist this.
That is the hook entering.”
The Space Before the Bite
Vashishtha pointed toward the river.
A leaf floated peacefully downstream.
Nearby another leaf became trapped against a branch.
“The branch is not the trap.”
“The leaf's contact with the branch is not yet the trap.”
“The trap begins when movement ceases and attachment holds.”
He looked at Rama.
“There is always a brief space before the bite.”
“Most people never notice it.”
“You are learning to notice it.”
Experiential Insight
Close your eyes.
Allow any thought to arise.
Perhaps:
a memory
a plan
a worry
a desire
Notice that the thought simply appears.
Now observe carefully.
Is there a moment when attention begins moving toward it?
A subtle interest.
A curiosity.
A reaching.
Before the story forms completely.
Before identification hardens.
There is a tiny opening.
A tiny freedom.
The thought has appeared.
The bite has not yet happened.
Remain there.
Ordinary Life Examples
Hearing Criticism
Words are heard.
Then comes a tiny movement:
"I need to defend myself."
The bite begins there.
See it early.
Checking the Phone
A notification appears.
The screen lights up.
The hook is not the notification.
The hook is the unconscious pull toward it.
Observe the pull.
Missing a Golf Shot
The ball drifts off target.
A thought appears:
"I always do this."
The thought is not the trap.
The trap begins when attention grabs hold of it.
Notice the grabbing.
Receiving Praise
Pleasant feelings arise.
The hook may become:
"I want more of this."
Observe the reaching.
Rama Asked
“Master, if I see the hook, must I avoid all thoughts?”
Vashishtha laughed softly.
“No.”
“The wise fisherman does not fear hooks.”
“He simply knows which ones not to bite.”
Today's Sadhana
Throughout the day, notice:
The appearance.
And then:
The bite.
Whenever something captures attention, ask:
What appeared?
Then ask:
Did I bite?
Notice especially:
the bite of irritation
the bite of praise
the bite of fear
the bite of distraction
the bite of self-judgment
No resistance.
No suppression.
Simply seeing.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha spoke:
“Thoughts are not bondage.
Feelings are not bondage.
Experiences are not bondage.
Bondage begins
 when attention bites the hook.
See the bite early,
and freedom remains near.”
