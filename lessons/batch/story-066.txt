Lesson: The World Appears In Awareness, Not Outside It
O Rama, until now you have quietly assumed:
“I am awareness… and the world is outside me.”
This assumption must now be dissolved.
Look Directly
When you see a tree, tell me:
Where does the tree appear?
Outside you… or within your knowing?
When you hear a bird:
Does the sound exist apart from awareness?
Or only as it is known?
O Lakshmana, examine this carefully:
Sight appears in awareness
Sound appears in awareness
Sensations appear in awareness
Even the sense of “body” appears in awareness
You have never experienced anything outside awareness.
The Great Turning
You once believed:
“I am in the world.”
Now see the truth:
The world is in you — as awareness.
Just as a dream arises within the mind,
 so too this waking world arises within consciousness.
Contemplation
Sit beneath the trees as the wind moves softly.
Do not withdraw from the world.
Instead, notice:
The rustling leaves… appear in you
The warmth of the sun… appears in you
The breath… appears in you
Let this recognition deepen:
There is no “inside” and “outside”
 There is only appearance within awareness
Practice
Keep your eyes open today.
Walk, move, act.
But remain established in this seeing:
Nothing is outside you
Everything arises in awareness
Awareness is never touched by what appears
Final Upadesh
O Rama…
When this is known not as thought but as direct seeing:
Fear begins to dissolve
Separation weakens
The world loses its weight
And what remains is a vast, effortless wholeness.
You are not a being moving through the world.
You are that in which the world itself appears and disappears.
Abide in this… and the next door shall open on its own.
