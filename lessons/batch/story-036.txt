The morning practice did not begin in the forest today.
Vashishtha sat near a flowing stream.
But unlike before, he did not ask Rama and Lakshmana to sit.
Instead, he handed each of them a pot filled to the brim with water.
“Walk,” he said,
 “but do not spill a single drop.”
They began moving along a narrow, uneven path.
Stones shifted beneath their feet.
The ground rose and dipped.
At first, their entire attention went to the pot.
Every step was careful. Controlled.
After some time, Vashishtha began speaking:
“Rama… are you aware of the trees?”
Rama replied, “No, Gurudev. My attention is on the pot.”
Vashishtha said:
“Lakshmana… are you aware of your breath?”
“No, Gurudev.”
Vashishtha stopped them.
“This is concentration… not stability.”
He took the pots back, then returned them again.
“This time… walk naturally.”
They hesitated.
“Gurudev, the water will spill.”
He replied:
“Let some spill… but do not lose yourself.”
They began again.
This time, their steps were less rigid.
Water moved slightly… a few drops fell.
But something else changed.
Rama noticed:
The sound of the stream
The movement of his body
The sensation of holding the pot
All appearing together.
Yet…
There was also a quiet background.
After some time, Vashishtha asked:
“Now, Rama… what is happening?”
Rama answered slowly:
“Walking is happening… sensations are known… attention is moving… but something is unchanged.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“That unchanged… is not disturbed by movement.”
He turned to Lakshmana:
“And when a thought comes?”
Lakshmana observed carefully.
A thought arose:
“I may spill more water.”
But he did not follow it.
The body adjusted naturally.
The walking continued.
“The thought came… but did not take me,” Lakshmana said.
Vashishtha spoke firmly:
“This is non-identification in action.”
They continued walking.
Now faster.
More uneven terrain.
Water spilled occasionally.
But there was no frustration.
No tightening.
Only adjustment… and continuation.
After a long silence, Vashishtha said:
“Previously…”
You tried to control action
You narrowed attention
You lost the whole
“Now…”
Action is happening
Attention is free
Awareness is stable
He paused, then gave the essence:
“Do not hold attention tightly…
 let it move within awareness.”
They stopped walking.
The pots were set down.
Vashishtha concluded:
“Stability is not in perfect action.”
“It is in undisturbed being… while action is imperfect.”
A deep silence followed.
Rama spoke:
“Gurudev… earlier I was trying to act correctly.”
“Now… action is correcting itself.”
Vashishtha smiled gently.
“When you do not interfere…
 intelligence flows through action.
The stream continued to flow beside them—
Never stopping… never holding… never disturbed.
