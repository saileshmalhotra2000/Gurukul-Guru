11 June 2026 — Lesson: The Birth of the Center
The ancient forest was quiet after dawn.
Mist floated above the river.
Birdsong rose and faded among the banyan trees.
Rama and Lakshmana sat before Rishi Vashishtha as sunlight filtered through the leaves and touched the forest floor in golden patterns.
For many days they had been observing the earliest movements of attention.
Now Vashishtha began the next step.
Vashishtha spoke
"Rama, yesterday you learned to notice the first movement.
Today you must learn to notice what happens immediately afterward."
Rama bowed.
"What happens, Master?"
Vashishtha picked up a dry leaf and dropped it into a still pool.
The water trembled.
Ripples spread outward.
Then something curious happened.
The floating leaves nearby slowly gathered around the disturbance.
Vashishtha pointed.
"Do you see?"
"Yes, Master."
"The leaf fell first.
The gathering came second."
The Second Movement
Vashishtha continued:
"When attention first moves toward something, there is not yet a self.
Only movement.
But if attention remains fascinated, energy begins to gather around the movement.
A center starts forming."
Lakshmana asked,
"What kind of center?"
"The center that says:
my concern
my success
my failure
my opinion
my problem
The center is not created all at once.
It condenses."
An Experiment
Vashishtha pointed toward the forest path.
"A traveler appears."
The brothers imagined the traveler.
"What is the first thing that happens?"
Rama answered:
"I notice him."
"Good.
That is simple perception."
"And then?"
Rama paused.
"Thought begins."
"Earlier than thought."
Rama became quiet.
After a while he said:
"Interest."
Vashishtha smiled.
"Yes.
Interest."
"And after interest?"
Lakshmana replied:
"Attention gathers."
"Exactly."
"And when attention gathers long enough?"
The brothers remained silent.
"A center appears."
Ordinary Life
Vashishtha gave examples.
Someone praises you.
First:
sound.
Then:
interest.
Then:
attention gathers.
Soon:
"I am important."
The center has formed.
Someone criticizes you.
First:
sound.
Then:
tightening.
Then:
attention gathers.
Soon:
"I am being attacked."
The center has formed.
You see a distraction.
First:
perception.
Then:
pull.
Then:
attention gathers.
Soon:
"I need this."
The center has formed.
The Insight
Vashishtha drew a circle in the sand.
"This circle is what most people call themselves."
He wiped it away.
"The circle is not the problem.
The forgetting is the problem."
Rama looked carefully.
"The forgetting of what, Master?"
"The forgetting that the center was assembled."
What Awareness Sees
Vashishtha continued:
"When awareness remains present, it sees:
something became important,
attention gathered,
a center began forming.
Nothing is hidden."
"When awareness is absent, the center finishes forming before it is noticed."
Today's Sadhana
Several times today, pause and observe:
What just became important?
Is attention gathering around it?
Is a center beginning to form?
Do not try to stop the process.
Simply notice it early.
Notice the gathering.
Notice the condensation.
Notice the birth of "me" around the experience.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha spoke softly:
"The self that troubles you is rarely born in a single moment.
It forms the way dew gathers upon a leaf.
Drop by drop.
Attention by attention.
See the gathering early,
and freedom remains near."
The forest returned to silence.
The river continued flowing.
Rama and Lakshmana sat quietly, watching not the world, but the subtle gathering of attention within themselves.
