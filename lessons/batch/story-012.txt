The forest is quiet in the early morning. The mist that covered the river yesterday has begun to lift. Sunlight slips through the tall sal trees, painting long beams across the ground.
Rama sits with his eyes lowered, watching the breath move slowly in and out, just as Vashishtha had instructed.
Lakshmana sits beside him, trying to remain still, though his eyes occasionally wander to the birds hopping among the roots.
After some time, Vashishtha speaks.
“Rama.”
“Yes, Gurudev.”
“When you paused this morning… what did you see?”
Rama reflects before answering.
“At first, many thoughts were moving.”
“What kind of thoughts?”
“Plans… memories… and small worries.”
Vashishtha nods gently.
“And when you continued watching?”
“They slowed.”
Lakshmana turns toward him.
“Did they stop?”
Rama shakes his head.
“No. But they became quieter.”
Vashishtha draws a slow line in the soft earth with a small stick.
“Imagine a lake during a storm.”
The brothers picture it.
“The waves are large. The surface is restless. Can you see the bottom?”
“No,” Lakshmana says.
“But when the wind settles,” the sage continues, “the water becomes clear.”
He looks at Rama.
“Did you notice something beneath the thoughts?”
Rama closes his eyes briefly, remembering.
“Yes.”
“What?”
“A space… that was not disturbed.”
Vashishtha smiles faintly.
“The mind produces waves. But awareness itself is deeper than the waves.”
Lakshmana frowns slightly.
“So the thoughts are not the problem?”
“No.”
The sage tosses the small stick gently into the river. It floats and begins drifting downstream.
“The problem is forgetting that you are the one watching the river.”
The brothers follow the stick with their eyes.
“Most people,” Vashishtha says, “fall into the water of their thoughts.”
“They are carried wherever the current pulls.”
The stick spins briefly around a small whirlpool before continuing its journey.
“But the one who watches from the shore…”
Rama finishes softly.
“…is not carried away.”
“Yes.”
A bird calls loudly from a nearby branch.
Lakshmana asks,
“Gurudev, if we keep observing like this, will the mind eventually become completely still?”
Vashishtha looks at the flowing river and chuckles softly.
“Lakshmana, will the river stop flowing?”
“No.”
“Then why expect the mind to stop thinking?”
Lakshmana smiles, slightly embarrassed.
“The purpose is not to stop the river,” the sage continues.
“It is to learn how to cross it.”
Rama’s eyes brighten.
“The boat.”
“Yes.”
The memory of the earlier teaching returns.
“Discipline builds the boat.”
“Clarity guides it.”
“And awareness keeps it from drifting.”
The stick disappears around a bend in the river.
The forest slowly fills with the sounds of waking life.
Rama bows his head slightly.
“I understand a little more now, Gurudev.”
Vashishtha nods.
“Good.”
He stands and brushes the dust from his robes.
“Then today, watch the river again.”
The brothers rise.
“But this time,” Vashishtha says as they begin walking along the bank,
“notice something else.”
Rama asks,
“What should we notice, Gurudev?”
The sage points ahead to where the river bends between the trees.
“Notice who is watching.”
