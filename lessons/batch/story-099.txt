01 June 2026 — Lesson 46: The Birth of the Center
The forest was still. Morning mist drifted above the river. Rama and Lakshmana sat quietly beneath a great banyan tree. Birds called from distant branches. For a long time, Rishi Vashishtha simply watched the river move.
Rama Asked
“Master, we can now see the first leaning.
We can sometimes see the grasp before it becomes strong.
But what happens next?
How does this small movement become a ‘me’?”
Rishi Vashishtha Replied
“The grasp itself is not the center.
The center is born when awareness forgets that it is witnessing.
A movement appears.
Attention gathers around it.
Then comes a silent assumption:
‘This is happening to me.’
The moment that assumption appears,
the center is born.”
The Birth of the Center
Vashishtha drew a circle in the sand.
At first he placed only a single dot.
“This is a sensation.”
Then he drew a ring around it.
“This is attention gathering.”
Then another ring.
“This is preference.”
Then another.
“This is ownership.”
Finally he pointed to the center.
“And now the mind says:
‘I am the one experiencing this.’
The center was not there at the beginning.
It was constructed.”
Experiential Insight
Close your eyes.
Wait for a sound.
When a sound appears, notice carefully:
First:
there is hearing.
Then:
attention notices the hearing.
A moment later:
the mind says,
“I hear.”
Can you detect the difference?
The sound arrived before the owner appeared.
The experience came first.
The center came later.
Observe this many times.
The sequence becomes visible.
Ordinary Life Examples
Hunger
A sensation arises in the stomach.
Immediately the mind says:
“I am hungry.”
Look more closely.
First there was sensation.
Then came the label.
Then came the owner.
Praise
Someone compliments you.
First there are words.
Then pleasant feeling.
Then:
“They appreciate me.”
The center enters after the experience.
Anxiety
A contraction appears in the chest.
Soon the mind says:
“I am anxious.”
Yet before the statement,
there was only sensation.
The owner arrived afterward.
Success
Something goes well.
Joy appears.
Moments later:
“I did this.”
Ownership quietly forms around experience.
What to Notice
Today, look for this sequence:
Experience → Attention → Ownership
Not:
Me → Experience
The mind assumes the second.
Careful observation reveals the first.
Today's Sadhana
Several times today, when a thought, emotion, or sensation appears:
Notice the raw experience.
Notice attention moving toward it.
Notice the moment ownership forms.
Silently ask:

 “When exactly did this become ‘mine’?”
Do not answer intellectually.
Simply observe.
Let the question illuminate the process.
Lakshmana Asked
“Master, if the center is constructed, does it truly exist?”
Vashishtha Smiled
“It exists as a whirlpool exists.
A whirlpool appears in a river.
It has shape.
It has movement.
Yet it is made entirely of flowing water.
The center is much the same.
It appears real.
But it is made of movements within awareness.”
Final Teaching
Rishi Vashishtha said:
“The center is not found.
It is formed.
See its formation clearly,
and awareness returns to its natural freedom.”
