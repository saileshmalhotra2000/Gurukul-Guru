At the sacred hour of dawn, Rishi Vashishtha continued the subtle teaching to Rama and Lakshmana, now refining their understanding of effortless abidance during constant activity.
The Illusion of Losing Awareness
“Rama,” said Vashishtha,
 “You believe that awareness is lost during action. This is not so.”
Reflect deeply:
Does awareness disappear…
Or does attention become absorbed?
👉 Awareness is never absent
 👉 Only attention becomes entangled
The Training of Returning Without Effort
“Lakshmana,” he continued,
 “Do not struggle to hold awareness. Instead, learn the art of gentle returning.”
Each time you notice:
You are lost in thought
You are carried by emotion
Do not resist, do not judge.
👉 Simply notice
 👉 And in that noticing, you are already back
No force is needed.
Awareness Within Engagement
Vashishtha spoke with clarity:
“True mastery is not withdrawal from life…
 but clarity within involvement.”
Practice this:
While speaking → know you are aware
While deciding → know you are aware
While feeling → know you are aware
Not as repetition…
But as a quiet, background fact.
The End of Inner Friction
“Rama,” he said,
 “Disturbance comes from resisting what arises, or clinging to what passes.”
See clearly:
Emotion arises → let it arise
Thought appears → let it appear
👉 Do not add a second movement:
no suppression
no indulgence
Then:
👉 the mind remains light
 👉 awareness remains clear
Action Without Center
Now Vashishtha revealed a deeper truth:
“You still feel there is a center from which you act.”
Investigate this.
Is there truly a doer?
Or is there only action appearing in awareness?
When seen rightly:
👉 Actions happen
 👉 But no inner ‘actor’ is found
The Natural State in Motion
“Lakshmana,” he said gently,
 “The witness is not something to maintain. It is what remains when effort ends.”
So in activity:
Do not try to stay aware
Do not try to avoid distraction
Instead:
👉 Let everything arise and pass
 👉 Remain as that which is unchanged
Closing Insight
Vashishtha looked upon them with stillness:
“When you no longer try to stabilize awareness…
 you discover it was never unstable.”
And in that seeing:
Activity continues
Life unfolds
But भीतर (within)… nothing is disturbed
