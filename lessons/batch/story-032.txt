The morning air was cool.
Rama and Lakshmana walked behind Vashishtha, each step softer than the last… not by effort, but by attention.
After a long silence, Vashishtha spoke:
“Rama… yesterday you saw that thoughts can arise without taking you.”
“Yes, Gurudev.”
“And today… you must see if action also can happen without taking you.”
They approached a fallen tree blocking their path.
Vashishtha said nothing.
Lakshmana immediately stepped forward, lifted one end, and strained to move it aside. His breath deepened, muscles tightened.
Rama joined him.
Together, they pushed.
The tree shifted.
When it was done, Lakshmana exhaled heavily.
Vashishtha looked at him.
“Lakshmana… while pushing, where were you?”
Lakshmana replied honestly:
“In the effort, Gurudev. In the body… in the strain.”
“And were you aware of that… while it was happening?”
Lakshmana lowered his gaze.
“No.”
Vashishtha turned to Rama.
“And you?”
Rama spoke slowly.
“At first, I was also in the effort… but then I remembered.”
“What did you remember?”
“That I can remain as the one who knows… even while acting.”
Vashishtha’s eyes softened.
“Then tell me… when the body strained… did you also strain?”
Rama closed his eyes briefly.
“The body strained… but something did not.”
A gentle breeze passed.
Vashishtha picked up a small stone and placed it in Rama’s hand.
“Hold this.”
Rama held it.
“Now walk.”
They began walking again.
“Rama, are you holding the stone?”
“Yes, Gurudev.”
“Are you thinking about holding it?”
“No.”
“Yet the holding continues.”
Vashishtha stopped.
“This is how action must become.”
Lakshmana listened carefully.
Vashishtha continued:
“Right now, your actions are mixed with ‘I am doing.’
That is why disturbance comes.”
He pointed to Rama’s hand.
“Does the hand say, ‘I am holding’?”
Lakshmana answered, “No, Gurudev.”
“Yet holding happens.”
They resumed walking.
“Rama… now expand this seeing.”
“Let walking happen… as holding is happening.”
Rama walked.
Feet moved. Arms swung. Breath flowed.
And quietly…
The sense of “I am doing” loosened.
After some time, Rama spoke:
“Gurudev… action is happening… but it feels lighter.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Because you are no longer inside it.”
Lakshmana tried the same.
For a few steps… it was clear.
Then suddenly, a thought arose—
“I must maintain this.”
And immediately, tension returned.
Vashishtha looked at him.
“Lakshmana… what came just now?”
“A thought to hold the state.”
“And what happened to your ease?”
“It disappeared.”
Vashishtha’s voice became firm:
“Because you left the witness… and entered the controller.”
Silence followed.
Even the birds seemed distant.
After a while, Vashishtha spoke again:
“Listen carefully, both of you.”
“You cannot do awareness.”
“You cannot maintain the witness.”
“You can only…”
He paused.
“…stop interfering.”
Rama absorbed this deeply.
They reached a stream.
Water flowed over stones, creating endless movement.
Vashishtha pointed.
“Does the water try to flow?”
“No, Gurudev.”
“Does it try to remain smooth?”
“No.”
“It simply follows its nature.”
He turned back to them.
“Let action follow its nature.”
“Let thought follow its nature.”
“Let emotion follow its nature.”
“And you…”
He looked directly into Rama’s eyes.
“Remain as that which does not follow.”
Rama stood still.
Water moved.
Wind moved.
Body breathed.
Mind whispered.
And yet…
Something remained untouched.
Lakshmana spoke softly:
“Gurudev… if I do nothing… life continues.”
Vashishtha replied:
“Yes.”
“And if I interfere… disturbance begins.”
“Yes.”
Vashishtha smiled faintly.
“Then learn this well.”
“Action without disturbance
 is not achieved by control…
 but by absence of interference.”
The sun rose higher.
The path ahead was clear.
But now…
They were no longer walking into the forest.
They were walking…
without being carried by anything at all.
