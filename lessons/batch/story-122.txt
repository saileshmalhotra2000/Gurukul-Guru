Gurukul Lesson — Stage 7: Feeding the Echo
Date: June 22, 2026
The river was calm this morning.
Not a ripple disturbed its surface.
The forest seemed to be holding its breath.
Vashishtha sat beneath the banyan tree.
Before him were the five circles.
What happened?
What did the voice say?
Did you believe it?
What happened next?
Echo
Rama and Lakshmana sat quietly.
For many days they had been studying the echo.
They had begun noticing something surprising.
Sometimes the echo faded quickly.
Sometimes it stayed for hours.
Sometimes for days.
Rama finally spoke.
"Master, yesterday I watched an echo."
Vashishtha nodded.
"What happened?"
"The event was over."
"The voice had stopped."
"But the echo continued."
The sage smiled.
"Good."
"What happened then?"
Rama became thoughtful.
"I kept returning to it."
The old sage nodded.
Without speaking, Vashishtha reached beside him.
He picked up a small piece of dry wood.
Then he lit it from the nearby fire.
A tiny flame appeared.
The princes watched.
The sage placed the burning stick on the ground.
The flame slowly weakened.
It was beginning to die.
Then Vashishtha added another dry twig.
The flame grew larger.
Then another.
Larger still.
Then another.
Soon the fire was alive again.
The sage looked at Rama.
"What kept the fire alive?"
"The wood."
Vashishtha nodded.
"What keeps the echo alive?"
Silence.
The princes looked carefully.
Then Lakshmana answered.
"Returning to it."
The sage smiled.
"Yes."
"The event does not feed the echo."
"The voice does not feed the echo."
"You feed the echo."
The words landed heavily.
The river continued flowing.
No one spoke.
After some time Vashishtha continued.
"When the mind says:
'Why did that happen?'
and you return..."
"You add wood."
"When you replay the conversation..."
"You add wood."
"When you explain it again..."
"You add wood."
"When you imagine tomorrow's version..."
"You add wood."
The princes watched the fire.
Every twig strengthened it.
Every twig prolonged it.
Rama asked,
"Master, should I stop returning to it?"
Vashishtha laughed softly.
"There is that word again."
The princes smiled.
The sage shook his head.
"No."
"First see the feeding."
"Before a man stops feeding a fire, he must know he is feeding it."
He pointed toward the river.
A leaf floated past.
The current carried it effortlessly.
"No feeding."
The leaf disappeared downstream.
Then he pointed toward the fire.
"Feeding."
The flames rose higher.
For a long time the princes sat silently.
Something subtle became clear.
The echo was not merely happening.
Participation was happening.
As the sun rose higher, Vashishtha drew one final symbol beside the circle labeled Echo.
A small bundle of sticks.
Then an arrow.
Pointing toward the fire.
"Many men think they are suffering from the echo."
The sage said softly.
"They do not notice they are feeding it."
Daily Sadhana
Use the five circles:
1. What happened?
2. What did the voice say?
3. Did I believe it?
4. What happened next?
5. What is the echo?
Then add one more question:
Am I feeding it?
Observe:
Replaying
Retelling
Explaining
Defending
Imagining
Revisiting
Simply notice.
No judgment.
No correction.
Just seeing.
Final Teaching
The voice lights the fire.
Belief creates the echo.
Repeated attention feeds it.
The wise man does not begin by extinguishing the fire.
He first learns to see every stick he adds.
End of Lesson — June 22, 2026
Vashishtha smiled.
"Rama, do not look directly for the echo."
The princes looked puzzled.
The sage continued.
"If a hunter wishes to find a deer, he does not begin by looking for the deer."
"He looks for tracks."
He pointed toward the river.
"An echo is subtle."
"The traces are easier to see."
Rama asked,
"What traces should I look for, Master?"
Vashishtha replied,
"Look for return."
He drew a line in the sand.
Then he touched the same spot again.
And again.
And again.
"What am I doing?"
"Returning to the same place."
"Exactly."
"The echo is simply the mind returning."
Returning to the event.
Returning to the person.
Returning to the conversation.
Returning to the mistake.
Returning to the future.
Returning to the past.
The sage became silent.
Then he said:
"Today do not ask:
Where is the echo?"
Ask:
What am I returning to?
The princes sat quietly.
The answer felt simpler.
Vashishtha continued.
"There are five doors through which the echo is often seen."
Door 1 — Replaying
You find yourself reliving the event.
The body is here.
The mind is there.
Door 2 — Retelling
You keep explaining it to others.
Or silently explaining it to yourself.
Door 3 — Defending
You keep proving you were right.
Even when nobody is arguing.
Door 4 — Imagining
The event has ended.
But new versions are being created.
Tomorrow's argument.
Tomorrow's outcome.
Tomorrow's problem.
Door 5 — Returning
This is the most important.
You simply notice:
"I am back here again."
The sage pointed to the river.
A leaf floated downstream.
It never turned around.
It never revisited the same rock.
It simply continued.
"The echo is different."
"It circles."
"It revisits."
"It returns."
Rama asked,
"Master, when I notice I have returned, what should I do?"
Vashishtha smiled.
"Nothing."
The princes laughed.
The answer was becoming familiar.
"Simply know:
This is the echo."
Not reality.
Not the event.
Not the person.
Not the future.
Only the echo.
Today's Practice
Several times today ask:
Where has my mind returned?
If you discover it returning repeatedly to the same thing:
Pause.
And gently say:
Echo.
Nothing more.
No analysis.
No fixing.
No fighting.
Just recognition.
Final Teaching
The event happens once.
The echo returns many times.
The easiest way to see the echo is not to look for the echo itself.
Look for where the mind keeps returning.
Return is the footprint of the echo.
