Observe the leaning
The forest was silent.
Only the river moved.
Rama sat quietly beside Lakshmana, watching Gurudev Vashishtha pour water slowly into a small clay bowl.
Not a drop spilled.
Then Vashishtha spoke:
“Rama…
 You now see when attention leaves the present.
The next step is subtler.
You must now learn:
what pulls attention away.”
Lakshmana asked:
“Master, is it desire?”
Vashishtha smiled gently.
“Sometimes.
But deeper than desire
 is unconscious leaning.”
He pointed toward the river.
A leaf floated peacefully downstream.
Then suddenly—
the current caught it.
The leaf began spinning.
Moving faster.
Pulled without choosing.
Vashishtha said:
“Attention behaves like this.
First:
 simple experience.
Then:
 a tiny leaning.
Then:
 identification.
Then:
 momentum.”
Rama watched carefully.
Vashishtha continued:
THE TEACHING
“Most suffering begins
 not with action—
but with
 unconscious micro-movements of attention.”
THE SUBTLE MOVEMENTS
Before anger:
 a leaning against.
Before craving:
 a leaning toward.
Before fear:
 a leaning into imagination.
Before distraction:
 a leaning away from what is here.
VASHISHTHA’S EXERCISE
That evening, Vashishtha gave no mantra.
No philosophy.
Only this practice:
“Today,
 do not control the mind.
Simply observe:
what the mind leans toward,
 and what it avoids.”
RAMA’S DISCOVERY
While eating:
 the mind leaned toward the next bite.
While walking:
 the mind leaned toward arrival.
While listening:
 the mind leaned toward replying.
While resting:
 the mind leaned toward becoming active again.
Rama returned and bowed.
“Master…
 the leaning happens before thought.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Yes.
Thought is often only the echo.
The movement began earlier.”
THE DEEPER UNDERSTANDING
“Freedom does not begin
 when thought ends.
Freedom begins
 when unconscious leaning is seen clearly.”
TODAY’S SADHANA
Several times today:
Pause.
Then quietly ask:
“What is attention leaning toward right now?”
Do not fight it.
Do not judge it.
Simply see the movement.
And gently return:
to breath
 to sensation
 to sound
 to step
 to now
VASHISHTHA’S FINAL WORD
As night fell, the guru said softly:
“Rama…
A disturbed mind is rarely forced away.
It is usually pulled away.
Learn the pull.
And attention becomes free.”
