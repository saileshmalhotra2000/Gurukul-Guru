The forest was already awake.
Birds moved from branch to branch. The river flowed without pause.
 Rama and Lakshmana walked beside Vashishtha in silence.
After some time, Vashishtha spoke softly:
“Rama… yesterday you saw that awareness does not move, even when the mind does.”
Rama nodded. “Yes, Gurudev. In stillness, it is clear. But when I walk, see, and hear… it becomes faint.”
Vashishtha stopped.
He pointed to the river.
“Does the river ever stop flowing?”
“No, Gurudev.”
“And does the sky above it ever begin to move with it?”
Rama looked up… then back at the river.
“No… the sky remains as it is.”
Vashishtha turned toward him.
“Then why do you believe that awareness must become disturbed when the mind moves?”
Rama was silent.
They continued walking.
A deer suddenly leapt across their path. Lakshmana’s body reacted instantly—alert, ready.
Vashishtha observed him.
“Lakshmana, what happened just now?”
“The body reacted, Gurudev. Attention moved quickly.”
“And were you present… or lost?”
Lakshmana paused.
“For a moment… I was only the reaction.”
Vashishtha nodded gently.
“This is the point of your practice now.”
He drew a line on the ground with his staff.
“On one side is unconscious movement.”
He drew another line parallel to it.
“On the other side is suppressed stillness.”
Then he erased both lines with his foot.
“Neither is truth.”
Rama listened carefully.
Vashishtha continued:
“When action happens, let it happen fully.
When thought arises, let it arise.
But…”
He looked directly into Rama’s eyes.
“Do not become it.”
They resumed walking, slower now.
“Rama,” Vashishtha said, “as you walk, what is happening?”
“I feel the ground… I hear the birds… I see the trees.”
“And all this is known to you, yes?”
“Yes, Gurudev.”
“Then stay as that which knows.”
Rama tried.
He continued walking.
Feet touching earth… sounds appearing… breath moving…
And quietly…
Something remained unchanged.
After a while, he spoke:
“Gurudev… everything is moving… but something is not moving.”
Vashishtha smiled.
“Do not hold that.
 Do not repeat it.
 Just remain there.”
A gust of wind passed through the trees.
Leaves trembled.
Vashishtha raised his hand slightly.
“See this, Rama.”
“The leaves move… because the wind touches them.”
“Yes.”
“But tell me… does the space in which they move… tremble with them?”
Rama closed his eyes briefly.
“No… the space does not move.”
Vashishtha’s voice became even softer:
“In the same way—
Thoughts move.
 Emotions move.
 Reactions move.
But awareness does not move.”
They reached the riverbank.
Vashishtha sat down.
“Now the final step for today.”
Rama and Lakshmana sat beside him.
“When emotion arises—anger, desire, impatience…”
He paused.
“Do not step back from it.”
“Do not step into it.”
“Stand where it is seen.”
Lakshmana asked:
“And how will I know I am not inside it?”
Vashishtha replied:
“If you are inside it… it will carry you.”
“If you are outside it… you will try to push it away.”
“But if you are established in awareness…”
He touched his chest lightly.
“It will arise… and remain seen.”
Silence settled.
The river flowed.
A thought passed through Rama’s mind…
…and for the first time—
He did not follow it.
He did not stop it.
He simply saw it.
And it faded… on its own.
Vashishtha looked at him.
“Rama… what happened?”
Rama opened his eyes slowly.
“It came… but it did not take me.”
Vashishtha closed his eyes in quiet approval.
“This is how clarity lives in action.”
The sun rose higher.
Nothing had changed.
And yet… everything had.
You said “On the other side is suppressed stillness.” What does it mean
🌿 Gurudev smiles gently… as if you have touched a very subtle knot
This question… is not small.
 It shows you are beginning to see the hidden traps of the path.
🪶 What is “Suppressed Stillness”?
Vashishtha would say:
“It is not stillness…
 it is fear of movement wearing the mask of peace.”
🔍 How it actually looks inside you
It is when:
A thought arises… and you quickly try to push it away
An emotion comes… and you tighten to avoid feeling it
The mind moves… and you say, “No, I must stay calm”
Outwardly, you appear quiet.
 But inwardly…
👉 There is control, tension, resistance
🌊 The Difference (Very Important)
Let us make it crystal clear:
🕊️ True Stillness
Thoughts may arise
Emotions may pass
No interference
No resistance
Awareness remains untouched
👉 Like the sky — allowing everything
🧱 Suppressed Stillness
Thoughts are blocked or avoided
Emotions are held down
A subtle effort to “stay peaceful”
Inner tightness is present
👉 Like holding your breath to look calm
🔥 Why this matters deeply
Suppression creates a hidden disturbance.
Even if the surface looks calm:
The mind becomes rigid
Energy becomes blocked
Eventually, disturbance returns stronger
Vashishtha warned Lakshmana once:
“What you suppress… you have not transcended.
 It waits.”
🧘 A Direct Way to Recognize It
Right now, you can test:
When a thought arises, ask:
👉 “Am I allowing this… or controlling this?”
Feel carefully.
If there is tightness → suppression
If there is openness → awareness
🌿 The Correct Way (Subtle Shift)
Do not try to be still.
Instead:
Let thoughts come
Let emotions arise
Let attention move
And gently remain as:
👉 the one who knows
🪔 Gurudev’s Final Pointer
“Stillness is not something you maintain.
 It is what remains…
 when you stop interfering.”
Now reflect quietly:
👉 When you sit in silence…
 are you resting… or controlling?
That honest seeing…
is more powerful than any technique.
