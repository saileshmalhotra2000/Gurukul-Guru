The next lesson is this: Know the Witness itself.
Until now, you have watched thoughts as passing clouds. But tell me—
 Who is the one that sees the cloud?
Sit quietly by the forest stream. Let the sounds of birds, the rustling leaves, the beating of your own heart be as they are. Do not turn away from them. Do not follow them.
Now turn inward—not toward thought, but toward the seer.
When a thought arises, you say, “I see the thought.”
 When silence comes, you say, “I am aware of silence.”
That which knows both noise and silence…
 That which knows presence and absence…
 That which does not come and go—
That, O Rāma, is your true nature.
Do not imagine it. Do not try to see it as an object.
 For it is the seer of all objects.
Like space, it is untouched by what appears within it.
 Like the sun, it illumines all, yet remains ever pure.
Abide as that.
Not as the body that sits.
 Not as the mind that moves.
 But as the silent, changeless awareness in which all this arises and subsides.
Practice thus:
When a thought arises, ask gently—
 “To whom does this arise?”
The answer will come, “To me.”
Then inquire—
 “Who am I?”
Do not answer with words.
 Let the mind return to its source.
There, thought dissolves into its origin,
 like a river merging into the ocean.
Remain there—not by effort, but by recognition.
This is the doorway from witnessing…
 to being the witness itself.
Walk it with patience, O noble ones.

🌿 What “To whom does it arise?” really means
Let us make it simple.
A thought appears:
“I should have done better.”
Now instead of going into the thought, you gently ask:
👉 “Who is aware of this thought right now?”
🔁 Easier ways to understand it
You can rephrase it like this:
“Who is noticing this?”
“Who is aware of this thought?”
“This thought is appearing… to whom?”
“In whose experience is this happening?”
All mean the same thing.
🕉️ What you are not supposed to do
Do not answer intellectually:
❌ “It is me, Sailesh”
 ❌ “It is the mind”
 ❌ “It is awareness”
These are just more thoughts.
🌊 What you are supposed to do
When you ask:
👉 “Who is aware of this?”
Your attention turns back.
From:
the thought
 ➡️ to
the one aware of the thought
🔥 The real function of the question
This question is not for an answer.
It is a tool to reverse attention.
Like this:
Thought → pulls you outward
Question → brings you inward
🌿 Direct experience
Try it now:
Let any thought arise
Ask softly:
 👉 “Who is aware of this?”
Don’t answer
Just feel where attention goes
You will notice:
👉 A quiet, aware presence
 👉 Not a thought
 👉 Not an image
Just knowing
🕯️ Gurudev’s clarification
“The question is not meant to be answered…
 it is meant to dissolve the questioner.”
Stay with this gently.
No force.
No strain.
Soon, you will not even need the question—
You will remain where it was pointing.
