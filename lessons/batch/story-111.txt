10 June 2026 — The Center Begins to Form
The forest was still.
Morning light filtered through the banyan leaves and painted shifting patterns upon the ground.
The river moved quietly nearby.
Rama and Lakshmana sat before Vashishtha.
For many days they had been studying the earliest movements:
the first taste
the first spark
the first leaning
the gathering of attention
Today Vashishtha picked up a handful of dry leaves and scattered them into the air.
The leaves fell in many directions.
Then he placed a small stone in the center of the courtyard.
Again he scattered leaves.
This time many landed around the stone.
Some remained there.
Some circled near it.
Some gathered against it.
Vashishtha pointed.
"What has changed?"
Lakshmana replied,
"The leaves now gather around a center."
Vashishtha nodded.
"Exactly."
The Teaching
"The gathering of attention creates something."
"At first there is only experience."
"A sound."
"A sensation."
"A thought."
"A memory."
Then attention gathers.
Then gathers more.
Then gathers more.
And gradually something appears:
a center.
Rama Asked
"Master, is this center the ego?"
Vashishtha smiled.
"It is the beginning of what later becomes ego."
"The center is born before the story."
"Before:
I am offended.
I am successful.
I am afraid.
I want this."
There is simply:
something around which attention is gathering.
Seeing It Directly
Vashishtha pointed toward the river.
"Yesterday you observed the gathering."
"Today observe what the gathering creates."
"When attention repeatedly gathers around something, a center begins to appear."
Around that center:
thoughts gather
emotions gather
memories gather
reactions gather
Soon the mind says:
'This is about me.'
Ordinary Life Examples
During Conversation
A comment is made.
Attention gathers around a phrase.
Soon a center appears:
"They are talking about me."
The center was not there at first.
It formed.
On the Golf Course
A poor shot occurs.
Attention gathers.
Judgment gathers.
Then a center appears:
"I am playing badly."
Notice how quickly the center forms.
During Meditation
A thought appears.
Attention gathers around it.
Soon there is:
"My distraction."
"My problem."
"My progress."
The center has quietly appeared.
At Work
An email arrives.
One sentence becomes important.
Attention gathers repeatedly.
Soon:
"Why did they send this to me?"
The center emerges.
Experiential Insight
Close your eyes.
Let sounds arise.
Let sensations arise.
Let thoughts arise.
Notice:
Most experiences come and go without creating a center.
Then something becomes important.
Attention gathers.
Stay very close.
Can you feel the moment a subtle "me" begins to appear?
Not a thought.
Not a sentence.
A felt center.
A point around which attention organizes.
This is what we begin studying now.
Today's Sadhana
Several times today, pause and observe:
What just became important?
Is attention gathering around it?
Is a center beginning to form?
What is the texture of that center?
Can the center be seen before the story appears?
Do not fight it.
Do not dissolve it.
Simply observe its birth.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha said:
"Attention gathers.
Gathering creates a center.
Around the center,
 thoughts assemble.
Around the thoughts,
 a story appears.
See the center being born,
and you stand at the doorway of freedom."
