03 June 2026 — Lesson 48: The Moment of Forgetting
The dawn air was cool. Mist drifted over the river. Rama and Lakshmana sat quietly before Rishi Vashishtha beneath the ancient banyan tree.
For many days they had watched attention gather. They had seen the first leaning, the first grasp, the birth of the center, and the strange way awareness seemed to forget its own witnessing nature.
Today Vashishtha picked up a polished copper bowl filled with still water.
Rama Asked
“Master, yesterday you said awareness appears to forget that it is witnessing.
Can this forgetting be seen as it happens?”
Vashishtha smiled.
“Not only can it be seen.
It must be seen.
For that moment is the doorway through which suffering enters.”
The Reflection
Vashishtha held the bowl before them.
The morning sky reflected perfectly upon its surface.
Clouds appeared.
Trees appeared.
Birds appeared.
The entire forest seemed to live within the bowl.
Then Vashishtha asked:
“Has the bowl become the clouds?”
“No, Master.”
“Has it become the trees?”
“No.”
“Has it become the birds?”
“No.”
“It reflects them.
Yet remains itself.”
He paused.
“So too with awareness.
Experience appears within it.
But awareness becomes fascinated by its reflections.
Soon it forgets itself and attends only to what is reflected.”
The Exact Moment
Vashishtha drew three marks in the sand.
First
An experience appears.
A sound.
A sensation.
A thought.
An emotion.
There is only appearance.
Second
Attention moves toward it.
Interest gathers.
Energy gathers.
The experience becomes brighter.
Third
Awareness unconsciously looks through the experience rather than at it.
This is the moment of forgetting.
The witness becomes hidden.
The experience becomes primary.
The center begins forming.
Experiential Insight
Close your eyes.
Wait for a sound.
When a sound appears, ask:
Am I aware of the sound?
Then ask:
Or have I become absorbed in the sound?
Notice the difference.
In one case:
the witnessing remains obvious.
In the other:
only the experience seems real.
The sound is identical.
Only the relationship changes.
Ordinary Life Examples
Reading a Message
A message arrives.
Immediately attention enters the content.
A story begins.
A reaction begins.
The witnessing is forgotten.
Watching a Match
You become completely absorbed.
Victory and defeat feel intensely personal.
For a time,
the witnessing disappears behind involvement.
Being Praised
Pleasure appears.
Attention follows it.
Soon there is:
"I am appreciated."
The witness has been replaced by the story.
Being Criticized
Pain appears.
Attention contracts.
Soon there is:
"I am under attack."
Again the witnessing is forgotten.
Lakshmana Asked
“Master, is forgetting a mistake?”
Vashishtha shook his head.
“No.
It is simply an old habit.
The problem is not that forgetting occurs.
The problem is that it remains unseen.”
Today's Sadhana
Many times today, pause and ask:
“What is receiving this experience?”
Not:
“What do I think about this?”
Not:
“How should I respond?”
Simply:
“What is receiving this experience?”
Let attention turn back toward the knower.
Again and again.
Not forcefully.
Gently.
Like remembering something you never truly lost.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha spoke softly:
“Experience is not bondage.
Forgetfulness is bondage.
The moment awareness remembers itself,
experience continues,
but the center loses its foundation.”
Gurukul of Rishi Vashishtha
