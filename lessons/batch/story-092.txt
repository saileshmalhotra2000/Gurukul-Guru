The Mind Arrives Before the Body
Morning practice had ended.
The river moved quietly beside the gurukul.
Rama carried water from the bank.
Lakshmana followed behind him.
As they walked, Vashishtha suddenly asked:
“Rama,
 where is your mind right now?”
Rama paused.
Then answered honestly:
“Partly here…
 partly already at the next task.”
Vashishtha smiled gently.
The Subtle Error
“The body walks slowly.
But the mind runs ahead.”
He pointed toward the river.
“Look carefully.
The feet are here.
But thought is already elsewhere.
This is how presence fractures.”
The Hidden Habit
Most humans are rarely where the body actually is.
While eating —
 the mind is in tomorrow.
While listening —
 the mind prepares replies.
While walking —
 the mind imagines arrival.
While resting —
 the mind replays movement.
The body lives in the present.
The mind lives in anticipation.
Vashishtha’s Teaching
“Rama,
 suffering increases
 whenever the mind abandons the moment
 in search of the next one.”
The Forest Demonstration
Vashishtha picked up a small bowl of water.
He began walking slowly across the stones near the riverbank.
Not a single drop spilled.
Then he said:
“Attention scattered into the future
 loses sensitivity to the present.
Then movement becomes unconscious.”
Today’s Sadhana
Several times today, pause and observe:
Has the mind arrived somewhere
before the body?
Notice:
eating while mentally elsewhere
listening while preparing action
reaching the destination before walking there
breathing while internally rushing ahead
Do not force slowness.
Simply reunite attention with immediate experience.
Body.
Breath.
Contact.
Movement.
Sound.
The Deeper Understanding
The future itself is not the problem.
Practical thought has its place.
The disturbance begins when:
attention abandons reality
 for psychological projection.
Then the nervous system begins living in tension.
Vashishtha Spoke Softly
“A steady mind
 does not drag the body through time.
It walks with life step by step.”
Then he drew two lines in the earth.
One line moved far ahead.
The other remained beneath his feet.
And he asked Rama:
“Which one is truly living?”
