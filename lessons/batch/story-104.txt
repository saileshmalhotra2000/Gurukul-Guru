4 June 2026 — The First Leaning
The forest was quiet after the morning rain.
Drops still clung to the leaves.
A river moved gently beyond the trees.
Rama and Lakshmana sat before Vashishtha.
For many days they had been observing restlessness, distraction, irritation, desire, and fear—not after they had fully formed, but at their very beginning.
Vashishtha picked up a dry leaf.
He held it above the river.
Then he released it.
The leaf floated effortlessly with the current.
After a while it drifted toward the bank and became caught in a small eddy.
The water circled.
The leaf circled.
Again and again.
Vashishtha pointed.
“Rama, when did the leaf stop flowing with the river?”
Rama watched carefully.
“Not when it began circling.”
“Earlier.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Yes.”
“The circling was visible.”
“The turning toward the bank was subtle.”
The Teaching
Vashishtha said:
“Identification does not begin when a thought appears.”
“It begins when attention leans.”
“A thought may arise.”
“A sensation may arise.”
“A memory may arise.”
“These are natural.”
“But then comes something smaller.”
“The leaning.”
“The movement toward.”
“The movement into.”
“The movement that says:
This matters.
 Follow this.
 Become this.”
Lakshmana asked,
“Master, is the leaning a thought?”
Vashishtha smiled.
“No.”
“It happens before the thought can speak.”
“It is a change in the direction of attention.”
Seeing It Directly
Vashishtha closed his eyes.
“Imagine hearing your name spoken from across the forest.”
“Before you decide to listen.”
“Before you turn your head.”
“There is already a movement.”
“A slight gathering.”
“A subtle orientation.”
“That is the leaning.”
He opened his eyes.
“The ego is not born from thought.”
“It is born from unnoticed leaning.”
Ordinary Life Examples
While Working
You are focused.
A notification appears.
You have not opened it yet.
You have not decided anything.
But attention has already tilted toward it.
See the tilt.
During Conversation
Someone criticizes you.
The words are heard.
Then comes a tiny movement:
I must explain.
See that first movement.
While Practicing Golf
You strike a poor shot.
Before frustration appears,
attention moves toward judgment.
That was bad.
See the movement before the sentence.
During Meditation
A memory arises.
The memory is not the problem.
The leaning toward the memory is what creates the story.
Experiential Insight
Vashishtha drew a line in the sand.
Then another line branching away from it.
“The mind believes suffering begins at the end of the branch.”
“It begins where attention first departs from its natural flow.”
He erased the branch with his hand.
“When the leaning is seen early,
the story never gathers strength.”
“The river remains a river.”
Today's Sadhana
Several times today, pause and ask:
What is attention moving toward right now?
Not:
What am I thinking?
But:
What is attention leaning toward?
Notice:
the first pull toward distraction
the first pull toward defense
the first pull toward approval
the first pull toward judgment
the first pull toward desire
Do not resist.
Do not follow.
Simply see.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha said:
“The thought is not the beginning.”
“The emotion is not the beginning.”
“The story is not the beginning.”
“Before all of them comes a subtle leaning.”
“See the leaning.”
“And the river remains free.”
