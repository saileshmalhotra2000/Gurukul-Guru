The first rays of dawn filtered through the forest canopy as Rama and Lakshmana walked toward the ashram grounds.
Today, Vashishtha was not near the river or the pond.
He stood beside a large bow resting against a tree.
A bundle of arrows lay neatly arranged nearby.
Lakshmana’s eyes lit up.
“Gurudev, will you teach us archery today?”
Vashishtha looked at him with a gentle smile.
“Archery, yes… but not only with the hands.”
Rama observed quietly.
“There is more to it?”
Vashishtha picked up the bow and held it out.
“Take it.”
Rama received it with both hands.
“String the bow.”
Rama did so carefully, his movements steady.
“Now,” Vashishtha said, pointing to a distant tree, “fix your gaze on that single leaf.”
Far away, a small leaf trembled slightly in the morning breeze.
Rama focused.
His body became still.
His breath slowed.
Lakshmana watched intently.
“Release.”
Rama let the arrow fly.
It struck the branch… just beside the leaf.
Lakshmana reacted,
“So close!”
Vashishtha raised his hand gently.
“Again.”
Rama prepared once more.
This time, his eyes softened, yet his focus deepened.
The forest sounds seemed to fade around him.
Only the leaf remained in his awareness.
He released.
The arrow pierced cleanly through the leaf.
Lakshmana smiled broadly.
“Perfect!”
Vashishtha nodded, but his gaze remained thoughtful.
“Rama, what changed between the first and second shot?”
Rama reflected.
“The first time… I was trying to hit the leaf.”
“And the second?”
“I became still… and the arrow followed.”
Vashishtha’s eyes brightened.
“Just so.”
He turned to Lakshmana.
“And you—what did you see?”
Lakshmana answered quickly,
“His body did not change much… but something inside became quiet.”
Vashishtha placed the bow back against the tree.
“Skill is not merely in the hands.”
“It is born from the state of the mind.”
He drew a line on the ground with a stick.
“When the mind is scattered, effort increases… but results weaken.”
“When the mind is steady, effort becomes precise.”
Rama listened deeply.
“So action reflects the condition of the mind.”
“Yes.”
A bird called from a nearby branch, then flew across the clearing.
Vashishtha continued,
“This is why discipline is not only for thought…”
“…it shapes every action.”
Lakshmana stepped forward.
“Then even strength is not enough?”
Vashishtha looked at him firmly.
“Strength without clarity leads to waste.”
“Clarity with even modest strength leads to success.”
The morning light grew warmer.
Vashishtha concluded the lesson:
“Do not aim with restless hands.”
“Aim with a quiet mind.”
He began walking back toward the ashram.
The brothers followed, each holding the teaching in silence.
Behind them, the pierced leaf swayed gently…
a small mark of a mind that had become still.
