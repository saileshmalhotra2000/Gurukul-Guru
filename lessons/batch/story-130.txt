Did Peace Really Leave?

The morning was quiet beside the river.
A pale mist moved over the water.
The birds called once, then rested again in the trees.

Rama sat before Vashishtha.
His face carried the tenderness of a question that had stayed with him through the night.

Master, he said softly, when disturbance comes, it feels as if Peace has gone.

Vashishtha looked toward the river.

A cloud passed before the sun.
For a moment, the water darkened.

Tell me, Rama, did the sun leave?

Rama looked up.
No, Master. The cloud only covered it.

Vashishtha nodded.

In the same way, disturbance may cover Peace from attention.
But covering is not leaving.

Lakshmana listened carefully.

So Peace is not lost? he asked.

The sage smiled.

Look and see.
When attention becomes fascinated with the disturbance, Peace seems absent.
When attention returns to its source, you discover that Peace was not destroyed.
It was only unnoticed.

The river continued to flow.
Nothing in it hurried to prove itself.

Today’s Sadhana
When disturbance appears today, ask gently: Did Peace really leave, or has attention become absorbed in the disturbance?

Final Teaching
Peace does not come and go as quickly as attention believes. Often, only attention has moved.
