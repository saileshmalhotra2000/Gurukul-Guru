6 June 2026 — The Spark Before the Fire
The forest was still.
The river moved quietly beneath the morning mist.
Sunlight filtered through ancient trees, touching the gurukul courtyard with gold.
Rama and Lakshmana sat before Vashishtha.
For many days they had learned to observe the leaning of attention.
Then they learned to recognize the hook—the moment attention bites experience and identification begins.
Today Vashishtha held a single dry twig.
Beside him lay a pile of firewood.
He raised the twig.
“Rama, which creates the forest fire?”
“The pile of wood, Master?”
Vashishtha shook his head.
“The wood allows the fire.”
“The fire begins with a spark.”
He snapped the twig in half.
“And the wise person learns to see the spark.”
The Teaching
“Most people attempt to control the fire.”
“They struggle with anger after anger has formed.”
“They struggle with fear after fear has spread.”
“They struggle with desire after desire has become powerful.”
He pointed toward the forest.
“But by then the flames are already moving.”
“The seeker learns to look earlier.”
“Earlier than the emotion.”
“Earlier than the story.”
“Earlier even than the hook.”
Lakshmana looked puzzled.
“What exists before the hook, Master?”
Vashishtha replied:
“A spark of importance.”
The Birth of Importance
“Watch carefully.”
“A thought appears.”
“Thousands appear every day.”
“Most vanish immediately.”
“Why?”
“Because attention grants them no importance.”
He drew a circle in the sand.
“Yet occasionally something receives energy.”
A second circle was drawn around the first.
“Importance is assigned.”
A third circle.
“Attention gathers.”
A fourth circle.
“Identification forms.”
A fifth circle.
“Suffering begins.”
Vashishtha looked at Rama.
“The hook enters only after importance has already been granted.”
Experiential Insight
Sit quietly.
Allow awareness to rest naturally.
Soon something will arise:
a sensation
a memory
a sound
a plan
Do not examine the content.
Instead ask:
When did this become important?
Notice the subtle moment.
A tiny brightening.
A tiny contraction.
A tiny sense of:
"Pay attention to this."
That is the spark.
Most of life unfolds automatically because this spark is never noticed.
Ordinary Life Examples
During Meditation
A thought appears.
Then another.
Then suddenly one thought feels important.
Attention follows.
The story begins.
See the moment importance was assigned.
While Working
An email arrives.
Many messages are ignored.
One catches attention.
Before opening it, importance has already been granted.
Observe that moment.
In Conversation
Someone makes a casual remark.
Most words pass through effortlessly.
One phrase catches attention.
Energy gathers around it.
Notice the instant it became important.
On the Golf Course
A poor shot occurs.
The shot is finished.
Then comes a small spark:
"This matters."
Moments later frustration appears.
See the spark before the frustration.
Rama Asked
“Master, should importance never be given to anything?”
Vashishtha smiled.
“No.”
“Life requires functional attention.”
“You must still act.”
“You must still learn.”
“You must still care.”
“The difference is this:
The wise assign importance consciously.
The unconscious assign it automatically.”
Today's Sadhana
Throughout the day, practice one question:
What just became important?
Notice:
what captures attention
what gains emotional weight
what begins gathering energy
what repeatedly pulls awareness
Do not interfere.
Simply notice the assignment of importance.
See the spark before the fire.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha said:
“Attention does not become trapped all at once.
First comes importance.
Then gathering.
Then identification.
Then bondage.
See what becomes important,
and you will discover where freedom begins.”
