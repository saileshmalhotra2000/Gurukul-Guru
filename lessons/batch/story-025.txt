The morning sun had just begun to touch the tops of the trees.
A thin mist still lingered over the ashram grounds.
Rama and Lakshmana approached Vashishtha, who was standing near a still pond.
The surface of the water was calm… almost like a mirror.
Without speaking, Vashishtha pointed toward the pond.
“Look.”
They both gazed into it.
They could see:
the sky
the branches above
their own reflections
Everything appeared clear and undisturbed.
Lakshmana spoke softly,
“It reflects perfectly.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“Yes. Because it is still.”
Just then, he bent down and dropped a small pebble into the water.
Ripples spread outward.
The reflection broke instantly.
The sky shattered into fragments.
Their faces distorted and disappeared.
The pond had not changed…
yet nothing could be seen clearly.
Lakshmana watched closely.
“It was clear… and now it is not.”
Vashishtha looked at him.
“What changed?”
“The movement,” Lakshmana replied.
Rama added,
“The water itself is the same.”
Vashishtha’s eyes softened.
“Just so.”
He let them observe as the ripples slowly began to fade.
The surface gradually returned to stillness.
And with it…
the reflections came back.
Clear again. Whole again.
After a long silence, Vashishtha spoke:
“Your mind is like this pond.”
“When it is disturbed, even truth appears broken.”
“When it is still, even the ordinary appears clear.”
Lakshmana folded his arms, thinking deeply.
“But Gurudev… the pebble came from outside.”
Vashishtha gave a slight smile.
“Yes… and no.”
Lakshmana looked puzzled.
Vashishtha continued:
“Sometimes the pebble is a sound… an event… a person.”
“And sometimes…”
“…the pebble is your own thought.”
This struck deeper.
Rama remained silent, absorbing it.
Vashishtha stepped closer to the water.
“Understand this carefully.”
“You cannot stop all pebbles from falling.”
“Life will not become free of disturbance.”
He then pointed again to the pond.
“But you can learn this…”
“How quickly does the water return to stillness?”
The brothers watched.
The ripples were already fading.
No effort.
No struggle.
Just a natural settling.
Lakshmana spoke slowly,
“So the practice is not to avoid disturbance…”
“…but to not remain disturbed.”
Vashishtha’s gaze held steady.
“Yes.”
A breeze passed.
This time, the surface trembled slightly…
but did not fully break.
The reflections shimmered… yet remained.
Rama noticed.
“It is moving… but still reflecting.”
Vashishtha nodded.
“When the mind is trained…”
“…even movement does not destroy clarity.”
Silence followed.
The lesson had entered beyond words.
Vashishtha turned to walk back.
“Remember,” he said gently,
“Do not fight every ripple.”
“Understand the nature of water.”
The brothers followed him slowly.
Behind them, the pond rested once more in stillness…
holding the sky without effort.
