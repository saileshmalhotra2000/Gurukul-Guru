Morning dew still clung to the grass.
 The forest breathed softly in the first light of dawn.
 Smoke from the sacred fire curled upward into the cool air as the gurukul slowly awakened.
Beneath the banyan tree, Rishi Vashishtha sat in stillness.
 Before him, attentive and silent, sat Rama and Lakshmana.
After a long pause, Vashishtha spoke.
From Thought as Servant… to Thought as Tool
“Rama…
Yesterday you learned to let the mind function
 without becoming identified with it.
Today you must learn something subtler:
Even useful thought must be put down when its work is complete.”
Rama looked up.
“Gurudev… does even necessary thinking become bondage?”
Vashishtha nodded.
“When held longer than needed—yes.”
The Mind’s Habit of Lingering
He picked up a bow lying nearby.
“When the arrow has been released—
 does the archer continue pulling the string?”
“No, Gurudev.”
“When the meal is eaten—
 does the cook continue stirring the pot?”
“No.”
“Then why,” Vashishtha asked gently,
 “does man continue thinking
 after thought has completed its purpose?”
The princes were silent.
The Subtle Waste of Energy
Vashishtha continued—
“Observe the mind carefully:
It plans…
 then continues planning.
It analyzes…
 then re-analyzes.
It judges…
 then replays.
It remembers…
 then clings.
Thus what began as useful thought
 becomes unnecessary occupation.”
The Teaching of Today
“Use thought fully.
 Then release it completely.”
Rama asked—
“How do we release it, Gurudev?”
Vashishtha replied—
“By noticing the moment
 when function ends
 but mental momentum continues.”
He tapped the earth.
“That is the moment of practice.”
Practical Instruction
“When planning is complete:
Stop planning.
When analysis is sufficient:
Stop analyzing.
When decision is made:
Stop revisiting.
Let the mind return to silence
 the way a sword returns to its sheath.”
Lakshmana’s Question
Lakshmana asked—
“But Gurudev… thoughts continue by themselves.
 How do we stop them?”
Vashishtha smiled.
“You need not stop them.
You need only:
Stop continuing them.”
He held up one finger.
“A thought may arise by itself.
But the second, third, and fourth thought
 are often fed by unconscious participation.”
Today’s Practice
“Watch for this throughout the day:
When thought has served its purpose—
Do not carry it further.
Return to simple presence.
Return to awareness.
Return to silence.”
Final Upadesh
Vashishtha’s voice softened:
“Rama…
 A disciplined mind is not one that never thinks.
It is one that thinks when needed,
 and falls silent when not.”
The forest became quiet once more.
And the princes sat absorbing the subtlety of the teaching.
