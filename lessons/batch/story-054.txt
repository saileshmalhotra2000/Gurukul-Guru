Forest Gurukul of Sage Vashishtha
Vashishtha gazes upon the two princes.
The image before him is no longer that of restless youth.
The breath has softened.
 The face no longer chases inner movements.
 The body sits—not as something being controlled—but as something that has begun to settle into stillness.
He speaks quietly.
“Rama… Lakshmana…”
“Do you see what is changing?”
Lakshmana opens his eyes slightly.
“There is less disturbance, Gurudev.”
Rama adds,
“And less effort to control it.”
Vashishtha nods.
The Teaching of Effort Becoming Subtle
“In the beginning,” he says,
“you used effort to bring attention back.”
“Again and again.”
“That effort was necessary.”
He draws a firm line in the soil.
“It trained the wandering force.”
He then softens the line with his hand.
“But now…”
“Too much effort becomes disturbance.”
The brothers listen carefully.
The Hidden Trap
Vashishtha’s voice deepens.
“There is a stage where the seeker continues to try…”
“…even when trying is no longer needed.”
He looks directly at Lakshmana.
“This creates a new agitation.”
“Not from thoughts—”
“but from the one who is trying to control them.”
Lakshmana lowers his gaze slightly.
He recognizes it.
Rama’s Observation
Rama speaks slowly.
“Gurudev… sometimes when I sit…”
“If I do nothing…”
“Attention rests naturally.”
“But when I try to maintain it…”
“there is a subtle tightening.”
Vashishtha’s eyes reflect approval—not of achievement, but of clarity.
“Yes.”
“You are seeing correctly.”
The Shift
Vashishtha raises his hand slightly.
“Listen carefully now.”
“This is a delicate transition.”
He speaks with precision:
“At first, you train attention.
 Then, you stop interfering with its natural resting.”
Silence.
The Lamp Without Wind
He gestures toward the small fire nearby.
“When the wind is strong…”
“You must shield the flame.”
“But when the air becomes still…”
A pause.
“Your hand itself becomes the disturbance.”
Direct Instruction
“Sit.”
They close their eyes.
“Bring attention to the breath.”
A few moments pass.
“Now…”
“Do not try to hold it.”
“Do not try to return it.”
“Do not try to control anything.”
A long silence unfolds.
The forest itself seems to listen.
After some time, Vashishtha speaks softly:
“If attention moves—”
“you will notice.”
“If it rests—”
“you will notice.”
“In both cases…”
His voice becomes almost a whisper:
“Remain as the one who notices.”
Lakshmana’s Insight
Lakshmana opens his eyes slowly.
“It feels… lighter, Gurudev.”
“As if something unnecessary has been dropped.”
Vashishtha nods.
“Yes.”
“Effort has done its work.”
“Now subtlety must take over.”
The Principle
Vashishtha summarizes:
Too little effort → distraction
Too much effort → agitation
Right effort → leads to effortlessness
He looks at them both.
“You are now learning the third.”
Practical Exercise for the Day
The Practice of Effortless Resting
During meditation:
Begin with gentle attention on breath
After a few minutes:
Release all control
Do not try to maintain attention
Simply observe:
Does attention rest?
Does it move?
If lost completely:
Gently return once
Then again allow effort to dissolve
During the Day
At moments of stillness:
Pause.
Notice:
“Am I holding attention… or is attention resting?”
Let unnecessary effort fall away.
Final Upadesh
Vashishtha’s voice is calm and deep:
“Discipline brings you to the doorway.
 But only relaxation allows you to enter.
When the river becomes calm,
 do not continue to stir it
 in the name of practice.”
The wind remains absent.
The flame burns steady.
And now—
within the two brothers—
stillness no longer feels created.
It begins to feel natural.
