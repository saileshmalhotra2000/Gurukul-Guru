The Weight of “Me” Inside Action
In the quiet of the gurukul forest,
 Rama asked:
“Master, I now see the stirring before action.
 I can detect the leaning.
 I can feel momentum gathering.
 What remains to be understood?”
And Vashishtha replied:
“Now you must discover
 who is claiming the movement.”
Most people never notice action beginning.
The seeker notices the beginning.
But the deeper yogi notices something subtler:
the silent formation of:
“I am doing”
“I must succeed”
“I am becoming”
“I am failing”
“I need recognition”
“I must protect my image”
This is the hidden contraction.
Not the action itself.
The owner of the action.
Observe Carefully
Before speaking,
 there is often:
a tightening in the chest
a leaning forward internally
a subtle psychological pressure
a desire to arrive
a desire to become somebody
This creates friction.
Then even simple movement loses rhythm.
Vashishtha’s Teaching
“Rama,
 the river flows,
 yet does not announce:
 ‘I am flowing.’
The wind moves through the trees,
 yet does not claim:
 ‘See what I have done.’
Action is natural.
Psychological ownership
 creates burden.”
Today’s Sadhana
Throughout the day, observe:
Not merely:
the action
But:
the one forming inside the action
See if you can detect:
the inner “me”
the subtle self-image
the psychological claimant
the tightening around outcome
Do not suppress it.
Do not judge it.
Just see it clearly.
The Deeper Discovery
When attention becomes whole:
action still happens
speech still happens
movement still happens
but inwardly:
there is less weight.
Less performer.
Less psychological noise.
More rhythm.
More silence.
More directness.
And in the fading evening light of the forest,
 Vashishtha struck the tabla softly once:
Dha.
Then he said:
“The purest beat
 is the one
 that does not carry the burden
 of the player.”
