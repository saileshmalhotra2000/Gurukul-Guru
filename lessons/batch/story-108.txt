7 June 2026 — The Moment of Leaning
The morning sun filtered through the ancient sal trees.
A cool mist still floated above the river.
Birdsong echoed softly through the forest while young students swept the courtyard and prepared for the day.
Rama and Lakshmana sat beside Rishi Vashishtha beneath a vast banyan tree.
For many days they had been learning to notice the earliest movements of mind.
Not thoughts.
Not emotions.
Not even attention itself.
But the subtle moment before attention fully commits.
Today Vashishtha drew a single line in the sand.
Then another line beside it.
He pointed to the first.
"This is seeing."
Then to the second.
"This is leaning."
Rama looked puzzled.
"Master, are they not the same?"
Vashishtha smiled.
"No.
Seeing is natural.
Leaning is optional."
The brothers listened carefully.
The Difference
Vashishtha pointed toward the river.
A leaf floated past.
"You see the leaf."
Another leaf appeared.
"You see that one too."
A bird called in the distance.
"You hear it."
He paused.
"None of these create suffering."
Rama nodded.
"They simply appear."
"Yes," said Vashishtha.
"But then something else happens."
He drew a small arrow in the sand.
"The mind begins to lean."
The sage gently tilted his hand.
"Not fully.
Not strongly.
Just slightly."
"A subtle movement toward."
"A subtle movement into."
"A subtle movement of interest."
The Birth of Involvement
Vashishtha continued.
"The moment attention leans, energy begins to flow."
"Before identification says:
I want this.
I fear this.
I need this.
there is merely a leaning."
Lakshmana asked,
"Is this the moment when importance is granted?"
"Exactly."
The sage nodded.
"Importance is not a thought.
It is a movement."
An Ordinary Example
Vashishtha pointed toward the gurukul path.
"Imagine you are walking."
"You hear your name spoken."
Instantly attention turns.
Why?
Because importance has already been assigned.
Not consciously.
Automatically."
He continued.
"You open a letter."
Most words pass unnoticed.
Then one sentence concerns you.
Immediately attention leans.
Energy gathers.
The sentence becomes larger than the rest."
"The sentence did not pull attention."
"The mind moved toward it."
A Deeper Observation
Rama closed his eyes.
"Master, how does this leaning feel?"
Vashishtha answered softly.
"It feels very small."
"Almost too small to notice."
"Like a thread becoming slightly taut."
"Like a flower turning toward sunlight."
"Like a ripple beginning before a wave."
"Most people only notice the wave."
"The yogi notices the ripple."
The Hidden Choice
The river flowed quietly beside them.
Vashishtha picked up a dry leaf and dropped it into the current.
The leaf floated away without resistance.
"See this leaf."
"It touches many currents."
"But it does not cling to any."
Then he placed a twig into the water.
The twig caught against a stone.
Immediately leaves began gathering around it.
"One small attachment."
"Then more gather."
"One small leaning."
"Then a story forms."
Today's Sadhana
Throughout the day, do not focus on thoughts.
Do not focus on emotions.
Instead, observe:
When attention begins moving toward something.
When interest suddenly increases.
When energy gathers around a person, memory, desire, fear, or irritation.
The tiny moment before the mind says, "This matters."
Do not stop it.
Do not judge it.
Simply notice:
The leaning.
Again and again, return to the body and breath.
Learn the texture of that first movement.
Final Teaching
Vashishtha spoke:
"Seeing is freedom.
Leaning begins bondage.
Notice the leaning early,
 and the chain never forms."
