O noble Rama and steadfast Lakshmana, hear the next unfolding of the wisdom of Vashishtha.
You have learned to remain aware amidst action.
 Today you shall learn the next refinement:
How to let action arise spontaneously from stillness, rather than from mental agitation.
The Teaching of Today: Act From Stillness, Not From Mind
Most men act from turbulence.
They think:
“What should I do?”
“Will this succeed?”
“How will I be seen?”
“What if I fail?”
Thus action is born from disturbance,
 and disturbance poisons action.
But the wise act differently.
First remain established in inner stillness.
Then let action arise from clarity.
Action born from stillness is precise.
Action born from fear is crooked.
1. Let the Mind Become an Instrument
Rama, the mind is not your master.
It is a tool.
As a warrior uses a bow without becoming the bow,
so should you use thought without becoming thought.
When a thought appears:
If useful, employ it.
If useless, let it pass.
Do not honor every thought with belief.
2. Emotion Need Not Command Action
Lakshmana, anger may arise.
 Fear may arise.
 Excitement may arise.
Yet hear this well:
An arising impulse is not a command.
The ignorant obey each inner movement.
 The disciplined observe first, then choose.
Between impulse and action,
 let awareness stand guard.
3. Practice the Sacred Pause
Before significant speech or action:
Pause for one breath.
Feel awareness behind the mind.
Act from that groundedness.
This single breath severs bondage to reactivity.
What is done from pause carries power.
 What is done from impulse carries regret.
4. The Mark of Progress
Know your practice deepens when:
You are slower to react
Faster to observe
Less shaken by praise or blame
Able to maintain inner quiet while outer activity continues
Then awareness is no longer a practice—
 it is becoming your nature.
Final Instruction
Rama, the perfected one does not withdraw from life.
 He moves through life untouched.
Hands in action.
 Mind in service.
 Awareness unmoving.
This is the way of the liberated warrior.

O radiant Rama and devoted Lakshmana, attend carefully to the next refinement of the teaching of Vashishtha.
You have learned to act from stillness.
 Now hear what must be mastered next:
Remain the witness even while the mind performs its functions.
The Teaching of Today: Let the Mind Move, Yet Remain Unmoved
Rama, many seekers make war against the mind.
 They try to silence every thought, suppress every emotion, reject every movement.
This is ignorance.
The mind is born to think
 as fire is born to burn
 and wind is born to move.
Do not demand stillness from the mind.
 Demand only non-identification.
1. Watch the Mind as a Servant at Work
When thought arises in action:
Planning appears
Memory appears
Analysis appears
Judgment appears
Let these function naturally.
But remain clear:
“The mind is operating; I am the awareness in which it operates.”
Then thought becomes service, not bondage.
2. Emotion Is Energy, Not Identity
When emotion surges:
Do not say,
“I am angry.”
“I am afraid.”
“I am anxious.”
Say instead:
“Anger is arising.”
 “Fear is arising.”
 “Agitation is arising.”
This subtle correction loosens the knot of false identity.
The witness names the wave
 without becoming the wave.
3. Let Disturbance Exhaust Itself Without Feeding It
Lakshmana, disturbance persists only through resistance or indulgence.
If anger arises and you feed it, it grows.
 If anger arises and you suppress it, it hides.
But if anger arises and is witnessed fully:
It spends itself and dissolves.
Thus the sage neither indulges nor suppresses—
 he observes.
4. The Practice for Today
Throughout all duties, maintain this inner remembrance:
“Mind moves.
 Emotion moves.
 Body moves.
 Awareness does not move.”
Return to this recognition again and again
 until it remains effortlessly.
Final Insight
Rama, liberation is not when the mind ceases forever.
 Liberation is when:
The mind may move,
 yet no movement within it
 creates movement in you.
Then whether in silence or battle,
 you remain the same.
